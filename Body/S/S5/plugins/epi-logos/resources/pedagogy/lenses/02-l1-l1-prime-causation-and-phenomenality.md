# L1 / L1' — Causation And Phenomenality

## Purpose

This file preserves the L1 family as the axis where outer explanation and inner apprehension first become clearly paired.

The family matters because it holds together:

- causation
- valuation
- force
- perception
- subjective and objective participation in events

## The Day Face: L1 As Causal Completeness

L1 should be taught as the recovery of full causation against modern reduction.

Its major movement is:

- material cause
- efficient cause
- formal cause
- final cause

with the deeper bookends of:

- absolute freedom as the meta-causal ground
- will as the quintessence of causation

The decisive claim is that modern thought usually privileges efficient cause and then wonders why purpose, pattern, and valuation keep returning as unresolved anomalies.

## The Night Face: L1' As Phenomenal Apprehension

L1' is the inside-view of the same field:

- sensation
- feeling
- thinking
- intuition

bounded by the two attitudes:

- introversion
- extroversion

This is not a personality typing toy. It is a map of how consciousness actually takes up a world.

## Why They Belong Together

L1 asks:

- what causes are actually operative?

L1' asks:

- how is the field being apprehended and valued from within?

Together they show that causation is never purely external. Every causal account already presupposes a mode of attention, valuation, and uptake.

## Why This Axis Matters

This family is especially strong for diagnosing:

- fields that claim to be purely material while depending on formal or final causes
- domains where valuation is denied while silently doing decisive work
- practical situations where a problem cannot be solved because the wrong cause has been privileged

It is also one of the best bridges between philosophy and experience. What a field says causes things and how a person feels, perceives, thinks, and intuits are not separate universes.

## Runtime Use

Foreground this family when the issue is about:

- why something is happening
- what is moving a system
- which cause is repressed
- whether the outer explanation and inner experience are mismatched

This family is often central in diagnostic and applicative modes.

## Teaching Cautions

Do not teach this family as:

- a museum-piece recovery of Aristotle
- a Jungian personality sorter
- a single-cause explanatory template

Do teach it as:

- a way to restore explanatory completeness
- a bridge between objective causation and lived phenomenality
- a diagnostic lens for repressed purpose and hidden valuation
