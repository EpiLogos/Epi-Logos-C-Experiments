# L4 / L4' — Encounter, Science, And Situated Knowing

## Purpose

This file preserves the L4 family as the primary encounter and inquiry axis of the whole plugin field.

It matters because this family brings together:

- lived disclosure
- world-situation
- scientific iteration
- context
- subjective and objective entry
- the shared `Thought/` field

## The Day Face: L4 As Phenomenological Disclosure

L4 should be taught as the subjective and lived side of encounter, but not in a merely private or introspective sense.

Its movement is:

- Being
- thrownness
- Dasein
- temporality
- concern
- releasement

The strong point is that inquiry always begins from a world already disclosed. We do not first stand outside reality and then inspect it. We are already situated in it.

## The Night Face: L4' As Scientific Iteration

L4' is the explicit investigative complement.

It is best taught in a `7-position` sense:

- `Learn` as the enclosing environment of cumulative memory and method
- then the six operational movements:
  - Observe
  - Think
  - Plan
  - Build
  - Execute
  - Verify

This is not only software-development rhetoric. It is the objective form of inquiry as current-state to ideal-state movement under explicit criteria and verification.

## Why They Belong Together

The project should not split these into separate systems.

L4 and L4' are one encounter axis:

- L4 asks how the issue is disclosed, lived, and situated
- L4' asks how the issue is investigated, changed, and verified

Every serious inquiry has both aspects. The real question is not which one is "the true entry," but how topological mode modulates their relation.

## Topological Modulation

This family is where topological mode becomes especially important.

- `torus`: one side is foregrounded for a coherent pass, subjective or objective depending on the task
- `klein`: both sides must be held together because inside and outside are structurally inseparable
- `lemniscatic`: the axis recursively folds through itself, often around `P4`, generating a deeper synthesis

This is why the encounter axis is so central to runtime.

## The Shared `Thought/` Field

The family also grounds the shared artifact field when persistence exists.

Subjective artifacts:

- `Being`
- `Thrownness`
- `Presence`
- `Temporality`
- `Care`
- `Releasement`

Objective artifacts:

- `Questions`
- `Traces`
- `Challenges`
- `Patterns`
- `Discovery`
- `Insight`

These should be understood as active process supports, especially for subagent handoff, cross-session continuity, and near-compaction reflection. They are not the essence of the lens-family, but they are the plugin's most concrete enactment of it.

## Runtime Use

Foreground this family whenever the issue is encountered as:

- lived ambiguity
- practical investigation
- bug or state-gap
- situated explanation
- research with both existential and verifiable dimensions

This is usually the strongest entry family for real runs.

## Teaching Cautions

Do not teach this family as:

- phenomenology on one side and engineering on the other with no structural relation
- subjective journaling versus objective execution
- a reason to privilege one side permanently

Do teach it as:

- the main encounter axis
- the place where lived world and explicit method braid
- the family that most directly grounds plugin execution
