# Pedagogical Resources

This directory is the deep pedagogical layer for Epi-Logos.

It sits between:

- `resources/canon/` for short runtime-facing summaries
- the raw corpus in `resources/` for prompt packages, research, and long-form source material

Use this layer when the work needs more than a concise runtime reminder but should not require trawling through the whole raw archive.

## Why This Layer Exists

The canon files are intentionally short. They preserve the operational backbone:

- source and method
- runtime modes
- coherence walk
- squares and rubrics
- `Thought` behavior

That makes them good for live execution, but too thin for serious teaching, deep explanation, or architecture work.

The raw corpus is rich, but it mixes:

- prompt-wrapper language
- older packaging assumptions
- long research accumulation
- tables and references that are useful as source but too diffuse for teaching

This pedagogical layer keeps the strongest seams that are worth learning from directly.

## Internal Structure

This layer now has two deep tracks:

- `deep/` for the main argumentative spine, cross-cutting theory, and pedagogical macro-throughlines
- `lenses/` for essay-grade lens-family resources when a question needs more than a summary of one axis

Use `deep/` when the work is about:

- the overall structure of the theory
- source, topology, psychoid ground, and runtime
- the evolution of knowledge and consciousness
- symbolic, mathematical, and topological justification
- pedagogy and dialogical use

Use `lenses/` when the work is about:

- what a specific lens or lens-family really is
- how a Day lens and its Night complement relate
- how a lens contributes to runtime, diagnosis, and pedagogy
- how to explain a lens without flattening it to a bullet list

## Layering Rule

Use:

- `canon/` for runtime compression
- `pedagogy/deep/` for serious explanation and teaching
- `pedagogy/lenses/` for lens-family essays and axis-specific depth
- raw `resources/` files when you need original detail, derivation, or corroborating context

Do not copy prompt-wrapper scaffolding or host-specific packaging into this layer unless it directly teaches the theory.
