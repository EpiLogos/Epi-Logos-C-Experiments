# QL Modeling, Question-Shaping, And Tetralemma

## Purpose

This file preserves the deepest pedagogical material for Quaternal Logic.

The main point to teach is not that QL offers a six-part template. The main point is that QL reshapes inquiry before analysis begins.

## QL Begins By Reshaping The Question

The most durable pedagogical distinction in the older QL materials is this:

- QL is not merely a way to analyze an already-stable question
- QL is a way to reveal what a question is actually asking

This makes QL a question-shaping organon.

That matters because many inquiries begin:

- vague
- thin
- falsely binary
- loaded with unspoken assumptions

QL gives the learner a way to move from a flat question to a shaped field of inquiry.

## The Six Positions As Question-Forms

The positions are best taught here as different kinds of questioning.

| Position | Teaching Question | Pedagogical Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `0` | What is the implicit question beneath this one? | Opens the ground and the unspoken concern |
| `1` | What is concretely here? | Clarifies the matter or givenness |
| `2` | How is it moving? | Reveals force, transformation, and contradiction |
| `3` | What pattern or kind is this? | Surfaces form, identity, and recurrence |
| `4` | In what context does this become what it is? | Re-situates the inquiry relationally |
| `5` | Why does this matter? What integration is sought? | Opens telos, meaning, and synthesis |

This is one of the best teaching bridges because it lets people use QL without forcing immediate doctrinal mastery.

## Thin Query Elicitation

When a query is too thin to work with directly, QL should be taught as an elicitation method.

Good teaching prompts include:

- What are you really asking beneath the surface wording?
- What concrete elements are actually in play?
- What is changing or pushing here?
- What pattern does this belong to?
- What context changes the meaning of the issue?
- What would answering this actually do?

This lets a learner feel QL as a live inquiry practice rather than a classification system.

## Tetralemma: MEF L2 Deployed At The Edge Of QL Work

Tetralemma is one of the strongest hard pedagogical resources in the corpus — but it is not a QL tool. This distinction is critical and commonly missed.

Tetralemma is the primary instrument of L2, the Logical lens in MEF's Square C. QL structures the inquiry and maps its positions; when that structured inquiry hits a binary wall — a contradiction the framing cannot resolve — MEF's L2 supplies tetralemma to open the logical space. QL and tetralemma cooperate, but they are not the same operation.

The sequence tetralemma works through is:

1. `IS`
2. `IS-NOT`
3. `BOTH`
4. `NEITHER`
5. `SILENCE`

The teaching point is not just that these are possible logical stances. The deeper teaching point is:

- binary framing usually hides the real topology of the issue
- paradox is often a sign that the frame is too flat, not that inquiry has failed
- tetralemma expands logical space so inquiry can continue without collapse

Teach this explicitly: QL identifies *that* the inquiry has hit a binary wall; MEF L2 and tetralemma show *how* to move through it. Conflating the two removes the distinction between structural inquiry and logical critique — which is exactly the distinction Epi-Logos was built to preserve.

## What Each Tetralemmic Move Teaches

### `IS`

Teach what the first claim genuinely discloses.

### `IS-NOT`

Teach what negation or correction makes visible.

### `BOTH`

Teach complementarity without reducing it to compromise.

### `NEITHER`

Teach transcendence of the malformed frame itself.

### `SILENCE`

Teach the limit of articulation as a productive opening rather than an empty stop.

## Holography And Recursive Application

QL should also be taught as holographic rather than merely sequential.

That means:

- each position can itself be explored sixfold
- the whole system can recur at local scale
- a single issue can open nested cycles without losing the larger rhythm

This recursive character is one of the strongest reasons QL remains pedagogically alive rather than hardening into a flat checklist.

## Möbius Return

The teaching of `5 -> 0` belongs inside QL pedagogy because it prevents linear misuse.

The key point:

- synthesis is not a final answer
- it becomes renewed ground for the next cycle

If this is not taught explicitly, learners often flatten QL into a six-step staircase.

## Common Misuses To Guard Against

- treating QL as a rigid sequence instead of a holographic field
- forcing sixfold structure where the inquiry does not need explicit labeling
- using tetralemma as rhetorical flourish instead of logical expansion
- treating `0` as empty preamble rather than living ground
- treating `5` as terminal closure
