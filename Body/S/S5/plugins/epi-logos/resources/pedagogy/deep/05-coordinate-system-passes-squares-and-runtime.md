# Coordinate System, Passes, Squares, And Runtime

## Purpose

This file explains how the ontology becomes runtime without flattening the ontology into runtime.

It is the main deep pedagogical bridge between:

- positions
- lenses
- Day and Night passes
- the three Klein squares
- topological modulation
- `Thought` artifacts

## Positions And Lenses

The key teaching rule is:

- positions and lenses are not rival subsystems
- positions are the numerical nature of the lenses
- lenses are the epistemic articulation of the positions

This is one of the most important corrections to preserve in pedagogy. If the learner treats positions and lenses as separate competing layers, the field becomes overly dualized.

## Day And Night

The Day pass should be taught as the explicate, orientable side of traversal.

The Night pass should be taught as the implicate, analytic, inversion-rich side of traversal.

That means:

- Day is not "good" and Night is not merely critique
- Night is where hidden residue, obstruction, latent pattern, missed context, and crystallized insight become accessible

The strongest practical teaching move is to show that Day and Night are complementary passes through the same field.

## The Practical Coherence Walk

For runtime and pedagogy together, the strongest practical walk remains:

`P1 -> P2 -> P3 -> P4 -> P5 -> P0`

Teach it as:

- `P1`: manifestation or what is concretely here
- `P2`: pressure, contradiction, dynamis
- `P3`: pattern, process, form
- `P4`: contextual fold and recursive nesting
- `P5`: synthesis opened by real coherence
- `P0`: renewed ground after return

This is more pedagogically honest than treating `P0` as the usual practical entrypoint.

## Why `P4` Matters

`P4` is not just another phase. It is the recursive hinge.

This is where:

- context becomes structurally decisive
- recursion can deepen
- the run can fold into a higher-order relation
- lemniscatic movement becomes visible

If `P4` is taught thinly, the field loses one of its main dynamic openings.

## Squares As Orchestration Backbone

The three Klein squares are one of the strongest teaching compressions in the corpus.

### Square A: Articulation

`L0`, `L5`, `L5'`, `L0'`

Teach this as:

- question
- number
- speech
- naming
- expression

This is the grammar of the field.

### Square B: Encounter

`L1`, `L4`, `L4'`, `L1'`

Teach this as:

- cause and experience
- inside and outside of the issue
- subjective and objective encounter

This is the main entry surface for live inquiry.

### Square C: Transformation

`L2`, `L3`, `L3'`, `L2'`

Teach this as:

- contradiction
- process
- history
- transformation

This is the movement engine of the field.

## `L4/L4'` As One Encounter Axis

One of the most important pedagogical clarifications is that `L4` and `L4'` should not be taught as separate systems.

They are one encounter axis:

- `L4` gives the subjective and phenomenological side
- `L4'` gives the objective and scientific side

Topological mode determines how this axis is handled:

- `torus`: one side foregrounded
- `klein`: both sides held together
- `lemniscatic`: recursive folding across the axis

## `Thought` As Ontology-To-Runtime Bridge

`Thought` should be taught as a runtime bridge, not as the essence of the ontology.

It matters because it externalizes:

- objective process artifacts
- subjective process artifacts
- cross-session continuity
- subagent handoff
- compaction-time synthesis

The key teaching move is:

- ontology remains primary
- artifactization is optional and persistence-dependent
- but when persistence exists, `Thought` becomes a real working layer

## Teaching Cautions

Do not teach this file as:

- a reference dump of all coordinates
- a runtime-only manual
- a flat mapping chart

Do teach it as:

- the bridge from ontology to live traversal
- the reason the runtime has the shape it has
- the place where squares, passes, and topologies become operational
