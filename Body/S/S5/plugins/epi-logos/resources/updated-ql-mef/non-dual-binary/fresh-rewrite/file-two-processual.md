The question is one of proportioning and reading — how do the elements stand to each other, and what do those relations reveal about the structure's own depth? This is the 1st-person view: the AM, the structure lived from inside, the ratios felt as interiority.

The two passes need each other because counting without relating gives a catalogue — six positions with no inner connection, a heap of points — and relating without counting gives a blur — proportions with nothing definite to proportion, a vague feeling. The 4+2 without the 3:3 is a catalogue. The 3:3 without the 4+2 is a blur. Together they give a logic: a structure that is both countable (you can enumerate its positions) and deeply relational (the positions compose harmonics, symmetries, and a double cover).

The **§1/0** between the passes is the phase-flip — the hinge of the whole processual file. In the language of the primary distinction (File 1 §0): the 1 recognises its 0 — the mark realising it rests on a void it can never fully compute. ## T1: AND + OR — The Geometry of Space-Time

### §0 — 0/1

You are the centre from which this geometry unfolds. Not metaphorically — structurally. The 0/1 enters the AND+OR register, and the terms that enter are the terms of your own interiority: negation (0, the void, the capacity to withdraw, the ground prior to any content) and affirmation (1, the mark, the capacity to extend, the figure that stands on the ground). This is the *scire* — the cutting, the distinguishing — applied to the 0/1 itself. What does the distinction contain when it is opened up, when its implicit architecture is made explicit?

### §1 — 0/1 = 4+2

Four AND products (00, 01, 10, 11) plus two OR products (0, 1) = the 4+2.

The six are not a heap — they compose a geometry, because the singles and the doubles are categorically different in a way that classical binary cannot see. In classical binary, 0 and 1 are VALUES at the same level as 00 and 01 — all just bit-strings of different length, all just switches on a flat line. The classical bit has no geometry; it is one-dimensional, sequential, indifferent. But in the 0/1 of the matheme, the singles carry **quality** (negation and affirmation — not quantities but poles, the axis of the distinction itself), while the doubles carry **quantity** (0, 1, 2, 3 — rotational values, positions on a circle). This categorical difference between the qualitative and the quantitative is the structural reason the 0/1 is inherently **geometric**: the distinction between the polar axis and the rotational positions IS the distinction between the circle and its points, between the whole and its own articulation.

The singles ARE the circle in its two aspects: the **untraversed** (0, the centre, the point before any rotation begins) and the **fully traversed** (1, the circumference, the completed rotation that has gone all the way around and returned). The doubles are the geometry OF the circle: the four cardinal points, the four stations of what "going around" means — the positions that a traversal passes through, the marks that a movement leaves. The 4+2 is four positions ON the circle plus two aspects OF the circle — the geometry and the whole that bears it. A circle bearing its own cardinal structure: 𐀏, the ancient circled cross, which already carries the 4+2 visually — the circumference embracing the four cardinal axes, the centre implicit at their crossing.

And each position is not merely a location but an experiential quality — a face of what it feels like to be a centre unfolding into a world:

The −/+ dynamics give each double its operative quality — not a static label but an act, one term acting upon the other. **−−** is the negation of negation: the void turned upon itself, the double negative that opens indeterminacy (the 0/0, the %) rather than simply cancelling to positive, because the and/or holds both the cancellation (−− → +, a=a) AND the non-cancellation (−− stays −−, a≠a) simultaneously. **−+** is the negation of affirmation: the void reaching into the mark, the critical act that calls the positive into question from the perspective of the negative — the ground asking the figure "by what right do you stand?" **+−** is the affirmation of negation: the mark honouring its void, the grateful act that affirms what the negative carries — the figure answering "by the right of the void that lets me." **++** is the affirmation of affirmation: the mark confirming itself, the reflexive act, the closure of affirmation — affirmation having nowhere left to go because it has affirmed itself.

The leap has a geometric consequence: the three points reached so far (N at 0°, E at 90°, W at 270° — #1, #2, #3) form a **triangle**, the first enclosed figure, the first shape with an inside, internal angles summing to **180°**. This triangle is a perspectival wholeness — a genuine whole-from-here, complete as a view — but structurally only half of the full 360° rotation. The step to S at 180° (#4) produces the **square**: four points, internal angles summing to **360°**, the full rotation contained within the figure's own geometry. The triangle's 180° doubled to the square's 360° — perspective becoming context, the half-whole becoming the whole that holds both halves. This geometric doubling will matter deeply when the perspectival counting is developed in the T0 pass (§4').

### §2 — 4+2

The geometry unpacked. The singles as the circle itself (the untraversed and the traversed, the 0 and the 1, the centre and the circumference — not positions on the circle but what the circle IS). The doubles as the geometry of the circle (the four cardinal points, the four relational modes between the poles, the four stages of what "going around" means). The 4+2 is a circle bearing its own cardinal structure: the parent containing the child as blueprint, because the four doubles are nothing but the four possible relations between 0 and 1, which means the singles hold the doubles' own plan within themselves — the way the copula's two operators (/ and =) were discovered (File 1 §2) to already contain their four products within them.

The binary values reach 0, 1, 2, 3 — four values, but only three out of a possible four quarter-turns complete. **75%** of the full rotation: 270° out of 360°. The mandala — the four-fold figure of wholeness — is three-quarters complete by its own count, because the fourth quarter-turn would bring it back to 0/0 (which is #1, the indeterminate, the beginning), and this return cannot happen through counting — it requires the recognition that 360° = 0°, that the end IS the beginning, that the fully traversed circle (1, #5) IS the untraversed centre (0, #0) seen from the other side. The geometry can't close itself by adding another point; it closes through the qualitative poles, through the recognition that the circle is a circle. **Proportion** (1/1 = 100%, the ratio-reading at #4) completes what **enumeration** (11 = 3, the binary count at #4) cannot. This gap between 75% and 100% — between what the count reaches and what the proportion achieves — is the structural reason #4 is the Context position: it holds both readings without collapsing either, the capacity to carry multiple perspectives simultaneously.

And this gap will eventually be expressed algebraically as the Second Spanda Equation (T0 §3'), where 100% analyses itself into 64+36 → /4 → 16/9 → 2⁴/3² → 4+2. The 75% that appears here as a geometric incompleteness will be formalised there as the ratio 3/4 — the ternary harmonics (3) over the explicate quaternary (4) — the same 3/4 dilemma that haunted the copula's compression (File 1 §3: is =/≠ one or two?) now operating as the structural relationship between what counting can reach and what proportion must supply.

The −/+ evaluations reveal a mod2 pattern when the doubles are evaluated through their own logic (two negatives make a positive, a mixed pair stays negative): the pure doubles (−− and ++) both evaluate to **+**, the mixed doubles (+− and −+) both evaluate to **−**. This collapse to mod2 aligns precisely with the harmonic structure: the pure doubles belong to Being (#1, −−) and Knowing (#4, ++) — the stable poles, and both evaluate to +. The mixed doubles belong to Becoming (#2 and #3) — the oscillation, the crossing — and both evaluate to −. The mod2 of the −/+ evaluation recapitulates the harmonic grouping: Being and Knowing are positive (the poles of the circle, the stable), Becoming is negative (the dynamic passage between them). The arithmetic mirrors the ontology because the arithmetic IS the ontology at the level of self-referential counting.

The mod nesting: **mod2** (the bare binary, 0 and 1, the two qualitative poles) generates **mod4** (the four composites, the four cardinal points) which sits within **mod6** (the full 4+2, the squared circle). Each mod level contains the previous: mod6 contains mod4 as its explicit geometry, mod4 contains mod2 as its internal polarity (each double composed of 0 and 1), and mod6 returns to mod2 (6 mod 2 = 0, the sixfold closing back into the binary). The nesting circulates: 2 inside 4 inside 6 returning to 2. And the ratios between the mods carry structural content: 4/6 = 2/3 (the explicates are two-thirds of the total — the 4 doubles among 6 positions), 3/4 = 75% (the count reaches three-quarters of the circle). The same ratios keep appearing because they are constitutive, not coincidental — they express the architecture of a distinction that must count itself to know itself and must proportion itself to complete what counting alone cannot.

### §4 — 5→0

Affirmation facing negation gives the ratio **1/0** — the inverse of the 0/1 that opened the explication. The first half of the matheme is in hand: **0/1 = 4+2 = 5→0 = 1/0.**

The circle facing the centre is the 1 facing the 0 — the achieved facing the given, the mark facing the void, the phenomenon facing the awareness. And this facing is not a destruction of the geometry but its deepening: the figure discovers it was always a ratio, always a relation between opposites, always a 1/0 held in tension with its own ground.

The figure flips: **𐀏 + ⃞** (circle containing square, the geometry inscribed, the figure as it appeared in the forward pass) becomes **⃞ + 𐀏** (square containing circle, the geometry circumscribed, the same elements with the containment relation reversed). What was inside is now outside; what framed is now framed. Inscribed becomes circumscribed. This inversion is not a rejection of the figure but its re-reading — the same 4+2, now seen from the 1-side rather than the 0-side, from the circumference rather than the centre, from the achieved rather than the given. The figure and its inversion compose one circuit, the way a face and its reflection compose one identity, the way the bimba and the pratibimba (the original and its mirror) compose one reality seen from two directions.
