## T1: AND + OR — The Geometry of Space-Time

### §1 — 0/1 = 4+2

Four AND products (00, 01, 10, 11) plus two OR products (0, 1) = the 4+2.

The six are not a heap — they compose a geometry, because the singles and the doubles are categorically different in a way that classical binary cannot see and that the and/or logic supersedes. In classical binary, 0 and 1 are VALUES at the same level as 00 and 01 — all just bit-strings of different length, all just switches on a line. The classical bit has no geometry; it is flat, linear, one-dimensional. In the 0/1 of the matheme, the singles carry **quality** (negation and affirmation — not quantities but poles, the axis of the distinction) while the doubles carry **quantity** (0, 1, 2, 3 — rotational values, positions on a circle). This means the 0/1 is inherently **geometric**: the distinction between the qualitative poles and the quantitative positions is the distinction between the circle and its points, between the whole and its articulation, between the topology and the geometry.

The singles ARE the circle in its two aspects: the **untraversed** (0, the centre, the point before rotation begins) and the **fully traversed** (1, the circumference, the completed rotation that has gone all the way around and returned). The doubles are the geometry OF the circle: the four cardinal points, the four stations of what "going around" means. The 4+2 is four positions ON the circle plus two aspects OF the circle — the geometry and the whole that bears it. A circle bearing its own cardinal structure: **𐀏**, the ancient circled cross, which already carries the 4+2 visually — the circle (circumference) crossed by the four (cardinal axes), the point (centre) implicit.

The binary values (0, 1, 2, 3) count rotational positions — four quarter-turns marking the cardinal directions. But the ordering goes N(0°) → E(90°) → W(270°) → S(180°), not smoothly around. The binary count JUMPS, and the jump is the phase-flip: the same structural discontinuity that the full matheme describes as the global turn at 5→0 is already operating locally between #2 and #3, between the negation of affirmation (−+) and the affirmation of negation (+−), between the expression (0/1) and its inverse (1/0). ### §2 — 4+2

The geometry unpacked. The singles as the circle itself (the untraversed and the traversed, the 0 and the 1, the centre and the circumference — not positions on the circle but what the circle IS). The doubles as the geometry of the circle (the four cardinal points, the four relational modes between the poles, the four stages of what "going around" means). The 4+2 is a circle bearing its own cardinal structure: the parent containing the child as blueprint, because the four doubles are nothing but the four possible relations between 0 and 1, which means the singles hold the doubles' own plan within themselves.

The binary values reach 0, 1, 2, 3 — four values, but only three out of a possible four quarter-turns complete. **75%** of the full rotation: 270° out of 360°. The mandala — the four-fold figure of wholeness — is three-quarters complete by its own count, because the fourth quarter-turn would bring it back to 0/0 (which is #1, the indeterminate, the beginning), and this return cannot happen through counting — it requires the recognition that 360° = 0°, that the end IS the beginning, that the traversed (1) IS the untraversed (0) seen from the other side. The geometry can't close itself by adding another point; it closes through the qualitative poles, through the recognition that the circle is a circle. Proportion (1/1 = 100%, achieved through the slash-reading at #4) completes what enumeration (11 = 3, the binary count at #4) cannot. This gap between 75% and 100% — between what the count reaches and what the proportion achieves — is the structural reason #4 is the Context position: it holds both readings without collapsing either, the capacity to carry multiple perspectives simultaneously.

The classical binary values (00=0, 01=1, 10=2, 11=3) are retained within the and/or as its quantitative face — the either/or sitting inside the and/or as one reading among others. The and/or does not destroy classical binary logic; it includes it as the explicate content while adding the implicate depth (the qualitative poles, the slash-forms, the harmonics) that the either/or alone cannot see. Classical logic is the #1-#4 centre; QL is the #0-#5 frame that holds it.

The −/+ evaluations reveal a mod2 pattern when the doubles are evaluated through their own logic (two negatives make a positive, a mixed pair stays negative): the pure doubles (−− and ++) both evaluate to **+**, the mixed doubles (+− and −+) both evaluate to **−**. This collapse to mod2 aligns precisely with the harmonic structure: the pure doubles belong to Being (#1, −−) and Knowing (#4, ++), which are the stable poles, and both evaluate to +. The mixed doubles belong to Becoming (#2 and #3), which is the oscillation, and both evaluate to −. The mod2 of the −/+ evaluation recapitulates the harmonic grouping: Being and Knowing are positive (the poles of the circle, the stable), Becoming is negative (the crossing between them, the dynamic).

The mod nesting: **mod2** (the bare binary, 0 and 1, the two poles) generates **mod4** (the four composites, the four cardinal points) which sits within **mod6** (the full 4+2, the squared circle). Each mod level contains the previous: mod6 contains mod4 as its explicit geometry, mod4 contains mod2 as its internal polarity (each double composed of 0 and 1), and mod6 returns to mod2 (6 mod 2 = 0, the sixfold closing back into the binary). The nesting circulates: 2 inside 4 inside 6 returning to 2. And the ratios between the mods carry structural content: 4/6 = 2/3 (the explicates are two-thirds of the total), 3/4 = 75% (the count reaches three-quarters of the circle). The same ratios keep appearing because they are constitutive, not accidental.

### §4 — 5→0

The figure and its inversion compose one circuit, the way the face and its reflection compose one identity.
