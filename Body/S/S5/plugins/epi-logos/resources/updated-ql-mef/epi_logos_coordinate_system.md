### L1 — Causal (Day, #1)
**Root:** Aristotle, Icchā Śakti, Schopenhauer
**Key Concept:** Will as Causal Force; Four Causes
**Element:** Earth
**Möbius Return:** L1 → L4'

The Causal lens refracts the P-positions through the question: *why do things happen?* It synthesizes three traditions of causal understanding: Aristotle's four causes (the most complete classical analysis), Kashmir Shaivism's Icchā Śakti (the divine will-power grounding all causation), and Schopenhauer's Will (the blind, irrational striving underlying all phenomena).

Aristotle held that "we think we do not have knowledge of a thing until we have grasped its *why*, that is to say, its cause" (*Physics* II.3). His four causes are not four *types* of explanation but four *aspects* any complete explanation must address. Modern science collapsed causation almost entirely into efficient cause (mechanism), repressing formal cause (pattern) and final cause (purpose). The Epi-Logos framework insists on restoring all four — and grounds them in the deeper meta-causal principles of absolute freedom (Svātantrya) and absolute will (Icchā Śakti).

The nondual perspective from the research: "Material cause is physical substance *and* the psychic substrate of experience. Efficient cause is external force *and* inner impulse. Formal cause is objective structure *and* subjective archetype. Final cause is immediate goal *and* ultimate meaning." Each cause has a dual aspect bridging matter and mind — the psychoid character of causation itself.

**6-fold:**

- **L1-0 — Svātantrya — Essential/Absolute Freedom Causation**: The meta-causal ground. In Kashmir Shaivism, *svātantrya* is the absolute freedom of consciousness — not a specific cause but the *capacity to cause*, the unconditioned power that makes all causal operation possible. This is the ground of causation itself: why is there causation at all?

- **L1-1 — Material Cause**: That out of which — the *hyle*, the substrate, the physical givenness. Mapped to Sensation: the immediate, concrete thereness of what is given. The earth-principle: solidity, resistance, raw material.

- **L1-2 — Efficient Cause**: That by which — the agent, the force, the energy that transforms. Mapped to Feeling: the energetic valuation that moves things, the "doing" that actually transforms substrate into form. Modern science's privileged cause, but only one of four.

- **L1-3 — Formal Cause**: The what-it-is — the *eidos*, the pattern, the organizing architecture. Mapped to Thinking: the logical structure that determines *what kind* of thing something is. The cause modern materialism most neglects yet cannot function without: every scientific "law" IS a formal cause.

- **L1-4 — Final Cause**: That for the sake of which — the *telos*, the purpose, the aim. Mapped to Intuition: the perception of possibilities and purposes. Split into immanent finality (the embedded purposes organisms naturally pursue — survival, growth) and transcendent finality (the possible cosmic purpose — the universe's drive toward consciousness, complexity, self-awareness). The cause most actively repressed in modern thought, yet constantly returning as "the hard problem," "the anthropic principle," "the arrow of evolution."

- **L1-5 — Icchā Śakti / Schopenhauer's Will — Ultimate Causation**: The synthesis — the *will* underlying all four causes. Icchā Śakti is the first movement of Śiva toward manifestation: the will-to-create that precedes and grounds all subsequent causal operation. Schopenhauer's Will is the thing-in-itself: the blind, insatiable striving that IS the inner nature of phenomena. This is the quintessence: will as meta-causal power that "expresses itself through all four causes as their unifying impulse."

The research documents show this lens's extensive reach: from Nāgārjuna and Jain *syādvāda* (seven-fold "maybe" logic), through Belnap's four-valued logic (True, False, Both, Neither), paraconsistent logics that reject the explosion principle, intuitionistic logic's rejection of excluded middle, to Wittgenstein's "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent." The lens also connects to computational paradigms: non-monotonic reasoning, belief revision, active inference — all of which rediscover the ancient wisdom of flexible, multi-valued logic in formal guise.

The lens asserts that "Logic is not one-dimensional... Contradiction, ambiguity, and context are not flaws to be eliminated from reasoning, but essential features of a *living, evolving logic* — a logic *of becoming*." The goal is not to abandon reason but to fulfill reason's highest calling: to lead to understanding in whatever form it must take.

**6-fold:**

- **L2-2 — IS-NOT — Non-being, negation, falsity**: The apophatic negation — what can be definitively denied. The boundary-drawing function: definition by exclusion.

- **L2-3 — BOTH: IS and IS-NOT — Contradiction, dialetheism**: The paradoxical position where contradictions genuinely coexist. - **L2-4 — NEITHER: IS nor IS-NOT — Void, ineffability**: Transcendence of the binary framework entirely. Nāgārjuna's *śūnyatā* — not mere nothingness but the emptiness of inherent existence in any conceptual determination. - **L2-5 — SILENCE — Irrepresentable / Unprovable Truth**: The fifth position beyond the four — the Buddha's silence on the *avyākata* (undeclared questions), Gödel's unprovable truths, Wittgenstein's silence. The recognition that the most fundamental truths may be precisely those that cannot be stated propositionally. "Pregnant silence" that is not absence of meaning but superabundance of it.

### L3 — Processual (Day, #3)
**Root:** Whitehead, Bergson, Lacan
**Key Concept:** Concrescence, Creative Advance into Novelty
**Element:** Water
**Möbius Return:** L3 → L2'

The Processual lens refracts the P-positions through the dynamics of *becoming*. Grounded in Whitehead's *Process and Reality* (1929), it views reality not as a collection of static substances but as a flow of **actual occasions** undergoing phases of development. In the MEF's organic-symbolic mapping, these phases become: Soil, Seeding, Sprouting, Blooming, Flowering, Maturity.

Whitehead's central insight: the fundamental units of reality are not substances but momentary events of experience that "come into being" by integrating many influences into a new unity. Each concrescing occasion "feels" its universe through *prehension* — grasping both the concrete facts of past occasions (physical prehension) and novel possibilities (conceptual prehension/eternal objects). Guided by its *subjective aim* — an inner telos — the occasion proceeds "from indeterminacy to determinacy" until it achieves *satisfaction*: a fully determinate state. Whitehead: "The many become one, and are increased by one."

The Lacan mapping is structural: Lacan's *manque* (lack) — the constitutive absence at the heart of desire — maps to the initial phase of concrescence where the occasion lacks its own completion. *Jouissance* (excessive enjoyment beyond the pleasure principle) maps to satisfaction/perishing — the ecstatic completion that is simultaneously dissolution. This is not substantive identity but formal isomorphism: the same structure of lack-drive-completion operating at psychoanalytic and cosmological densities.

Bergson's *durée* (lived duration) provides the experiential dimension: time as continuous creative flow rather than measured sequence. Prigogine and Stengers' work on self-organization in far-from-equilibrium systems provides the scientific dimension: order emerging spontaneously from chaos through dissipative structures.

**6-fold:**

- **L3-0 — Concrescent Desire As Creative Urge — Lacanian Lack**: The ground of becoming — pure appetition toward determination. The occasion is not yet anything; it *lacks* its own completion and is driven toward it. Lacan's *manque-à-être*: the lack-of-being that drives the subject toward meaning.

- **L3-1 — Actual Occasion**: The fundamental unit of reality — a "drop of experience" receiving its initial data through physical prehension of the entire past actual world. Not an enduring substance but a momentary event of integration.

Dasein is not an isolated subject observing an objective world but *being-in-the-world*: always already engaged with equipment, projects, and other Dasein in a meaningful context. As the research shows: "We do not start as isolated minds and then somehow connect to the world. We start as beings already engaged with a world of meaningful things and other people."

The meta-epistemic research documents the full progression: from Husserl's *Lebenswelt* (life-world — the taken-for-granted background of all knowing) through world-disclosure (things showing up as intelligible), epistemological reflection (knowing examining itself), psychological depth (the knower's unconscious structures), contextual situatedness (the irreducibility of perspective), to *Jñāna* — integral wisdom where knower, knowing, and known unite. **6-fold:**
