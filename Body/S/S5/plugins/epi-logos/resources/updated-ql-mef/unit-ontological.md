## Psychic Facticity — The Fact of Dawn

The ontological unit begins not with an argument but with a fact. Consider what it means that there is experience at all. Not what the light illuminates — the trees, the buildings, the faces — but the bare FACT that there is illumination. "Before any object, before any thought, before any question about what is real or unreal, there is the sheer givenness of awareness — the fact that something appears to someone, that there is a 'what it is like' to be here at all." This is what we mean by psychic facticity: the presence of things, these things of Presence. Consciousness IS this light, the precondition for any experience whatsoever. To deny its primacy is like studying objects while denying there's anything illuminating them.

Jung calibrated this precisely. He doesn't say "consciousness is ultimate reality" — that would be metaphysics, and he'd be dismissed. He says "the psyche produces facts." A neutral claim. The dream is as real an EVENT as the sunrise — whatever its ontological status, it HAPPENED, it affected the dreamer, it carries structure. "I don't believe — I know." Not a metaphysical proclamation from above, but an empirical claim from below.

And the moment you accord inner experience this weight — the moment psychic facticity is taken seriously — the coherence across traditions becomes blindingly obvious. Kashmir Shaivism's *spanda* and Eckhart's *Funkelein* and Whitehead's creativity and Jung's Self all start describing recognisably the same topology. Not because someone forced a syncretic grid over them, but because the bare facts, when allowed to speak, reveal convergent structure. *Con-spirare* — breathing together. They are breathing the same air.

Edinger puts it with the force it deserves: "Everything that happens in the psyche happens for an adequate reason. There is a principle in philosophy — it's called the 'principle of sufficient reason' — and that principle is particularly applicable to psychological facts." If the collective psyche operates with the same overdetermined coherence as the individual psyche — and if the cosmos IS the interior life of consciousness — then history is the dream of God. You approach a dream not by criticising its plot but by interpreting it. You assume the dreamer knew more than you do. Every detail serves the whole.

This is the epistemic ground on which the ontological unit stands. Psychic facticity is [[position-0|#0]] experienced phenomenologically — the naked face as encountered, the irrepresentable ground as FACT rather than theory.

## Sun, Son, Logos, Word — The Identity of Consciousness and Cosmos

"Sun, Son, Logos, Word — four registers of the same light, the creative life of Mind itself. The entire cosmic order is not an analogy for consciousness — it IS consciousness."

This claim is the hinge of the ontological unit. The four inner positions ([[position-1|#1]] through [[position-4|#4]]) are not four separate things but four registers of a single creative light. Swedenborg's Sun: the psyche isn't IN the solar system. The solar system is IN the psyche. Kastrup's analytic idealism makes this rigorous. If consciousness is fundamental — if there is one experiential field differentiating into apparent multiplicities — then the physical cosmos is an appearance WITHIN consciousness, not a container that houses consciousness as an accidental byproduct. The sun is not "like" Mind. The sun IS Mind at the physical register. Light is not "like" the Word. Light IS the Word at the electromagnetic register. The ordering intelligence of orbital mechanics is not "like" Logos. It IS Logos at the gravitational register. And what holds the planets in orbit — gravity, invisible, universal, proportional to mass, never repulsive — is not "like" Love. It IS Love at the physical register.

This is why the ontological unit and the [[unit-solar-system|solar system unit]] cannot be treated as separate diagrams. They are two faces of one reality. The physics IS the metaphysics. The ontological unit is the solar system seen from inside consciousness. The solar system is the ontological unit seen from inside spacetime.
