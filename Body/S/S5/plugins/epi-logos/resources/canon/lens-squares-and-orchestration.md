# Lens Squares and Orchestration

## The Architecture

The twelve lenses (`L0–L5` and their primes `L0'–L5'`) are organized into three squares. This organization is not arbitrary — each square has a distinct role in the architecture of a run, and the squares are designed to work together rather than independently.

The twelve is not six plus footnotes. The prime lenses (`L0'–L5'`) are genuine lens forms in their own right — the inversive, analytic, implicit face of the same investigative territory that the Day lenses address explicitly. L2' is not "L2 applied critically." It is the face of the logical territory that official logic cannot see. L4' is not "L4 checked" — it is the scientific encounter with objective structure, a full lens in its own right (and the home of the L4' loop). Each prime exists because the topology requires it: six positions in one orientation plus six in the inversive orientation equals twelve, following the same 4g+2 structural necessity as the positions.

Do not treat the primes as derived or secondary. The Day/Night structure is constitutive of what the lenses are, not a mode you enter for extra rigor.

The design principle: implement square-aware orchestration plus a small number of strong rubrics. Do not implement twelve unrelated lens skills.

## Square A: Articulation

`L0`, `L5`, `L5'`, `L0'`

Square A governs articulation — question grammar, number, naming, expression, compression into sayable form. This is where the inquiry gets its shape as something that can be stated, structured, and communicated.

**`L0`** works with the quantitative-qualitative split at the most fundamental level. It is concerned with how number, void-relation, archetype, and formal pattern operate before content is fully differentiated. Things that "go without saying" in a discourse often live here — the implicit counting logic, the assumed units, the tacit structural decisions.

**`L5`** operates at the level where speech and logos meet incarnation — where structure becomes embodied, where the density of a description touches what it describes. This is the lens concerned with how language is or is not adequate to its object, how formalism and experience are or are not commensurable.

**`L5'` and `L0'`** are the prime inversions of these — the shadow side of articulation, where what cannot be said or counted shows itself as absence, gap, or aporia.

Square A is often in the background during active runs, but it becomes foregrounded when the inquiry is about how something is framed, named, or made expressible — and when that framing is itself part of the problem.

## Square B: Encounter

`L1`, `L4`, `L4'`, `L1'`

Square B is the main runtime entry surface. Most runs begin here and return here.

**`L1`** deals with causal structure — what is treated as cause, what kinds of causation are recognized as legitimate (efficient, formal, material, final), and which causes are repressed or silenced. The privilege-repression dynamic in science, philosophy, and institutional thinking is typically an `L1` phenomenon.

**`L4`** is phenomenological disclosure — how the issue is lived, encountered, inhabited. This is the subjective face of the encounter axis. It does not mean "feelings" in a dismissive sense. It means the full depth of how something shows up for the person or community that is inside it: disclosure, mood, situatedness, the transformation of the knower through encounter.

**`L4'`** is scientific intervention — the objective face of the same encounter axis. Current state, desired state, constraints, observable evidence, candidate interventions, verification conditions. This is the `L4'` loop: Observe, Think, Plan, Build, Execute, Verify, Learn.

**`L1'`** is phenomenal immediacy — the raw given of experience before it is structured causally or scientifically. It is often the residue that `L1` and `L4'` cannot account for.

`L4` and `L4'` are one encounter axis, not two separate frameworks. This is the structural heart of the plugin. A run that routes subjective and objective as parallel but independent systems misses the point. The tension between how something is lived and how it is observed is where most of the real work lives.

## Square C: Transformation

`L2`, `L3`, `L3'`, `L2'`

Square C is the movement engine — contradiction, process, history, alchemical change, paradox carried into transformation.

**`L2`** operates at the logic-breakdown dialectic — the official logic of a field, and where that logic hits its own limits. Tetralemma is L2's primary instrument: the five-move rubric (IS / IS-NOT / BOTH / NEITHER / SILENCE) that makes the logic-breakdown traversable rather than terminal. This is the correct address for tetralemma — not QL, which structures the inquiry, but L2, which opens the logical space when that inquiry hits a contradiction it cannot dissolve.

**`L3`** operates at substance-process contradiction — what is named as stable and thing-like, versus where process, becoming, and unfolding break through anyway. Fields that insist on substance-language often betray themselves at `L3` when they try to account for change, development, or history.

**`L3'`** is the prime of process — the latent or unacknowledged processual structure that a discourse depends on while refusing to name.

**`L2'`** is the prime of logic — the logical breakdown that a field cannot acknowledge because doing so would destabilize its foundations.

Square C comes to the foreground in diagnostic work, in tracking how contradictions develop over time, and in understanding what transformation actually means rather than what it is said to mean.

## Orchestration Principles

**Squares are not independent.** They are three faces of one structure. A run that uses only Square B misses the articulation work of Square A and the transformation work of Square C. In practice, Square B is the entry because encounter is where most runs begin, but the work moves through all three.

**The encounter axis (`L4/L4'`) is the primary runtime pair.** It is where subjective and objective aspects of the same inquiry are held in relation. Square B being the "main runtime entry surface" means this — not that the other squares are subordinate, but that most inquiries begin by encountering something that needs to be understood from both inside and outside simultaneously.

**Lenses are instruments, not labels.** Naming a lens is not the same as applying it. `L1` applied means actually tracing the privilege-repression structure of a specific field — not noting that privilege-repression is a relevant concept and moving on.

**Square-aware orchestration means using lenses in relation.** CMEA sweeps through multiple lenses deliberately, using each one to reveal something the others might miss, and then asking what they show together. This is different from running lenses sequentially as independent modules.
