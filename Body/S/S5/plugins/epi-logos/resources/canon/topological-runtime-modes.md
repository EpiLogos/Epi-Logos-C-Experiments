# Topological Runtime Modes

Topology in Epi-Logos is not metaphor or decoration. It is the runtime shape of a traversal — the logic that determines how the encounter axis moves, what kinds of contradiction can be held, and what synthesis is actually available. Choosing the wrong topology for a run does not just make the work aesthetically off. It makes certain truths unavailable.

## Why Six Positions: The 4g + 2 Formula

The system has exactly six positions because algebraic topology requires it.

The fundamental polygon of a genus-g orientable surface has **4g edges**. For a torus — genus g = 1 — this gives 4 edges, corresponding to the four explicate positions (P1–P4) that define the working manifold of an inquiry. The two poles — P0 as ground, P5 as synthesis-return — correspond to the two identified vertices of the fundamental polygon. The structural formula is **4g + 2**.

For g = 1: 4(1) + 2 = **6 positions**. Not five, not seven — six, because that is the minimum structure of a genus-1 orientable closed surface.

This is not numerology or design preference. It is the algebraic topological DNA of the torus, which is why the system keeps producing sixfold rhythms at every scale. The positions are forced by the topology.

The same formula explains why there are twelve lenses (L0–L5 and their primes L0'–L5'): six Day positions and six Night positions — the same surface traversed in both orientations. The prime positions are not extras added for coverage. They are what the topology generates when you traverse the surface in its complementary orientation.

## The 6 → 12 Move: Torus to Klein

Six positions (torus: one orientation, stable circulation) become twelve when the full inversive structure is held simultaneously (Klein: both orientations at once, inside and outside as one surface). This is not a traversal option layered on top of the positions — it is the structural relationship between torus and Klein as modes of the same positional field.

**Traversal shape IS positional shape.** The prime positions and lenses are not artifacts of checking or critique. They are genuine positional and lens forms in their own right — the other face of the same topology. P1' is not "what P1 looks like when you're being critical of it." P1' is the night face of that positional territory, as structurally real as P1. The same applies to every prime lens: L2' is not a critical application of L2 — it is the inversive face of the same logical territory.

This is what *complexio oppositorum* means structurally: the Day and Night faces (P and P', L and L') are not held together by the act of traversal — they are constitutive of what the positions ARE. Each position contains its inversion as a structural feature. The primes don't emerge when you "do Night mode." They are always already there as the other half of the surface. Process is structure.

## Möbius: The Underlying Logic

The Möbius strip is the image of self-inversion. Its surface has only one side — inside and outside are the same surface, connected through a half-twist. This is the topology that underlies the whole plugin.

Möbius logic is always present in any Epi-Logos run. It is why paradox is treated as coherent rather than self-defeating. It is why positions and lenses can be holographically related rather than merely parallel. It is the structure that makes inversion and return possible without collapse.

Möbius is not typically selected as a standalone run mode. It is more like the ground that the other modes stand on.

## Torus: Stable Circulation

Choose `torus` when the inquiry can move through a coherent one-sided circulation without the field needing to turn inside-out.

**Runtime character:** A torus circulates. One aspect of the encounter axis leads — `L4` or `L4'` — while the other remains available but not foregrounded. The traversal can move through the positions without the topology itself becoming the problem.

**Use when:**
- the inquiry is tractable without heavy inversion
- one side of the encounter axis can lead while the other serves as reality check
- the task is practical, exploratory, or explanatory without deep frame conflict
- contradiction is not the primary material of the inquiry

**Failure mode:** Using `torus` while contradiction is still active. This flattens what needs to remain paradoxical. The run will look coherent while hiding the real issue.

## Klein: Doubled Encounter

Choose `klein` when the inquiry must hold inside/outside, subjective/objective, or explicit/implicit together — when one-sided circulation would hide the issue.

**Runtime character:** A klein bottle has no inside or outside in the usual sense. Like the Möbius strip, it is non-orientable, but it is a closed surface. Each side exposes and generates the other. In a klein run, both sides of the encounter axis are required simultaneously, and the work consists partly in holding them without forcing premature resolution.

**Use when:**
- contradiction persists and cannot be dissolved by taking one side
- the problem changes depending on who names it or from what perspective
- diagnosis is central — the explicit structure is cracking against the implicit
- each side exposes the other's blind spots in ways that are productive rather than merely frustrating
- frame conflict is the actual material of the inquiry

**Failure mode:** Using `klein` to sound profound when one-sided circulation would do. Klein is not inherently deeper than torus — it is appropriate to a different class of problem.

## Lemniscatic: Recursive Contextual Fold

Choose `lemniscatic` when the inquiry must generate its own higher-order opening through recursive contextual folding — when `P4` is doing real work and the synthesis has to be earned by traversing context rather than arrived at from outside.

**Runtime character:** The lemniscate (∞) crosses itself at a center point. A lemniscatic run folds between both sides of the encounter axis through `P4`, and each pass through the fold reframes what came before. The transcendence toward `P5` is generated internally — through the recursion — not imposed from outside the inquiry.

**Use when:**
- `P4` is doing real recontextualizing work rather than merely adding background
- the inquiry contains nested reframings that change the nature of earlier positions
- neither `torus` nor `klein` alone can produce the synthesis needed
- the `.0–.5` nesting structure is genuinely at work

**Failure mode:** Using `lemniscatic` to avoid making concrete progress — treating recursive unfolding as endless rather than as a process with a specific shape and endpoint.

## Switching Modes Mid-Run

Topology can be revised mid-run when the inquiry shows its real shape. If a run begins `torus` and contradiction emerges that torus cannot hold, the honest move is to escalate to `klein`. If `P4` begins doing recursive work that neither `torus` nor `klein` can accommodate, escalation to `lemniscatic` is appropriate.

What is not appropriate is leaving the topology implicit. The topology is always operating — the choice is between choosing it consciously and letting it operate by default, which usually means defaulting to `torus` even when the field demands more.
