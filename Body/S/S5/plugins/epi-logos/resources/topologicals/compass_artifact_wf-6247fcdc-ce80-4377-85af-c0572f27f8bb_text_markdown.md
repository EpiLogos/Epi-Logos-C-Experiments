# The torus as instrument: Declassified government consciousness research and its geometric cosmology

The CIA's 1983 Gateway Process report presents the universe as a **self-contained toroidal hologram** where human consciousness operates as a tunable vibrational pattern, capable of accessing information across space-time by "attuning" to different frequencies within this structure. This instrumental framework—treating consciousness as technology rather than phenomenon requiring ethical grounding—runs consistently through **$30+ million** in declassified US government psi research spanning 1950-1995, alongside parallel Soviet "psychotronics" programs estimated at **$0.5-1 billion**. The research treated toroidal geometry, holographic information encoding, and consciousness manipulation as engineering problems rather than metaphysical questions.

The through-line connecting these programs is remarkable: from J.B. Rhine's statistical card experiments at Duke in the 1930s, through MKULTRA's electromagnetic consciousness manipulation in the 1950s-60s, to the explicit toroidal cosmology of the Gateway Process and its operational descendants in Stargate remote viewing. Government researchers consistently sought to instrumentalize consciousness—whether for interrogation enhancement, intelligence collection, or psychic warfare—without engaging with what such capacities might imply about the nature of reality or human responsibility.

## The Gateway torus: A universal hologram of consciousness

The most explicit government articulation of toroidal consciousness appears in **"Analysis and Assessment of Gateway Process"** (CIA-RDP96-00788R001700210016-5), authored by Lt. Col. Wayne M. McDonnell for the US Army Intelligence and Security Command in June 1983. Drawing heavily on biomedical engineer Itzhak Bentov's work, the document describes the universe as:

>"A 'Torus' because it is thought to have the overall shape of an immense, self-contained spiral... Working from the widely accepted 'Big Bang' theory Bentov presents a conceptual model to depict the process of time-space evolution the relative position of the universal hologram."

The model describes a **"cosmic egg"** structure with a white hole at one pole emitting matter, which travels around the torus shell and exits through a black hole at the opposite pole back into the nucleus—a continuous creation cycle generating an interference pattern that "constitutes the universal hologram or Torus." Human consciousness, according to the Gateway framework, operates as another hologram that can "attune itself to the universal hologram by the medium of energy exchange thereby deducing meaning and achieving the state which we call consciousness."

**The "click out" mechanism** provides the operational core: when consciousness achieves sufficient coherence through Hemi-Sync binaural beat entrainment, its vibrational frequency can drop below the Planck distance threshold (10⁻³³ centimeters per second), at which point it "clicks out" of normal space-time and gains access to information anywhere within the toroidal structure. The document states explicitly: "Since the Torus is being simultaneously generated by matter in all the various phases of 'time,' it reflects the development of the universe in the past, present, and future."

The report integrates **David Bohm's implicate order theory** and **Karl Pribram's holonomic brain model**, stating that "the human mind is also a hologram which attunes itself to the universal hologram." This provides theoretical justification for remote viewing—consciousness can access information encoded anywhere in the universal hologram regardless of space-time location. The right brain hemisphere receives holographic input; the left hemisphere translates it through binary comparison into conscious awareness.

## Bentov's biomedical foundation for the toroidal model

Itzhak Bentov (1923-1979), the Czech-born Israeli-American biomedical engineer whose work forms the scientific backbone of the Gateway Process document, developed what he called the **"physio-kundalini reflex"**—a mechanical model explaining how altered consciousness states arise from physiological oscillation. His framework treats the human cardiovascular system as a "finely tuned oscillator" that can synchronize with both brain rhythms and Earth's magnetic field pulsations.

The mechanism: During deep meditation, heart rate, respiratory rate, and diaphragm depth reach a critical balance creating a **standing wave at 7 Hz** in the abdominal aorta. This frequency corresponds precisely to brain alpha rhythm and Earth's Schumann resonance (**7-7.5 Hz**). The rhythmical stimulus creates standing waves within brain ventricles that mechanically stimulate the sensory cortex, generating what Bentov described as a circulating "current" traveling in closed loops around each hemisphere.

Bentov's **toroidal cosmology**, elaborated in his posthumous work *A Cosmic Book* (1988), proposed:
>"The Creation is a big hologram in the shape of an elongated torus, or smoke-ring doughnut. Its holographic nature guarantees that all information about itself is distributed uniformly throughout, which means the human, embedded in this matrix, has full access in consciousness to knowledge of the entire Creation."

He suggested **49 universes** arranged in a helical coil, with 500,000 of these forming one super-cosmos. The Gateway document adopted Bentov's framework wholesale, including his analogy comparing "the still depths of the sea to the dimension of the Absolute while assigning the storm-tossed waves above to represent the physical universe."

This represents perhaps the most striking feature of the government research: treating mystical cosmological claims as engineering specifications. The Monroe Institute's Hemi-Sync technology was explicitly designed to entrain the brain to frequencies Bentov identified as consciousness-altering, with the stated goal of achieving vibrational coherence between brain hemispheres.

## Historical origins: From Rhine to MKULTRA electromagnetic research

### Rhine's statistical parapsychology and government interest

Government interest in consciousness manipulation predates the Cold War. **J.B. Rhine** at Duke University (1930-1965) developed the first statistically rigorous parapsychology research program, introducing Zener card experiments and coining the term "Extra-Sensory Perception." According to declassified documents, **the CIA purchased ESP cards directly from Rhine**, and "Dr. J.B. Rhine, the dean of American parapsychology, conducted extrasensory perception (ESP) tests for the Army as far back as 1952."

Rhine's contribution was methodological: he conducted what historians recognize as **the first meta-analysis in science** (*Extra-Sensory Perception After Sixty Years*, 1940), applying statistical inference to claims about consciousness. This framework—treating psi as measurable signal against noise—would define all subsequent government research.

### Operation Paperclip and consciousness research continuity

**Operation Paperclip** (established July 1945) brought over 1,600 German scientists to the United States, including figures with direct relevance to consciousness manipulation research. **Kurt Plötner**, an SS physician at Dachau concentration camp, conducted mescaline experiments on Jewish and Russian prisoners attempting to develop a "truth serum" to "eliminate the will of the person examined." He was recruited by US Navy intelligence in 1945 and protected from French prosecution.

Historian Stephen Kinzer documents that MKULTRA was, in significant part, **a continuation of earlier Nazi experiments**—MKULTRA's use of mescaline on unwitting subjects replicated previous Nazi experiments at Dachau. This represents the darkest dimension of the instrumental approach: consciousness research inherited methods developed in concentration camps.

### MKULTRA Subproject 119: Electromagnetic activation

The Korean War "brainwashing" scare triggered institutional commitment. On April 13, 1953, CIA Director Allen Dulles authorized MKULTRA as a "Manhattan Project of the Mind" with an initial **$300,000 budget**. The program eventually comprised **149 subprojects** across 80+ institutions, with estimated total funding exceeding **$10 million** (approximately $87.5 million inflation-adjusted).

**Subproject 119** specifically addressed electromagnetic consciousness manipulation. Approved in 1960 based on Professor Saul B. Sells' proposal, it included among its five research objectives:

>"Techniques for Activating the Human Organism by Remote Electronic Means"

Researchers Dr. Mary Brazier and Dr. Ross Adey at UCLA's Brain Research Institute realized that "the brain irradiates extremely low frequencies" and brainwaves could potentially be manipulated electromagnetically. This research recognized that consciousness had electromagnetic correlates that could, in principle, be externally influenced—a finding that would later inform both the Gateway Process binaural beat technology and continued interest in bioelectromagnetic effects.

## Stargate and operational remote viewing

### Program evolution and funding

The CIA's psi research evolved through multiple code names: **SCANATE** (1972-1976), **GONDOLA WISH** (1977-1978), **GRILL FLAME** (1978-1983), **CENTER LANE** (1983-1985), **SUN STREAK** (1985-1991), and finally **STARGATE** (1991-1995). Total expenditure reached approximately **$20 million over 23 years**, with peak staffing of **22 active military and civilian remote viewers**.

At Stanford Research Institute (SRI), physicists **Hal Puthoff** and **Russell Targ** worked with claimed psychic **Ingo Swann** to develop **Coordinate Remote Viewing (CRV)**—a structured six-stage protocol designed to minimize "analytical overlay" (conscious interpretation contaminating raw psychic impressions). Swann proposed the term "remote viewing" in December 1971, and by 1981-1983 had developed the formal CRV training methodology documented in classified SRI reports.

The program operated on an implicit theoretical framework derived from the holographic model: if information is encoded non-locally in a universal hologram, properly trained consciousness could access it regardless of spatial or temporal distance. This was never treated as metaphysical speculation but as engineering hypothesis to be operationally tested.

### The 1995 termination and evaluation

The American Institutes for Research (AIR) review, commissioned when funding collapsed in 1994, produced famously ambivalent conclusions. Statistician **Jessica Utts** found statistically significant positive effects—some subjects scored **5-15% above chance expectation** with effect sizes greater than 0.6 (four standard deviations). Skeptic **Ray Hyman** countered that Utts' conclusion "that ESP had been proven is premature, to say the least."

The executive summary's operational verdict proved definitive:
>"Remote viewings have never provided an adequate basis for 'actionable' intelligence operations—that is, information sufficiently valuable or compelling so that action was taken as a result."

The program was terminated on September 6, 1995. Former participants disputed this assessment vehemently—Paul H. Smith described the AIR report as a "poorly-executed hatchet job."

## Soviet psychotronics and the absence of toroidal models

Soviet psi research, running parallel from approximately 1919-2003 with estimated expenditure of **$0.5-1 billion**, operated under different theoretical constraints. Marxist dialectical materialism required that any genuine psi phenomena have a materialist explanation, pushing Soviet researchers toward **electromagnetic field theories** rather than the holographic or toroidal models adopted by American programs.

**Leonid Vasiliev** (1891-1966), the "father of Soviet parapsychology," initially proposed that telepathy operated through high-frequency electromagnetic radiation from the brain. Experiments using Faraday cages and metal shielding ultimately **falsified** this hypothesis—telepathy appeared to work through shielding that blocked all known electromagnetic frequencies. Vasiliev admitted the electromagnetic hypothesis "encounters a number of difficulties and contraindications which have not yet been eliminated."

Later Soviet research turned to **torsion field theory** (proposed by Akimov and Shipov in the 1980s), claiming spin-spin interactions could transmit information at speeds up to a billion times light speed. This theory was **officially exposed as pseudoscience** by physicist Eugene Alexandrov in 1991; the USSR Academy of Sciences concluded it was "a scam to get money from the state."

**No explicit toroidal consciousness models** appear in accessible Soviet documentation. The theoretical divergence is striking: American research gravitating toward holographic and toroidal frameworks while Soviet research attempted (and failed) to develop materialist electromagnetic explanations. Both programs shared the instrumental orientation—treating consciousness as potential military capability rather than philosophical puzzle.

## The belief effect: Recognition as activation

Perhaps the most significant finding across these programs concerns the role of belief and expectation in psi performance. The **"sheep-goat effect"**, named by Gertrude Schmeidler in 1942, refers to the consistent finding that subjects who believe ESP is possible ("sheep") score significantly above chance, while skeptics ("goats") score significantly below—not at chance, but actively suppressed.

A 1992 meta-analysis by Lawrence covering **73 experiments by 37 researchers** confirmed: "subjects who believe psi is real average higher results than those who do not believe in it." The Parapsychological Association interprets this as evidence that "one's attitudes toward psi affects the likelihood that such phenomena will occur in the first place."

**Experimenter effects** compound this finding. In the famous Schlitz-Wiseman collaboration, psi proponent Marilyn Schlitz and skeptic Richard Wiseman used **identical protocols**—Schlitz obtained significant results while Wiseman found none. Stanford's PMIR (psi-mediated instrumental response) model proposes that experimenters with strong desire for positive outcomes may unconsciously use their own psi to achieve results.

The Gateway Process acknowledged this implicitly: McDonnell noted that only approximately **5-10% of participants** experienced significant out-of-body experiences or remote viewing, and that confidence and expectation significantly affected results. Standard operating procedure kept results secret from viewers "so that failures would not damage the viewer's confidence and skill."

This creates a profound methodological—and perhaps ontological—puzzle: if the capacity for psi requires belief in its possibility, and belief actively suppresses or enables the phenomenon, then the instrumental framework (treating consciousness as neutral technology) may fundamentally misunderstand its object of study. The toroidal model suggests consciousness interacts with universal information through "attunement"—but attunement may require recognition of what one is attuning to.

## Navy toroidal field research and advanced propulsion

Contemporary government interest in toroidal geometry continues through advanced aerospace research. **Dr. Salvatore Pais**, aerospace engineer at Naval Air Station Patuxent River, filed several patents between 2016-2019 featuring toroidal configurations:

- **Plasma Compression Fusion Device** (2018/2019): Uses toroidal magnetic coils for plasma confinement
- **High Frequency Gravitational Wave Generator**: Employs toroidal magnetic coil arrangements
- **Craft Using an Inertial Mass Reduction Device**: Features resonant cavity electromagnetic systems

Naval Aviation Enterprise Chief Technology Officer James Sheehy labeled Pais's patents "operable" and attested to their national security importance, specifically citing Chinese advances. However, **testing results proved negative**: after spending $508,000 over three years on the High Energy Electromagnetic Field Generator, the Navy concluded the "Pais Effect" could not be proven—"what appears to be a scientific dead-end."

**Eric Davis's** career bridges these domains. His doctoral dissertation at University of Arizona (1991) studied "Diffusion in the Io Plasma Torus at Jupiter"—conventional plasma physics research on the Voyager missions. He subsequently authored the controversial **"Teleportation Physics Study"** (2004) for the Air Force, claiming psychic teleportation is "quite real and can be controlled." For the DIA's AAWSAP program, Davis co-authored a Defense Intelligence Reference Document on "Warp Drive, Dark Energy, and the Manipulation of Extra Dimensions" which includes **"Figure 6: A Toroidal Higher Dimension"** showing toroidal geometry in spacetime manipulation concepts.

The AAWSAP program (2008-2012, **$22 million** through Senator Harry Reid) explicitly included **"Project Consciousness"** exploring "the connection between UAP/UFO and paranormal phenomena and the human brain." Research documented "convergences between UAP effects on people and parameters associated with the afterlife and the survival of human consciousness after death."

## Weaponization and the instrumental imperative

The classification rationale for these programs reveals the purely instrumental orientation. A 1972 DIA report expressed fears that Soviet psychotronics "sooner or later, might enable them to":

- "Know the contents of top secret US documents"
- "Mould the thoughts of key US military and civilian leaders at a distance"
- "Cause the instant death of any US official at a distance"
- "Disable, at a distance, US military equipment of all types"

The **First Earth Battalion** concept (explored under Major General Albert Stubblebine III, Commander of Army Intelligence and Security Command 1981-1984) envisioned "warrior monks" with psychic abilities, including programs to burst animal hearts at a distance. Stubblebine himself reportedly attempted to walk through walls and required battalion commanders to learn spoon-bending.

Programs remained classified primarily due to:
1. **Soviet threat response**: Soviets allegedly spent 60 million rubles annually on psychotronics by 1970, rising to 300+ million by 1975
2. **Intelligence advantage**: Any breakthrough "would have been too valuable and too dangerous for public release"
3. **Embarrassment**: The Air War College acknowledged that "Western researchers' credibility is jeopardized because of the spiritual undertones associated with psychics"

The instrumental orientation appears most starkly in the research goals: consciousness was treated as potential military capability to be developed, deployed, and defended against—with no consideration of what such capacities might imply about the nature of consciousness, reality, or human moral responsibility.

## Conclusion: The torus as ungrounded technology

Across five decades of government consciousness research, the toroidal form appears as a persistent geometric motif—from Bentov's cosmic egg model through the Gateway Process holographic universe, from plasma physics research to advanced propulsion patents. The framework consistently treats consciousness as a vibrational pattern interacting with toroidal information structures, potentially accessible through frequency entrainment and trained attention.

What remains absent throughout is any engagement with what these capacities would mean if real. If consciousness genuinely interfaces with a universal information structure transcending space-time—if, as the Gateway Process document claims, "the Torus reflects the development of the universe in the past, present, and future"—this carries profound implications for human identity, responsibility, and relationship to reality that the instrumental framework systematically ignores.

The belief/expectation findings suggest this may be more than oversight. If psi capacities require recognition of what one is engaging with, the purely instrumental approach—treating consciousness as neutral technology for military advantage—may constitute a fundamental category error. The toroidal model describes consciousness "attuning" to universal holographic information, but attunement implies relationship, not manipulation.

The government research empirically engaged with phenomena suggesting consciousness interfaces with information structures far larger than individual brain function. It developed methods (Hemi-Sync, CRV protocols) that reportedly produce consistent effects in some practitioners. It documented belief effects suggesting these capacities are bound to recognition and expectation rather than neutral operation. Yet it never asked what such findings might require of those who develop them—treating the torus as instrument while leaving its implications as archetype unexplored.
