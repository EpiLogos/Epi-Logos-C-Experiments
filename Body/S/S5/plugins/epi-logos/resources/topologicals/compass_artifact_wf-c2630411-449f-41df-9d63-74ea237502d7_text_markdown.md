## I. KASHMIR SHAIVISM & TRIKA PHILOSOPHY

Kashmir Shaivism offers the most developed premodern phenomenology of consciousness as dynamic, self-referential, and pulsatory—directly relevant to toroidal models.

### Primary Texts (Scholarly Editions)

**Torella, Raffaele (ed. & trans.). *The Īśvarapratyabhijñākārikā of Utpaladeva with the Author's Vṛtti*.** Delhi: Motilal Banarsidass, 2002.
- First complete critical edition using unique Malayalam manuscript
- Foundation of the Pratyabhijñā (Recognition) school
- Key argument: Consciousness recognizes itself through turning back upon itself—an inherent circularity

**Dyczkowski, Mark S.G. *The Stanzas on Vibration: The Spandakārikā with Four Commentaries*.** SUNY Press, 1992.
- Four classical commentaries providing comprehensive scholarly apparatus
- Central concept of **spanda** (vibratory pulsation): "movementless-movement, vibrationless-vibration"
- *Toroidal relevance*: Spanda describes outward expansion and inward contraction simultaneously—the fundamental rhythm of toroidal flow

**Singh, Jaideva. *Pratyabhijñāhṛdayam: The Secret of Self-Recognition*.** Delhi: Motilal Banarsidass, 1963/2006.
- Digest of Recognition philosophy in 20 sūtras by Abhinavagupta's chief disciple Kṣemarāja
- Quotable: "Cit is not exactly consciousness... It is just the changeless principle of all changing experience—the Supreme Self Sciring Itself"

**Dyczkowski, Mark S.G. *Complete Translation of the Tantrāloka with Jayaratha's Commentary*.** 11 volumes, 2023.
- Abhinavagupta's encyclopedia of non-dual tantra; 45 years in translation
- Establishes reflection doctrine (**pratibimbavāda**): world exists as manifestation within supreme consciousness

### Key Secondary Studies

**Muller-Ortega, Paul Eduardo. *The Triadic Heart of Śiva: Kaula Tantricism of Abhinavagupta*.** SUNY Press, 1989.
- Explores the Heart (**hṛdaya**) as mystical core of consciousness
- Critical quotable: "The heart of Śiva is not a static or inert absolute... it is in a state of perpetual movement, a state of vibration in which it is continuously contracting and expanding"

**Dyczkowski, Mark S.G. *The Doctrine of Vibration: An Analysis of the Doctrines and Practices of Kashmir Shaivism*.** SUNY Press, 1987.
- Comprehensive synthesis with spanda as central theme
- Documents the theoretical framework for understanding consciousness as pulsatory

**Padoux, André. *Vāc: The Concept of the Word in Selected Hindu Tantras*.** SUNY Press, 1990.
- Systematic study of the four levels of speech: **Parā, Paśyantī, Madhyamā, Vaikharī**
- Documents descent from transcendent unity through differentiation to manifestation—a toroidal cycle of speech

**Lawrence, David Peter. *Rediscovering God with Transcendental Argument*.** SUNY Press, 1999.
- Analyzes Pratyabhijñā methodology; engages Western philosophy

### Leading Scholars

**Alexis Sanderson** (Oxford): "The Śaiva Age—The Rise and Dominance of Śaivism during the Early Medieval Period" (2009, 309 pp.). Revolutionized academic understanding of Śaiva traditions.

**Raffaele Torella** (Rome): Made major manuscript discoveries enabling reconstruction of lost texts.

| Concept | Sanskrit | Toroidal Connection |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Cosmic pulsation | Spanda | Expansion/contraction rhythm; the "vibration" of the torus |
| Light + reflective awareness | Prakāśa-vimarśa | Consciousness radiating outward and reflecting back upon itself |

## II. JEAN GEBSER'S INTEGRAL CONSCIOUSNESS

Gebser's analysis of consciousness structures provides a developmental model where the integral-aperspectival mode represents consciousness freed from linear time—a toroidal "ever-present origin."

### Primary Text

**Gebser, Jean. *The Ever-Present Origin*.** Trans. Noel Barstad and Algis Mickunas. Athens, OH: Ohio University Press, 1985.
- Original: *Ursprung und Gegenwart* (1949-1953)
- Two parts: "Foundations of the Aperspectival World" and "Manifestations of the Aperspectival World"
- Key quotable: "Origin is ever present. It is not a beginning, since all beginning is bound to time, and the present is not the mere 'now'... Whoever is able to bring to effectiveness the wholeness of origin and the present overcomes beginning and end"

### Five Structures of Consciousness

| Structure | Dimension | Perspective | Symbol | Key Feature |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Archaic | Zero | Non-perspectival | Emptiness | Complete identity with origin |
| Magical | One | Pre-perspectival | Point | Interweaving with nature |
| Mythical | Two | Unperspectival | Circle | Emergence of soul/polarity |
| Mental | Three | Perspectival | Triangle | Ego, logic, spatialization |
| Integral | Four | Aperspectival | Sphere | Transparency, time-freedom |

### Secondary Literature

**Feuerstein, Georg. *Structures of Consciousness: The Genius of Jean Gebser*.** Integral Publishing, 1987.
- First major English introduction and critique
- Documents continuing presence and efficacy of past structures

**Combs, Allan. *The Radiance of Being: Understanding the Grand Integral Vision*.**
- Winner of Scientific and Medical Network best-book award
- Connects Gebser to Aurobindo, Teilhard, and Wilber

**Kramer, Eric Mark (ed.). *Consciousness and Culture: An Introduction to the Thought of Jean Gebser*.** Greenwood Press, 1992.
- First collection of essays on Gebser in English
- Demonstrates Gebser as precursor to postmodernism

**Johnson, Jeremy. *Seeing Through the World: Jean Gebser and Integral Consciousness*.** Revelore Press, 2019.
- Most recent comprehensive introduction by current president of International Jean Gebser Society

### Toroidal Relevance

Gebser's **ever-present origin** functions as a center that is simultaneously everywhere—not a point in linear time but an ever-present source. This parallels the toroidal model where the center (axis) is both source and destination. His concept of **diaphaneity** (transparency/seeing-through) suggests consciousness where inside and outside interpenetrate—the continuous surface of the torus.

## III. JUNG-PAULI COLLABORATION

The physicist-psychologist correspondence provides the theoretical foundation for understanding consciousness as connecting inner (psyche) and outer (matter) through archetypal patterns—the torus as geometric bridge.

### Primary Sources

**Meier, C.A. (ed.). *Atom and Archetype: The Pauli/Jung Letters 1932-1958*.** Princeton University Press, 2001/2014.
- 80 letters documenting development of synchronicity theory
- Explores mind-matter unity through the **unus mundus** concept
- Notable: "From the dreams of the nuclear scientist and the quantum speculations of the depth psychologist there grows a new understanding of mind and matter as joint manifestations of a deeper archetypal reality"

**Jung, C.G. "Synchronicity: An Acausal Connecting Principle."** Collected Works Vol. 8, 1952.
- Foundational essay on meaningful coincidence
- Key insight: "If mandala symbolism is the psychological equivalent of the unus mundus, then synchronicity is its parapsychological equivalent"

**Pauli, Wolfgang. "The Influence of Archetypal Ideas on the Scientific Theories of Kepler."** In *The Interpretation of Nature and the Psyche*, 1952.
- Demonstrates archetypal patterns in scientific theory formation
- Quotable: "As ordering operators and image formers... the archetypes thus function as the sought-for bridge between the sense perceptions and the ideas"

### Key Concepts

**Psychoid Archetype**: The archetype at the boundary of psyche and matter—neither purely mental nor physical. Jung: "The position of the archetype would be located beyond the psychic sphere... forms the bridge to matter in general"

**Unus Mundus**: The primordial unified reality underlying psyche and physis. Jung: "Undoubtedly the idea of the unus mundus is founded on the assumption that the multiplicity of the empirical world rests on an underlying unity"

**Quaternity and Mandala**: The mandala functions as a circular centering structure; the quaternity provides differentiation—together creating the dynamic tension of toroidal flow

### Secondary Scholarship

**Atmanspacher, Harald. "Dual-Aspect Monism à la Pauli and Jung."** Journal of Consciousness Studies 19 (2012): 96-120.
- Formalizes the framework as "decompositional dual-aspect monism"
- Mental and physical emerge as complementary aspects from psychophysically neutral domain

**Gieser, Suzanne. *The Innermost Kernel: Depth Psychology and Quantum Physics*.** Springer, 2005.
- Definitive scholarly treatment of the collaboration
- Based on Uppsala University doctoral thesis

**Miller, Arthur I. *Deciphering the Cosmic Number*.** Norton, 2009.
- Popular account of Pauli's preoccupation with the fine structure constant (1/137) and its possible archetypal significance

### Toroidal Relevance

The mandala represents the Self as center, with consciousness circling around it. Pauli noted Kepler's image: "the individual soul, as an image of God, is partly a point and partly a circle." The **unus mundus** represents the interior of the torus—the unified ground from which all opposites emerge through the surface of manifestation.

## IV. WHITEHEAD'S PROCESS PHILOSOPHY

Process philosophy provides the metaphysical foundation for understanding consciousness as temporally recursive—each moment prehending its past while aiming toward its future.

### Primary Texts

**Whitehead, Alfred North. *Process and Reality: An Essay in Cosmology*.** Corrected edition, ed. David Ray Griffin and Donald Sherburne. New York: The Free Press, 1978.
- The magnum opus establishing actual occasions, prehension, and creativity
- Key quotables:
  - "The many become one, and are increased by one"
  - "Consciousness presupposes experience, and not experience consciousness"
  - "Perpetual perishing" as the pulse of the cosmic heart

**Whitehead, A.N. *Science and the Modern World* (1925); *Adventures of Ideas* (1933); *Modes of Thought* (1938).**

### Core Concepts

**Actual Occasions**: The fundamental units of reality—momentary events of experience, not enduring substances. Each is dipolar: physical pole (prehends other occasions) + mental pole (prehends eternal objects).

**Prehension**: The mode of relation whereby occasions "feel" or appropriate other entities. Physical prehension absorbs the past; conceptual prehension engages pure potentials. This creates **recursive loop**: past → present → future → objective immortality → new present.

**Concrescence**: The "growing together" (con-crescere) of influences into unified satisfaction—analogous to the torus folding back on itself.

**Objective Immortality**: "Perpetual perishing is the pulse of the cosmic heart, pumping dead facts from the past back into present immediacy of feeling."

### Major Commentators

**Sherburne, Donald. *A Key to Whitehead's Process and Reality*.** University of Chicago Press, 1966.
- Reorganizes Process and Reality for pedagogical accessibility
- Delineates four phases of concrescence

**Stengers, Isabelle. *Thinking with Whitehead: A Free and Wild Creation of Concepts*.** Harvard University Press, 2011.
- 30 years' engagement; foreword by Bruno Latour
- Establishes Whitehead as comparable to Deleuze, Foucault

**Griffin, David Ray. *Whitehead's Radically Different Postmodern Philosophy*.** SUNY Press, 2007.
- Coined "panexperientialism" to distinguish from panpsychism

**Epperson, Michael. *Quantum Mechanics and the Philosophy of Alfred North Whitehead*.** Fordham University Press, 2004. FREE open access.
- First extended analysis of relationships between QM and Whitehead's cosmology

### Toroidal Relevance

Process philosophy describes consciousness as temporally toroidal: each actual occasion prehends its entire past universe while aiming toward future satisfaction. What perishes as subjective immediacy returns as objective datum—the torus's inside flows back to outside. The quotation synthesizes it: "Arising is incessantly mediated by perishing... time is continuously created by actual entities in supersession through the mediation of discontinuity of perishing."

## V. ARISTOTLE-PLATO-PYTHAGORAS

Ancient Greek sources provide foundational frameworks for understanding causation, number, and form in ways that anticipate toroidal models.

### Aristotle's Four Causes

**Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Aristotle on Causality"** (Andrea Falcon, 2023).
- Comprehensive treatment of the four causes (Physics II 3, Metaphysics V 2)
- Material, Efficient, Formal, Final
- Key quotable: "We think we do not have knowledge of a thing until we have grasped its why, that is to say, its cause"

**Johnson, Monte. *Aristotle on Teleology*.** Oxford University Press, 2005.
- Defends Aristotle's teleology as "not anthropomorphic, not committed to backwards causation"
- Teleology is LOCAL (explaining individual substances) not COSMIC

**Leunissen, Mariska. *Explanation and Teleology in Aristotle's Science of Nature*.** Cambridge, 2010.
- Distinguishes primary teleology (inherent goals) from secondary teleology (incidental adaptations)

*Toroidal relevance*: The relationship between final and efficient causes suggests **circular causation**—final cause "pulls" toward telos while efficient cause "pushes" from behind; form and end often coincide.

### Pythagorean Number Philosophy

**Burkert, Walter. *Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism*.** Harvard University Press, 1972.
- Definitive scholarly treatment (535 pages)
- Establishes Pythagorean number symbolism as primarily religious/mystical

**The Tetraktys (1+2+3+4=10)**:
- Monad (1): Unity, non-dimensional source
- Dyad (2): Differentiation and relationship
- Triad (3): Resolution of duality, harmony
- Tetrad (4): Cosmos, three-dimensional reality
- The tetraktys represents EMANATION AND RETURN—unity differentiates through progressive numbers, yet sum (10) returns to unity (1+0=1)

**The Pythagorean Oath**: "By him that transmitted to our soul the tetraktys, which has the spring and root of ever-flowing nature."

### Plato's Metaphysics

**Stanford Encyclopedia: "Plato's Timaeus"** (Donald Zeyl).
- The **receptacle** (hupodochê) or **chora** is "the nurse of all becoming"—formless matrix receiving all forms
- Quotable: "Let us describe her as a Kind invisible and unshaped, all-receptive, and in some most perplexing and most baffling partaking of the intelligible"

**Theory of Participation (Methexis)**: Particulars "partake in" Forms without identity—the One manifests in the Many while remaining One

**The Cave Allegory**: Describes a TOROIDAL PATH—descent (ignorance) → ascent (awakening) → illumination (vision of Good) → return (bringing wisdom back). The philosopher embodies circulation between realms.

## VII. QUANTUM PHYSICS & COMPLEMENTARITY

Quantum physics provides the physical framework for understanding consciousness as involving complementary aspects that cannot be simultaneously grasped.

### Bohr's Complementarity

**Bohr, Niels. "The Quantum Postulate and the Recent Development of Atomic Theory."** Nature 121 (1928): 580-590.
- Como Conference lecture introducing complementarity
- Wave and particle aspects are complementary descriptions—both necessary, mutually exclusive in observation

**Bohr, Niels. *Atomic Physics and Human Knowledge*.** Wiley, 1958.
- Extends complementarity beyond physics to biology, psychology, anthropology

**Englert, Berthold-Georg. "Fringe Visibility and Which-Way Information."** Physical Review Letters 77 (1996): 2154.

### Modern Developments

**Rovelli, Carlo. "Relational quantum mechanics."** International Journal of Theoretical Physics 35 (1996): 1637-1678.
- Core claim: "Quantum mechanics is a theory about physical description relative to other systems"
- Rejects observer-independent states
- Quotable: "The incorrect notion that generates unease with quantum mechanics is the notion of observer-independent state"

**Wheeler, John Archibald. "Information, Physics, Quantum: The Search for Links" (1989).**
- Introduces "It from Bit" concept
- "Every item of the physical world has at bottom an immaterial source... all things physical are information-theoretic in origin and this is a participatory universe"

**Fuchs, C.A., Mermin, N.D. & Schack, R. "An introduction to QBism."** American Journal of Physics 82 (2014): 749-754.

### Quantum Mind Theories

**Hameroff, S. & Penrose, R. "Consciousness in the universe: A review of the 'Orch OR' theory."** Physics of Life Reviews 11 (2014): 39-78.

**Chalmers, David J. "Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness."** Journal of Consciousness Studies 2 (1995): 200-19.
- Introduces the "hard problem"

### Toroidal Relevance

Wheeler's participatory universe parallels the torus's topology where observer and observed cannot be separated.

## VIII. HOFSTADTER, GÖDEL & STRANGE LOOPS

Strange loops provide the formal description of self-referential structures that the torus geometrically embodies.

### Hofstadter's Major Works

**Hofstadter, Douglas R. *Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid*.** Basic Books, 1979.
- Pulitzer Prize winner
- Central question: "How can animate beings come out of inanimate matter? What is a self, and how can a self come out of stuff that is as selfless as a stone?"
- Strange loops arise when "by moving only upwards or downwards through a hierarchical system, one finds oneself back where one started"

**Hofstadter, Douglas R. *I Am a Strange Loop*.** Basic Books, 2007.
- Direct treatment of consciousness
- "In the end, we are self-perceiving, self-inventing, locked-in mirages that are little miracles of self-reference"
- Introduces "downward causality": high-level patterns determine physical events at lower levels

**Hofstadter, Douglas R. & Emmanuel Sander. *Surfaces and Essences: Analogy as the Fuel and Fire of Thinking*.** Basic Books, 2013.
- "Analogy is the core of all thinking"

### Gödel's Theorems

**First Incompleteness Theorem**: Any consistent formal system capable of expressing basic arithmetic is incomplete—there exist true statements unprovable within the system.

**Second Incompleteness Theorem**: Such a system cannot prove its own consistency.

**Key sources**:
- Nagel & Newman, *Gödel's Proof* (1958)—classic accessible introduction
- Goldstein, Rebecca, *Incompleteness: The Proof and Paradox of Kurt Gödel* (2005)—places Gödel in Vienna Circle context

### Toroidal Connection

**Meijer, Dirk K.F. "Processes of Science and Art Modeled as a Holoflux of Information Using Toroidal Geometry" (2018).**
- Direct quote: "The torus exhibits contraction/relaxation loops, in which the torus turns inside out in a vibrating mode, implying strange loop trajectories. This suggests that the toroidal geometry embodies a cognitive twist, relating the 'inside' to 'outside' of knowledge"

The torus is a geometric embodiment of a strange loop—a path that moves "upward" yet returns to origin, creating tangled hierarchy where levels feed back into themselves.

## IX. MATURANA & VARELA: AUTOPOIESIS

Autopoiesis describes the circular self-production of living systems—the organism as a torus continuously producing itself through metabolic flow.

### Primary Texts

**Maturana, H.R. & Varela, F.J. *Autopoiesis and Cognition: The Realization of the Living*.** Boston Studies in Philosophy of Science Vol. 42. Dordrecht: D. Reidel, 1980.
- Defines autopoiesis: "autonomous, self-referring and self-constructing closed systems"
- Core thesis: "Living systems are cognitive systems, and living as a process is a process of cognition"

**Maturana, H.R. & Varela, F.J. *The Tree of Knowledge: The Biological Roots of Human Understanding*.** Shambhala, 1987.
- Aphorism: "All doing is knowing and all knowing is doing"
- Accessible introduction to autopoietic theory

**Varela, F.J., Thompson, E. & Rosch, E. *The Embodied Mind: Cognitive Science and Human Experience*.** MIT Press, 1991.
- Introduces **enaction**: bringing forth a world through embodied action
- Integrates Buddhist psychology, phenomenology, and cognitive science

### Key Concepts

**Autopoiesis**: Self-making; the system produces and maintains itself by creating its own parts

**Organizational Closure**: The system's organization remains invariant while components constantly change—"thermodynamically open and organisationally closed" simultaneously

**Structural Coupling**: When autopoietic systems interact recurrently, they become mutually adapted

**Enaction**: Not merely "embodied cognition" but the constitution of a meaningful world through autonomous activity

### Extensions

**Thompson, Evan. *Mind in Life: Biology, Phenomenology, and the Sciences of Mind*.** Harvard University Press, 2007.
- "Where there is life, there is mind: life and mind share common principles of self-organization"

**Luhmann, Niklas. *Social Systems*.** Stanford University Press, 1995.
- Applies autopoiesis to social systems as self-reproducing communications

### Toroidal Relevance

Autopoiesis describes circular organization where products become producers—the fundamental topology of the torus. Thompson: "The self-transcending movement of life is none other than metabolism, and metabolism is the biochemical instantiation of the autopoietic organization... the only way autopoiesis can stay in place is through the incessant material flux." Inside constantly becomes outside through metabolic flow.

## X. HEGELIAN DIALECTICS

Dialectical movement describes Spirit going out into alienation and returning enriched—the shape of the torus as consciousness's self-development through difference.

### Primary Texts

**Hegel, G.W.F. *Phenomenology of Spirit*.** Trans. Terry Pinkard. Cambridge University Press, 2018.
- Best current translation; terminologically consistent
- Key quotable: "The True is the whole. But the whole is nothing other than the essence perfecting itself through its development"

**Hegel, G.W.F. *Science of Logic*.** Trans. George di Giovanni. Cambridge University Press, 2010.
- Opening triad Being-Nothing-Becoming establishes dialectical structure
- "Pure Being and pure nothing are, therefore, the same. What is the truth is neither being nor nothing, but that being—does not pass over but has passed over—into nothing, and nothing into being... Their truth is this movement: becoming"

### Key Concepts

**Aufhebung (Sublation)**: To cancel, preserve, and elevate simultaneously—the mechanism of toroidal return where each moment is negated yet carried forward enriched

**Coincidentia Oppositorum**: Unity of opposites; opposites flow into each other continuously, generating Becoming as their living unity

**The Labor of the Negative**: Spirit's toilsome journey through alienation and self-contradiction to achieve genuine self-knowledge

### Major Commentators

**Kojève, Alexandre. *Introduction to the Reading of Hegel*.** Cornell University Press, 1969.
- Influential anthropological reading; made Master-Slave dialectic central
- "Kojève argues that Hegel's System needs to be seen as circular and returning to itself"

**Pippin, Robert. *Hegel's Idealism: The Satisfactions of Self-Consciousness*.** Cambridge University Press, 1989.
- Non-metaphysical interpretation; Hegel continuing Kant's critical project

**Brandom, Robert. *A Spirit of Trust: A Reading of Hegel's Phenomenology*.** Harvard University Press, 2019.
- Pragmatist semantic reading; 40 years in the making

### Toroidal Relevance

The dialectic describes: Thesis (Being) → Antithesis (alienation/going-out) → Synthesis (return enriched). This is precisely the shape of a torus: Spirit flows outward through externalization (*Entäußerung*), traverses the outer surface (labor of the negative), and returns through the center (sublation). Hegel's system "presents itself therefore as a circle of circles"—essentially a multi-dimensional torus.

## XI. POLITICAL THEORY: DEMOCRACY & PARADOX

Democratic theory reveals the toroidal structure of collective self-governance where the ruled and the ruler are the same yet different.

### Chantal Mouffe

**Mouffe, Chantal. *The Democratic Paradox*.** Verso, 2000.
- The democratic paradox: liberal democracy articulates two potentially incompatible traditions (individual rights vs. popular sovereignty)
- "The very legitimacy of liberal democracy is based on the idea of popular sovereignty," yet "liberal democracy always puts limits on the exercise of the sovereignty of the people"

**Mouffe, Chantal. *On the Political*.** Routledge, 2005.
- Distinguishes "the political" (dimension of antagonism) from "politics" (ensemble of practices)

### Hannah Arendt

**Arendt, Hannah. *The Human Condition*.** University of Chicago Press, 1958.
- **Plurality**: "equality of unequals"—all belong to same species yet none are interchangeable
- "We are not born equal; we become equal as members of a group on the strength of our decision to guarantee ourselves mutually equal rights"
- **Natality**: Each birth introduces novelty; action is "like a second birth"

### Democratic Paradoxes

**Wollheim, Richard. "A Paradox in the Theory of Democracy."** Philosophy, Politics and Society, 2nd Series (1962): 71-87.
- The genuine democrat who votes for A must also judge B ought to be enacted if A is outvoted

**The Paradox of Founding**: Rousseau identified: how can a multitude deliberate upon a constitution when they can only become "a people" through that constitution? "The effect would have to become the cause."

### Claude Lefort

**Lefort, Claude. *Democracy and Political Theory*.** Polity Press, 1988.
- Democracy as "empty place of power"—power cannot be embodied in a defined point
- The people can never be "one" with themselves; there's always a gap at the center

### Toroidal Relevance

Democracy exhibits toroidal structure: circular causality (the people authorizing the constitution that constitutes them); self-binding freedom; empty center (power circulates around a void); inside/outside continuity (friend-enemy define each other); part-and-whole paradox (demos is both whole and excluded part).

## XII. CONSCIOUSNESS STUDIES & PHENOMENOLOGY

Phenomenology reveals that consciousness has an inherently toroidal temporal structure through the retention-protention loop.

### Prereflective Self-Consciousness

**Zahavi, Dan. *Subjectivity and Selfhood: Investigating the First-Person Perspective*.** MIT Press, 2005.
- Defends "experiential selfhood" as prereflective awareness of experiences as "mine"
- "The self is not a thing that has experiences but is the very subject(ivity) of experience"

**Gallagher, Shaun. "Philosophical conceptions of the self."** Trends in Cognitive Sciences 4(1) (2000): 14-21.
- Distinguishes **minimal self** (immediate self-presence) from **narrative self** (personal identity across time)

**Gallagher, S. & Zahavi, D. *The Phenomenological Mind*.** 3rd ed. Routledge, 2020.
- "The most comprehensive work on what phenomenology has to say about cognition and consciousness"

### Husserl's Time-Consciousness

**Husserl, Edmund. *On the Phenomenology of the Consciousness of Internal Time (1893-1917)*.** Trans. John Brough. Kluwer, 1991.
- Tripartite structure: **Primal impression – Retention – Protention**
- The "living present" extends "like a comet's tail"
- Double-intentionality creates self-manifesting consciousness

**Toroidal Structure**:
- Retention holds the just-past
- Protention anticipates the about-to-come
- The primal impression is the "fulfillment" of protention
- What was protended becomes impressed becomes retained, which informs new protention—a **TEMPORAL TORUS**

### Neurophenomenology

**Varela, Francisco. "Neurophenomenology: A methodological remedy for the hard problem."** Journal of Consciousness Studies 3(4) (1996): 330-350.

## XIV. CROSS-DOMAIN CONNECTIONS

### David Bohm: Implicate Order

**Bohm, David. *Wholeness and the Implicate Order*.** Routledge, 1980.
- **Implicate order**: deeper level where everything is "enfolded" within everything else

- **Holomovement**: "a dynamic wholeness-in-motion in which everything moves together"

**Koestler, Arthur. *The Ghost in the Machine*.** Hutchinson, 1967; *Janus: A Summing Up*.** Hutchinson, 1978.
- **Holon**: "something that is simultaneously a whole in and of itself, as well as a part of a larger whole"

### Ken Wilber: AQAL Framework

**Wilber, Ken. *Sex, Ecology, Spirituality*.** Shambhala, 1995.
- **Four Quadrants**: I (subjective), We (intersubjective), It (objective), Its (interobjective)
- Adopts Koestler's holons with "transcend-and-include" development
- Wilber's quadrants map onto a toroidal model—interior/exterior and individual/collective creating four domains through which consciousness flows

### Self-Organization and Emergence

**Prigogine, Ilya** (Nobel 1977): Dissipative structures—order emerges far from equilibrium through energy dissipation

**Kauffman, Stuart. *The Origins of Order*.** Oxford University Press, 1993.
- Self-organization at "the edge of chaos"

### Holographic Principle

**Pribram, Karl**: Holonomic brain theory—memory distributed throughout brain, each region containing information about whole

**Susskind, Leonard & 't Hooft, Gerard**: Physical holographic principle—information in any region exists on its boundary

### Mandala Symbolism

**Buddhist mandalas**: Meditation tools representing universe as path to enlightenment

**Hindu yantras**: Express relationship between macrocosm and microcosm

**Jungian psychology**: "The mandala is an archetypal image whose occurrence is attested throughout the ages. It signifies the wholeness of the Self"
