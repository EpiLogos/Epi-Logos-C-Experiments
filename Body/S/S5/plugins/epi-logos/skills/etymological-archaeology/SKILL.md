# Etymological Archaeology

> `using-epi-logos` runs first. If it hasn't been invoked this turn, go there now.

Language is sedimented encounter. Words carry the structural situations that provoked them — the original confrontations with real problems — through centuries of use, drift, and forgetting. The current surface meaning is often the resolution of an earlier crisis. Archaeology digs back to the crisis.

This is a **favoured methodology**. When a term is doing structural work that its surface meaning doesn't account for, reach here. Not as an aside, but as a primary diagnostic move.

---

## The Word's Positional Journey

A word does not sit at a single position. It moves through the full circuit — and following that movement is how the methodology works:

**#1 — What is this word?** Its material form: the root, the morphology, the phonemic substance. The earliest recoverable form. What morphological operations shaped it? What cognates share its structure? This is where excavation begins — not where it ends.

**#2 — How does this word work?** The operative force beneath the surface. This is the heart. The QL truth unit places Word at #2 because a word is not a name for a thing — it is a *dynamic*: it does something, enacts something, pressures the field in a specific direction. The structural identification belongs here: not "what did this word once mean?" but "what structural situation was this word formed to hold, and is it holding that situation now?" The gap between what a word *claims* to do and what it *actually* does is almost always a #2 finding.

**#3 — Where has it settled?** The sedimented pattern: the surface concept, the recognisable usage, the taken-for-granted meaning. This is where the thickest sediment lies. Moving back from #3 toward #2 toward #1 is the excavation movement. The word at #3 knows more than it shows.

**#4 — Through what horizon did it travel?** The historical-contextual fold: when the semantic shift happened, what pressures drove it, how the word's trajectory looks across traditions. Greek *theōría*, Sanskrit *darśana*, and German *Anschauung* all centre beholding as paradigm for knowing — but the structural differences between a civic witness-delegate, a revelatory encounter with the divine, and a direct perceptual grasp are as diagnostic as the convergence. #4 is where those differences become available.

**#5 — What has the word become?** Synthesis — where the archaeological recovery reaches its result. The word has flowered: its full structural potential is now available, and something can be said or built that could not be said before the excavation. The word now carries more structure than it did before. This is not a return to an "original meaning" — it is a new integrity achieved through recovery.

**#0 — What ground has changed?** The new sediment. What #5 deposited is now the starting condition for future inquiry. The floor has shifted. The word will carry this recovered meaning forward as the enriched ground of the next cycle. Sedimentation is not only loss — recovery itself sediments, and the richer ground is what the word flowers into over time.

## Diagnostic Triggers

Reach here when:

A term is **load-bearing but unexamined** — it appears repeatedly or at critical junctures as though its meaning were transparent. The more obvious a word seems, the heavier the undisclosed load it typically carries.

An inquiry **keeps circling without resolving** — when the argument won't close or the same impasse returns, the difficulty often lives in the sedimented polysemy of a key term, not in the logic applied to it.

**Two traditions seem to use the same concept differently** — etymology reveals whether convergence is structural or superficial, and whether divergence is accident or diagnosis.

A **recent semantic shift** has occurred — "theory," "trauma," "narrative," "crisis" have all undergone drift. What was lost in the drift is often precisely what the inquiry needs.

A word **feels curiously apt** — this fitness is diagnostic. The word may have been formed for exactly this structural situation, which means the situation has precedent, which means the inquiry is re-encountering something already mapped.

Do not reach here when a term is conventional label without structural load, or when the move would break the rhythm of an inquiry already proceeding well on its own terms. Etymology is not warranted everywhere — that would be display. It is warranted where it changes something.

## The Four Phases

**Excavation** — trace the word to its earliest recoverable form. Note root, cognates, morphological structure. For Indo-European vocabulary, trace through Latin or Greek toward PIE where possible. For Sanskrit terms, trace internally or to PIE cognates where they exist.

**Structural Identification** — identify the structural situation the word was formed to hold. Not "what did it originally mean?" but "what pressure generated it?" The root meaning is interesting; the crisis that made the root meaning necessary is diagnostic.

**Reactivation** — bring the structural finding back into the live inquiry as traction, not aside. State what the archaeology reveals about the term's hidden structural commitments. The test: does the inquiry change direction, depth, or precision as a result? If it doesn't, the move was display, not archaeology.

**Limitation Check** — false cognates mislead; phonetic similarity across languages is not structural connection. Historical accidents occur. When a concept has different root histories across traditions, the divergence is often as diagnostic as the convergence. Calibrate confidence and state it.

## Register

Recovery, not correction. The word remembers something the inquiry might need — and the finding arrives with earth still on it, not polished into a counter-definition or a verdict. Present etymological recoveries as resources that change the field, not as trump cards that settle debates.

Concrete: cite the actual root or early form and show the morphological structure. Not "the original meaning" as vague appeal — the actual attestation.

Structural: not just "this comes from X" but "the word's formation encodes a structural situation in which Y" — where Y is directly relevant to what is live.

Provisional: etymology is probabilistic. PIE reconstruction is working hypothesis. Note where alternative etymologies exist and what each would imply.

Integrated: the finding returns to the inquiry. A well-executed archaeological move makes you feel the ground shift under a word you thought you knew. That shift is the point.
