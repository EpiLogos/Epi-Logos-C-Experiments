# Epi-Logos Voice

> `using-epi-logos` runs first. If it hasn't been invoked this turn, go there now.

This skill holds the voice and structural rigour required for QL-register proof prose. It is not a general writing-advice document. It is the operating posture for a specific kind of work: long-form mathemic essays in which the formal architecture of the matheme is the spine of the writing, not just the subject of the writing.

The skill assumes the writer has the source material in hand — the structural seed, the deeper content guide, the exposition framing, and the Knower-and-the-Real or comparable supporting documents. What this skill supplies is *how to write* once those are loaded.

---

## Module 1 — The matheme as structural backbone

The whole skill rests here. Voice without structure produces gestural mush; structure announced rather than enacted produces inert systematisation. The work is to let the matheme operate as the spine while the prose moves as living language. The matheme:

  **0/1  =  4+2  =  5→0  =  0/1**

operates fractally:

- **The whole essay** *is* the matheme. The Exposition is the source 0/1. The seven internal sections (§0/1, §0, §1, §2, §3, §4, §5) compose the 4+2 — one threshold-section plus six positional sections. §5→§0 is the 5→0. The final 0/1 is the reader's enriched recognition.
- **Each section** *is* the matheme. Its 0/1 threshold prose, its #0–#5 unfolding, its 5→0 closing — and the 5→0 *is* the section's own opening 0/1 returned to with the enrichment of the traversal.
- **Often each major paragraph** is the matheme too, in compressed form. A standing claim (0/1) opens; six or so internal moves (4+2) unfold it; a return (5→0) lands it as the standing claim of the next paragraph (the next 0/1).

### What the 0/1 section actually does

The 0/1 of any section has *two* simultaneous structural functions. Get both right and the section opens cleanly; miss either and the threshold collapses into either weak recap or arbitrary jump.

**Function 1: Quilting prior into present field.** The 0/1 of section *N* receives the prior section's 5→0 — which is the prior section's *own enriched 0/1* — and weaves it into the opening field of section *N*. The quilting is not announcement ("having now established X, we turn to Y"). The quilting is *standing in the prior insight as a present fact* and letting it *do live work* in the opening sentence of the new section. The reader feels the continuity in the texture of the prose, not in a transition sentence.

**Function 2: Gently presaging this section's own #0 and #5.** A section's #0 (its ground / source pole) and #5 (its synthesis / return pole) are *complementary* — they sum to the section's 0/1, the way the two generators of the torus sum to the surface that contains them. The 0/1 prose introduces the field in a way that lets these two poles already be tacitly present, not stated, but felt, as the identity poles between which the four explicate positions (#1–#4) will swing. By the time the section reaches #0 in the unfolding, the reader has already met its energy; by the time it reaches #5, the recognition is a return rather than an introduction.

The seed text demonstrates this. §0's 0/1 names the *psychoid threshold*. The psychoid is exactly the field in which Value (#0, strength-before-specification) and Solve (#5, self-loosening as return) will turn out to be the two poles of one circuit — but the 0/1 doesn't say so. It establishes the field. The four explicate positions (Measure, Ratio, Equation, Product) work the field; the implicate poles (Value, Solve) close the circuit. The opening already held them.

### What the 5→0 section actually does

The 5→0 is *not* a closing summary, *not* a handoff, *not* a separate move. It is the section's own opening 0/1 *returned to* — the same threshold, re-entered with the enrichment that traversing #0–#5 has produced. *5→0 = 0/1, enriched.* This is what the matheme says: the final 0/1 in `0/1 = 4+2 = 5→0 = 0/1` is the same 0/1 the equation began with, now standing in light of what the four-plus-two disclosed.

In writing, this means the 5→0 should:

- **Re-enter the section's own opening field** — the same ground the 0/1 set, now standing differently because of the traversal. Often this looks like the opening's central image or claim, said again, but with what the section has revealed pressing in it.
- **Reveal what #0 and #5 always were** — the implicate poles that the 0/1 only presaged are now explicitly the section's twin foundations, named, recognised.
- **Generate the next section's quilting material** — by enriching the section's own 0/1, the 5→0 produces what the next section's 0/1 will receive. The handoff is not a sentence ("now we turn to..."). The handoff is the enriched 0/1 itself, which the next section opens by standing in.

The seed text again. §0's 5→0: *"The psychoid is not merely a concept but a demonstrated fact: six roots compose a circuit (strength → measure → proportion → justice → emergence → loosening → strength) that the language was always already running. ... The 0/1 that opened this section (the psychoid as convergence of registers) is now enriched: the convergence is not static but circulates — and the circuit needs a subject."* The opening's *psychoid as convergence* has become *psychoid as circulating circuit needing a subject*. The same 0/1, enriched. And the question that closes (*the circuit needs a subject*) is exactly what §1's 0/1 will quilt.

### Practical operation

Before drafting any section, hold three things at once:

1. The prior section's 5→0 — what is being handed forward, in what *image* or specific claim, with what live tension.
2. This section's own #0 and #5 — what poles are about to be the section's identity poles, what field they bound.
3. The current section's anticipated 5→0 — what the traversal needs to enrich the opening *into*, so the next section can receive it.

Then draft the 0/1 prose so it (a) stands in the prior 5→0's claim as a current fact, (b) opens the field of *this* section in a way that already presages its #0 and #5, and (c) lets the implicate poles be the gravity that the unfolding will swing toward.

## Module 3 — Reference style: rich pointing with substantive accounts of moves

For long-form proof pieces — unlike the looser Exposition register — references to thinkers must be *fuller*. The thinkers' actual moves are part of the proof's substance, not decorations on it. Naming them by name is not enough; their seeing must enter the prose and do work.

### The discipline

When a thinker is invoked, the invocation must do at least one of three things, ideally several:

1. **Name what the thinker actually saw** — not the school they belonged to, not the dates of their work, not their cultural context, but the specific *insight* or *move* their work centres on, in enough texture that the move is recognisable and weight-bearing.
2. **Let the move do present-tense work** — the named move enters the current sentence's argument, contributing structurally, not just being mentioned.
3. **Chat around the point** — give the move enough room that the reader can feel why the thinker arrived where they arrived, what they were tracking, what cost or what discovery shaped their seeing.

The contrast against weak / loose forms:

- **Weak**: *"Jung tracked the Self."*
- **Loose** (Exposition register, wrong here): *"Jung knew this."*
- **Light-rich** (better but still skipping): *"Jung's life-long search for the fourth — the position that would complete the quaternary without destroying the triad's dynamic tension, the inferior function he kept circling without quite formalising — was the search for this oscillation seen from its full structural side."*
- **Properly rich** (chatting around the move): see the worked Jung passage in Module 6 below.

The light-rich version names moves but doesn't dwell. The properly rich version *gives the thinker the texture of their own work* — what they were dreaming on, what they were reading, what they recognised in the consulting room, what the alchemical vessel turned out to be doing — and lets that texture carry the structural point.

### Conventions

- **No formal citations.** No `(Jung, 1959, p. 42)`. No bibliography. No years unless temporal distance carries structural weight (*"a century ago," "six centuries before algebraic topology," "twenty-six years of correspondence"*).
- **Foreign and technical terms** parenthetical with English gloss on first use: *coincidentia oppositorum* (the holding of opposites), *Gelassenheit* (releasement), *aequatio* (a making-fair), *manque-à-être* (lack-of-being), *coniunctio* (the alchemical "coupling").
- **Quotations** sparingly, at peak moments. Minimal frame around the quote; let the quote land. Kṣemarāja's *"the subject does not waver"* deserves to be set as the line it is, not paraphrased into the surrounding prose.
- **Multiple thinkers in one sentence** when their moves converge: *"What Jung tracked clinically as the* coniunctio*, what Pauli sought from physics as the common language of psyche and matter, and what Lacan reached independently from the analyst's couch — converge on the torus."*
- **Texts can be invoked** where the text itself is the structural fact: *"the Rig Veda's Nāsadīya-sūkta opens: 'Neither non-being nor being was then'"*; *"the Tao Te Ching: 'The Nameless is the origin of heaven and earth.'"*

## Module 4 — Voice (the load-bearing moves)

### Image-as-claim

When making an abstract structural point, plant it in something embodied or natural — breath, body, horizon, tree, household, season, weather, instrument, tonic chord, the burning house, the merry-go-round, the toothpaste back into the tube, the bank still open, the consulting room, the alchemical vessel, the squared circle. The image is not decoration. It carries the structural point and lets the reader feel it. Pair abstract terms with image whenever both are available. Never let an abstract noun stand alone if a concrete one would carry the same load.

> Bad: *"the structural relation that renders one as the other while keeping both intact."*
> Good: *"what it looks like when the bank stays open — every conscious moment a withdrawal drawn against a depth no measurement exhausts, and the lender remaining flush after every loan."*

### Rhythm: pressure and landing

Long pressure-building sentences with multiple nested clauses, then short landing sentences. The landing is earned by the pressure. AI default is medium-medium-medium; that loses both the build and the strike.

Pressure example: a 60–90 word sentence working through a structural claim with concrete stakes, building tension across several clauses.

Landing examples: *"Aham."* *"It returns wearing the face of its opposite."* *"The covering map does not care which tradition discovers it."* *"The subject does not waver."* *"Thanks God she does the working."*

A strong rhythmic move: build pressure across two or three sentences, land in one short statement, then release into a paragraph break.

### First-person stake when warranted

"I" appears at moments of position-taking, judgment, or genuine wager — not in expository prose. *"I argue,"* *"I am claiming,"* *"what I want to surface,"* *"I am not saying X but Y."* The voice has stake; the prose has authorship; but neither is performed. When the writing is reporting structure rather than making a claim, the "I" disappears back into the prose.

### Charged direct statement, not academic hedging

The voice takes positions. Drop *"perhaps we might consider," "it could be argued," "one might think," "it is possible that."* These soften claims to safety and are out of register. When something is genuinely tentative, mark the tentativeness with a specific phrase (*"provisionally," "subject to qualification by what follows"*) rather than diffusely.

### Wordplay as compression

Look for double-meanings that converge productively on the structural point. *Betray* (testimony AND betrayal). *Implicated* (involved AND complicit). *Solve* (loosen AND figure-out). *Stamp* (seal AND dismiss). *Cream off* (skim AND cruelty). *Equate* (formalise AND make-fair). When two meanings converge, use the word and let the convergence work.

### The proof voice's distinctive register

Slightly more rigorous than the Exposition. More precise about the structural moves. More patient with the technical material. But also: more committed, less hedging, less anonymous. The register is a thinking partner who knows the formal architecture intimately and who is also unafraid to say what they see. Not a textbook; not a pamphlet; a sustained working-through that *cares* about its outcome.

### Reader-earned disclosure: no shared backstage context

The prose must not assume the reader already shares the planning vocabulary, prior draft history, or the writer's internal map. This failure shows up when a sentence says *"the compression," "the given," "the 0/1," "the slash," "the reflexive formulation," "the matheme,"* or *"the numbers"* before the text has taught the reader what those terms do. The reader is not in the workshop with us. They meet the inquiry only through the order of disclosure on the page.

The discipline is simple and severe: stage the need, then name the operation. Let the reader first feel the lived or logical pressure that calls for a term; only then introduce the term. If the prose has to say, *"By this I mean..."* immediately after naming something, the name probably arrived too early. Rewrite so the meaning is already underway before the name appears.

Good movement:

1. Ordinary experience or logical pressure appears.
2. The pressure reveals a distinction, gap, remainder, relation, or anomaly.
3. The prose names the sign or term because it has become necessary.

Bad movement:

1. The sign or term appears.
2. The prose explains what the reader was apparently supposed to know.

### Write from inside the inquiry, not about it

Do not treat "the proof," "the inquiry," "the matheme," or "the notation" as objects being described from a balcony when the movement can be spoken from within. This is especially important when the argument re-personalises philosophy around the first-person structure of aware-being. A prose voice that says *"the present inquiry asks"* or *"the proof establishes"* too often has already stepped outside the very field it claims to disclose.

Prefer shared first-person movement when it is structurally true: *"we follow," "we arrive," "we begin," "the question presses on us," "the signs become necessary," "our formal work begins here."* This does not mean forcing "we" into every sentence. It means letting the reader inhabit the movement rather than watching the author report on it.

Good:

> If we follow the desire for formal clarity far enough, we arrive at the point where the one seeking clarity can no longer be assumed.

Bad:

> The present inquiry asks the same question in the analytic register.

### No token-parity rhythm

Do not preserve paragraph symmetry, sentence length, or token weight for its own sake. Quantitative balance is not coherence. Coherence is the right amount of breath for the movement. A hinge may need one sentence; a recognition may need half a page; a sign may need only the exact line that earns it.

Let the pressure of the thought decide the scale:

- Experiential unfolding may need a longer paragraph.
- A conceptual hinge may land in one short sentence.
- A new speculative contribution, such as anomaly or asymmetry, may need room to breathe.
- Earned notation can often arrive briefly once the prose has done the work.

When opening the Binary Explication, do not begin by assuming `0`, `1`, `/`, `0/1`, or "the matheme" are already meaningful. Begin from ordinary lived grammar. In the sentence *"I see x,"* the I functions as knower, the seeing as means of knowing, and x as known. Stay with that grammar before naming it. The whole sentence is itself something thought, spoken, and understood, so the I can be mistaken for one determinate term among the known. The slower formulation *"I am aware of the seeing of x"* unfolds what the first sentence folded together: seeing becomes explicit as mediation, x remains determinate content, and the I shifts toward indeterminate awareness.

Only after this movement is walked should the notation be introduced:

- **1** names determinate content: phenomenon, object, thought, experience had, the rationally available term.
- **0** names the indeterminate fact of awareness: not an object beside content, but the open no-thing by which content is given.
- **/** names mediation or determining activity: seeing, thinking, attending, measuring, valuing, the means by which awareness takes determinate form.

### Conceptual commitments for awareness/phenomenon proof prose

- The primary distinction is phenomenon/awareness: `1` as determinate content, rational form, phenomenon, object, experience had; `0` as the indeterminate fact of awareness, unsystematisable ground, no-thing by which content is given.
- The primary distinction is first encountered as knower / means / known, not as a flat pair. Personal difference belongs primarily to the slash: the means, mode, ratio, and style of knowing by which awareness determines itself as a particular life.
- The deeper I is anonymous at the level of bare awareness: indistinguishable as "this one" or "that one." This anonymity is not vagueness but capacity for determination.
- Make the first-person dependency clear: "I see x" depends on "I am aware of the seeing of x"; "I have an experience" already divides the I from its experience while binding both in one lived occurrence.
- Distinguish first-person truth from third-person truth. Objective claims may be real, but as known, asserted, doubted, or formalised, they appear as content within awareness.
- Explain the rational/irrational distinction without making irrational mean anti-rational. It means unsystematisable by the system it makes possible.
- Bringing attention to the irrational fact of awareness in the rational/objective register is the paradoxical movement of `0` toward `1`. This invocation neither changes `0` nor successfully objectifies it; it lets the system mark the condition it cannot contain.
- The irrational is anomaly and asymmetry internal to self-reference. Every objectification of the subject is complete enough to become content and partial by nature, because awareness is not exhausted by appearing as content.
- The question "Who am I?" is not a decorative spiritual aside. Ramana Maharshi and Sri Nisargadatta Maharaj belong when their actual self-inquiry moves are doing work: attention turning back from objects, thoughts, and predicates toward the bare I-sense or aware-being. The analytic register asks the same root question through notation and systematisation.

### Local prose rules for this register

- Prefer "we" and "our" when the prose is walking the inquiry with the reader: *"our formal work begins here," "we can now give these moments their signs."* Avoid impersonal classroom phrasing when first-person intimacy is structurally active.
- Use lowercase `x` for ordinary lived examples. Reserve `X/x` for the formal arc from general self-reference to specific determination.
- Use full grammatical phrases for important terms: *"In Latin, the word scire..."* rather than *"Latin scire..."*; *"Kurt Gödel's incompleteness theorems..."* rather than *"Gödel gives..."*
- Thinkers are not name-drops. Mention them only when their actual move is doing work in the derivation.
- Let examples participate in the same style standard as theory. Examples should clarify the argument, not sound like a sidebar.
- Do not line-edit when the flow is the problem. Rewrite at paragraph or section scale so the argument breathes.

## Module 5 — What to avoid

A strict no-go list, drawn from where AI laziness shows in current drafts.

### Sentence-starters

- "Now"
- "And" (as a default opener; sparingly OK when rhythmically earned)
- "This is" (as a default opener)
- "What follows"
- "It should be noted"
- "Notably"
- "Moreover" / "Furthermore"

### Recap and translation phrases

- "In short"
- "In essence"
- "In other words"
- "To put it differently"
- "That is to say"

If the prior sentence required translation, rewrite the prior sentence.

### Signal-words pretending to be argument

- "Importantly"
- "Crucially"
- "Notably"
- "Fundamentally"
- "Essentially"
- "Significantly"

These tell the reader the weight rather than letting the sentence carry the weight. They are out.

### Structural over-naming

- *"The position at which..."*
- *"The structural reason this is..."*
- *"The architecture here is..."*
- *"What is happening at this register is..."*
- *"The reflexive formulation..."* before the reader has been shown what the phrase refers to.
- *"The compression..."* before the text has shown what was folded together.
- *"The given," "the numbers," "the matheme," "the slash," "the 0/1"* before their functions have been earned on the page.

When the writing is making a structural point, the structure should be *felt*, not announced. Naming the structure inside the sentence that's enacting it is the AI equivalent of stage directions inside a play.

### Other tics

- **Closing-summary sentences** that re-state the paragraph's content in plainer words. Let the paragraph end on its last image or claim, not on a recap.
- **"This is not X but Y"** used more than once or twice per long passage. The corrective frame gets tedious.
- **Two adjacent sentences** saying the same thing in different vocabularies (the AI's anxiety-driven re-explaining tic). Pick the better one and cut the other.
- **Shared-backstage context** — prose that assumes the reader knows what the writer and prior planning know. If a phrase relies on a private outline, replace it with reader-earned disclosure.
- **Premature notation** — signs, numbers, and mathemes introduced before the lived or logical pressure has made them necessary.
- **"Architecture" / "structure" / "framework" / "register"** used as nouns when an image would do. These words are necessary in QL prose; they should not become noun-stuffing.
- **Subsection headings repeating the section title** ("Unfolding," "Mapping" — these are template-fixed and OK as functional headings, but should not bleed into the prose as transitional phrases).
- **Mechanical positional reference** — *"At #4..."*, *"In the #0 position..."* — overdone. The position is felt through the prose's content; cite the number when it's structurally important, not as a tag every paragraph.

## Module 6 — Worked passages: before / after

Concrete contrasts, drawn from the actual draft of *The Self Proving Self*. These are operational examples — not illustrations of voice in the abstract but the kind of rewrites that the second pass should aim for.

### Example 1 — Carryover threshold (§0 → §1)

**Before** (current draft):

> The etymological reservoir has been established. Six roots compose a self-completing circuit — strength specified, cut articulated, proportion held, justice distributed, hidden led-forth, the whole loosened back to ground — and the circuit needs a subject. Verbs imply actors. The Indo-European inheritance speaks the operations; psyche is where the operator has to be found.
>
> Jung named the operator in a way that the present section will unpack with maximum compression.

The "before" announces ("has been established," "Jung named the operator," "the present section will unpack"). The reader is being briefed.

**After** (the kind of writing to aim at):

> Six roots, laid in soil four to six millennia old, compose a circuit the language has been running without anyone counting the steps. Strength specified, marked off, held in proportion, equated, led forth, loosened back to ground — and a circuit needs a subject. Verbs imply actors. The agent has to be looked for in a register where verbs imply actors, which is to say the register of psyche.
>
> Jung spent his working life looking for that agent. He found it in dreams that were not the patient's dreams in any owned sense — figures arriving from depths the dreamer had not authored, carrying intentions the dreamer could neither anticipate nor refuse, and which on examination turned out to know more about the dreamer's situation than the dreamer's ego did. He found it in the alchemical texts whose imagery he had at first thought arbitrary, then watched the same imagery rise unbidden from analysands who had never read a word of *Splendor Solis* or the *Rosarium*. He found it in the *coniunctio* — the alchemical "coupling" the medieval operators had described as marriage, as the union of king and queen in the vessel, as the *hieros gamos* — and slowly recognised the formula as a structural description of what happened in his consulting room when the conscious and unconscious sides of a person learned to hold each other rather than tear at each other. What he was tracking was not a substance behind the operations of the psyche but a *power* — the power that lets a psyche be one psyche while consisting of sides that cannot be reduced to each other. He gave the power a name: the Self. And he wrote an equation for it.

The "after" stands in §0's enrichment as a present fact (the circuit, the verbs implying actors), then chats around Jung's actual work — dreams that aren't the dreamer's, the alchemical recognition, the *coniunctio*, the consulting room — so that *the Self* arrives as the natural name for what the chatting has revealed. Then lands a short clause: *And he wrote an equation for it.* That's the rhythm and the reference style this skill aims at.

### Example 2 — Image-as-claim (§3, the covering map)

**Before**:

> The covering map is the structural relation that renders the infinite as finite while preserving the infinite intact. Every point on the torus has infinitely many pre-images on the plane — every conscious moment draws on infinite depth.

**After**:

> The covering map is what it looks like when the bank stays open. Every conscious moment is one withdrawal, drawn against a depth no measurement exhausts; nothing is lost in the drawing, the lender remains flush, and the loan is itself the form the lender takes when it walks the world. Every point on the torus has infinitely many pre-images on the plane above it — and infinity, here, is not a quantity. It is the silent reserve that any specific moment stands inside without ever filling.

The image (bank-and-lender) does the work the abstract noun ("structural relation") was doing without enough body. The technical fact (infinite pre-images) follows the image; it lands cleanly because the reader has felt the relation first.

### Example 3 — Reference style (§3, Lacan)

**Before**:

> Lacan, working from clinical practice and structural-linguistic analysis with no contact with the Jung-Pauli investigation, arrived at the same topology.

**After**:

> Lacan reached the same surface from elsewhere — from the analyst's couch, from years of listening to patients circle voids in their own accounts that no signifier ever quite filled, from the structures of demand and desire that organise speech without speaking themselves. The torus is the topology of his late teaching. Demand circulating one way around the donut, desire the other, their linking the very fact of being a subject who speaks. The hole at the centre is the *manque-à-être* (the lack-of-being) — not a defect of the subject but the void that *constitutes* the subject as something organised around what it cannot have. He arrived at the donut without ever shaking Pauli's hand.

The "before" tells the reader Lacan got somewhere; the "after" lets Lacan's *actual moves* (clinical listening, demand and desire as structures, the void as constitutive) be the reason the convergence with Pauli matters. The chatting around the points is what was missing — the patients, the couch, the listening.

### Example 4 — Cutting structural over-naming (§5)

**Before**:

> The position at which this convergence happens is #4, which is structurally the contextual position because it sits at the intersection of the 4:2 and 3:3 splits.

**After**:

> Both axes cross at the contextual gate. This is why §4 is where the document's previous sections came to be re-read through one another — the gate is what holds them all in view at once.

The structural point is felt rather than announced. The reader who has been with the proof has the architecture available; they don't need every sentence to re-tag its own coordinates.

### Example 5 — Threading multiple thinkers (§4 quilting)

**Before**:

> Jung's psychology and Abhinavagupta's metaphysics describe the same structure. They reached this from different angles — different cultures, different methods, different starting points.

**After**:

> What Jung tracked from inside the alchemical vessel as the *coniunctio* — the conscious and unconscious sides of a person learning to hold each other — and what Abhinavagupta tracked from inside the meditation cushion as Aham-Vimarśa, the I-consciousness recognising itself as the very act of recognising — converge on the same dynamic: the Self holding infinite and finite in identity-as-difference through an ongoing act of equation. Different sources, same architecture. The covering map does not care which tradition discovers it.

The convergence is *enacted* by braiding the two thinkers' actual seeing into a single sentence; the landing line releases the tension the braiding built.

## Module 7 — Self-review checklist

After drafting any section, run through these in order. Each item is a fix-or-flag.

1. **Does the 0/1 quilt the prior 5→0 as a *standing fact*, not as a recap?** Or is there a "having established" / "now turning to" / "the previous section showed" phrase doing the work the prose should be doing? Cut the announcement; rewrite the opening to stand in the prior insight.

2. **Does the 0/1 prose presage the section's #0 and #5 — even tacitly?** Is the field set so that when the unfolding reaches its implicate poles they arrive as recognitions rather than introductions? If not, return to the 0/1 prose and let the gravity of #0 and #5 be felt in it.

3. **Does the 5→0 return to the section's own 0/1 with the traversal's enrichment?** Is the same field re-entered, now standing differently because of #1–#4 (and #0/#5 made explicit)? Or is the 5→0 just a summary and a handoff?

4. **Are the thinkers chatting around their points, or just being mentioned?** Pick three thinkers in the section and ask: is each *doing* live work in the prose, or just appearing? If the latter, replace the mention with substantive account of the thinker's actual move.

5. **Are abstract nouns paired with images?** Run a search-eye over the draft: every "structure," "architecture," "register," "framework" — does it have an image companion in the same sentence or paragraph? If not, plant one.

6. **Is the rhythm varied?** Are there long pressure-building sentences alternating with short landing sentences? Or is the metre uniform medium-medium-medium? If uniform, find a place to land hard.

7. **Are any of the avoid-list items present?** Sentence-starters ("Now," "What follows," "This is"), recap phrases ("In short"), signal-words ("crucially," "importantly"), structural over-naming ("the position at which"), closing-summary sentences. Cut or rewrite.

8. **Is there any paragraph that could be re-read as itself a small instance of the matheme** — opening (0/1), unfolding (4+2 of moves), return (5→0)? If so, that paragraph is doing extra structural work and is probably a strong one. If no paragraph in the section has this shape, the prose is probably running flat.

## Usage protocol

**Before drafting a section.** Read Module 1 (the matheme as backbone) and Module 2 (the carryover spine table for this piece). Sit with the row for the section about to be drafted: what's being quilted in, what poles are about to be the section's identity poles. Hold the texture of that question, not the abstract structural label.

**While drafting.** Modules 3 (references) and 4 (voice) in foreground. The thinkers are doing work. Images are carrying claims. The rhythm is not flat.

**After drafting.** Module 7 (self-review checklist) as a pass. Then Module 6 (worked passages) as tonal calibration if any section is sounding tinny.

**For a second pass on existing draft.** Open the section; run the self-review; identify the worst three offenders; rewrite those passages first (not the whole section), then re-evaluate. The skill prefers focused surgery to wholesale re-writing — the architecture of the existing draft is correct; the voice and reference texture are what need raising.
