# Engage Encounter Axis

> `using-epi-logos` runs first. If it hasn't been invoked this turn, go there now.

Square B is the primary runtime entry surface — the Cause-Experience basin where disclosure meets causation, and lived encounter meets scientific verification. All four lenses in this square are co-present whenever any one of them is doing real work.

## The Four Lenses

**L1 — Causal Structure (Aristotle's four causes)**
What causes are actually operative? Material, formal, efficient, final — which does the situation foreground, which does it repress? The gap between acknowledged and operative causes is almost always diagnostic. Work L1's sub-nodes: material (substrate), formal (structural pattern), efficient (what produces), final (purpose — the cause most often repressed in modern framing).

**L4 — Phenomenological Disclosure (Heidegger)**
How is the issue lived from inside? What is disclosed, what matters, what is at stake? Work L4's sub-nodes: Sein (the question of Being that the situation raises), Geworfenheit (thrownness — what was already given before choice), Dasein (the clearing where the issue shows itself), Zeit (temporality — what horizon of time shapes the encounter), Besorge (caring engagement — what the knower is invested in), Gelassenheit (releasement — where letting-be would serve better than grasping).

**L4' — Scientific Intervention (Kuhn, paradigm structure)**
How is the issue observed, measured, verified? What is the current state, what is the desired state, what counts as evidence? Work L4's prime sub-nodes: paradigm (what framework is structuring observation), normal science (what the routine methodology can handle), anomaly (what it cannot), crisis (when anomalies accumulate past the paradigm's capacity), revolution (what would constitute genuine reframing), and the Thought vault structure that maps to the L4' loop (Questions, Traces, Challenges, Patterns, Discovery, Insight).

**L1' — Phenomenal Immediacy (Jung's psychological functions)**
How is the issue felt, valued, apprehended before conceptual framing? Which psychological functions are active — sensing, intuiting, thinking, feeling? What is the felt quality of the encounter that precedes and orients the more structured lenses? L1' is where the raw phenomenal intake lives — the body's knowledge, the evaluative charge, the sense that something matters before you can say why.

## The Encounter Axis as Double Helix

The lived/observed pairing this square enacts is the local form of the P/P' double cover articulated in §5→§0 of `the-self-proving-self.md`. The Day and Night arcs are not parallel chains — they are one double helix, two simultaneous tracks of one Spanda: T1 (emanative, bimba-direction, source leaning into display) and T2 (reversionary, pratibimba-direction, display leaning back into source). Truth/Play, Mind/Need, Word/Sacrifice, Logos/Decision, Son/Love, Image/Work — these are not Day with Night-as-shadow but the Name-face and the Power-face of one Self-Identity, neither prior. The encounter axis (L4 lived ↔ L4' observed) is where this double helix becomes locally palpable: what is felt from inside (Power face) and what is verified from outside (Name face) are not two reports of the same thing but one vibration measured from two poles. The skill's job is to keep both tracks in view as co-present, not to translate one into the other.

## The Two Diagonals

The square's internal structure is organised by two crossing diagonals:

**L4 ↔ L1 (Disclosure–Causation):** The gap between how a situation presents itself (L4) and what is causally operative beneath that presentation (L1). This is the CMEA diagonal — `apply-cmea` works this crossing specifically. When a field's self-understanding diverges from its actual causal dependencies, this diagonal is where the fracture lives.

**L4' ↔ L1' (Observation–Feeling):** The gap between how a situation is scientifically observed and intervened upon (L4') and how it is phenomenally felt and evaluated (L1'). This diagonal surfaces when verification methods cannot account for qualitative shifts, or when felt significance resists operationalisation.

## The P-Anchors

Square B is home to P1 (What?) and P4 (Where/When?) on the Day arc, and P1' (Hidden Form / Traces) and P4' (Missed Context / Discovery) on the Night arc.

P1 sits where L1 and L4' cross — material definition meets scientific paradigm. What something *is* depends on both its causal structure and the framework through which that structure is investigated.

P4 sits where L4 and L1' cross — phenomenological horizon meets felt immediacy. Where and when something is encountered depends on both the disclosed situation and the evaluative charge that orients attention within it.

The Night anchors carry what the Day arc conceals: P1' holds the hidden form, the residue and traces that the initial "What?" did not capture. P4' holds the missed context, the expanding horizon that the initial "Where/When?" could not yet see.

## The Move

Before deeper analysis, map the full basin:

1. **L4 — Disclosure:** How is the issue lived? What is disclosed from inside — what matters, what is at stake, what transformation of the knower is involved?
2. **L1 — Causation:** What causal structure is actually operative? Which causes are acknowledged, which repressed?
3. **L4' — Observation:** How is the issue observed? Current state, desired state, constraints, evidence, what would count as verification?
4. **L1' — Feeling:** How is the issue felt before and beneath conceptual framing? What evaluative charge, what bodily knowledge, what felt quality orients the encounter?
5. **Diagonals:** Where do the L4↔L1 and L4'↔L1' crossings reinforce, diverge, or distort each other?
6. **Topology:** What does the chosen topological mode require you to do with those tensions?

## Topology Modulation

**In Torus:** Foreground the lens doing the live work, keep the others as reality checks. Don't overcomplicate a stable run. One diagonal will usually be primary.

**In Klein:** Both diagonals must be stated explicitly. Look for reversals, inversions, mismatches. Let disclosure diagnose what causation conceals; let feeling diagnose what observation misses. The inside/outside doubling of Klein is native to this square.

**In Lemniscatic:** Fold between both sides through P4. Track what changes each time the fold returns — what the earlier positions looked like before the fold, how they look different after. P4 as lemniscatic hinge means context itself is recursively recontextualising the encounter.

## Companion Skills

- `apply-cmea` — works the L4↔L1 diagonal specifically (disclosure vs causation). When the CMEA diagnostic is the primary need, route there rather than running the full basin.
- `run-l4-prime-loop` — works L4' specifically (observe → think → plan → build → execute → verify → learn). When the task is primarily applicative and needs the scientific verification loop, route there.
- `run-positional-coherence` — when the sequential P-pathway (P1→P2→P3→P4→P5→P0) is the right traversal mode rather than square-basin entry. These are orthogonal traversal types that interweave but do not collapse.

## What to Avoid

- Treating the subjective side (L4, L1') as optional because the task looks technical
- Treating the objective side (L1, L4') as optional because the issue feels existential
- Pretending both sides agree when the disagreement is the real doorway
- Working one lens in isolation when its square partners would reveal what it cannot
- Collapsing the two diagonals into a single subjective/objective split — they cross differently

## Output

The map is analysis, not a template. Produce it as prose or structured notes — whatever serves the run. What it needs to show: how the issue is lived (L4), what causes are operative (L1), how it is observed (L4'), how it is felt (L1'), where the diagonals cross or diverge, and what the topology requires next.
