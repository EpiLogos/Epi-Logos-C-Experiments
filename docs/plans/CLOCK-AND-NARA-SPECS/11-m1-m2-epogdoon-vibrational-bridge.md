## III. Why Bitwise Operations Supersede FFT

This section establishes an explicit architectural decision. The M1–M2 bridge does not use Fourier analysis. The correct operations are integer arithmetic and bitwise logic.

**Why NOT Fourier/FFT:**

The hexagram codes, decan assignments, chakra activations, and tattva mappings are discrete symbolic structures with exact, pre-defined correspondences. They are not samples of a continuous periodic waveform.

Concretely:

1. FFT assumes a continuous periodic signal distributed over a uniform domain. The 64 hexagrams do NOT distribute evenly over the 360° circle: 360 / 64 = 5.625°, which is not an integer. Attempting to apply FFT over a non-integer-resolution domain introduces aliasing artifacts that have no symbolic meaning and destroy the exact decan assignments.

2. FFT destroys the symbolic precision of individual hexagram–decan correspondences. The meaning of hexagram 42 at its specific decan position is a discrete fact. A frequency-domain approximation averaging over neighboring positions loses this information entirely.

3. The 64-fold hexagram space and the 72-fold half-decan space are not commensurable as waveform sample spaces. Their relationship is algebraic (64 × 9/8 = 72), not spectral.

**Why bitwise IS correct:**

1. A hexagram is already a 6-bit number (0–63). The XOR of two hexagram values produces the changing-lines mask directly — this is the native arithmetic of the I-Ching. The entire oracle computation (primary hexagram, shadow, temporal transform) is native bitwise logic.

2. The Watson-Crick complement (the # operator applied to nucleotide pairs) is bitwise NOT on the 4-nucleotide encoding. In the ACTG `{6, 9, 7, 8}` encoding: T↔A is `6 XOR 15 = 9`, C↔G is `7 XOR 15 = 8`. The # inversion at the base-pair level is a bitwise operation.

3. The epogdoon translation 64 → 72 is integer multiplication followed by integer division with no remainder (64 × 9 = 576, 576 / 8 = 72). Integer arithmetic on discrete symbolic indices preserves meaning and is O(1).

4. The M2 resonant structures — DECAN_BODY_PARTS[36], PLANET_CHAKRA[9], CHAKRA_BODY_ZONES[8], DECAN_HERBS[36] — are all pre-indexed to the 72-fold half-decan grid (via the half-decan index and the `half_decan / 2` reduction to decan index). Lookup via integer index is exact, O(1), and admits no approximation error.

**The cymatic isomorphism without FFT:**

The claim that hexagram structures are cymatically isomorphic with M2 vibrational structures (decan patterns, planetary harmonics, tattvic shapes) does NOT rest on waveform analysis. It rests on the shared arithmetic: both systems are generated from mod-6/mod-12 discrete arithmetic.

Specifically:

- I-Ching: 2^6 binary arithmetic, 6-line codes, 12-fold complement structure (see base-pair rule in `03-spanda-double-helix-12fold.md`)
- Decans: 36 = 6^2 / 1 = 360 / 10. The decan system has a natural mod-6 structure (6 decans per quadrant, 6 decans per Strand A period in the double helix). The 72-fold half-decan is 72 = 6 × 12 = 6 × (the 12-fold spanda period).
- Cymatic nodal patterns (Chladni figures): resonant mode shapes for a circular membrane in mode (m, n) have `2m + n` nodal lines. The lowest modes satisfying the boundary conditions follow the same mod-6/mod-12 count sequences that govern the hexagram and decan systems.

The isomorphism is in the arithmetic, not in the waveform. The same discrete counting structure appears in all three registers (hexagram / decan / cymatic mode) because they all arise from the same fundamental combinatorics of 6-fold symmetry.

## V. The 72 in Esoteric and Symbolic Traditions

The recurrence of 72 across traditions is not a sequence of coincidences. Each instance expresses the same discrete arithmetic structure in a different symbolic register.

**72 divine names (Shem HaMephorash):** The 72 three-letter names of God in Hebrew tradition are derived from Exodus 14:19–21 (three verses of exactly 72 letters each, read in forward, reverse, and forward order to form 72 three-letter combinations). Each name is assigned to one 5° segment of the sky — one half-decan. The 72 names and the 72 half-decans are in exact bijective correspondence. This is not an external analogy: the Shem HaMephorash tradition was explicitly organized around the same 5° half-decan grid used by Hellenistic astrology.

**72 decans (some Hellenistic traditions):** In systems where each 10° decan is governed by two spirit names (rather than one), the count doubles to 72. The half-decan at 5° is the natural unit in these traditions, giving 72 decan spirits total. This is the B-strand half-decan tradition, explicitly marking both the Strand A (explicit) and Strand B (implicit) assignments per 10° segment.

**72 = 8 × 9:** Both the chakral series (8 terms) and the planetary series (9 terms) as factors. Any symbolic system built around either the 8-fold or the 9-fold structure will find the 72-fold grid as their natural least common multiple meeting point.

**72 hours = 3 days:** The traditional 3-day initiation cycle in mystery traditions. Three full Earth rotations. At 15°/hour (the backbone clock rate — see `02-16-lenses-backbone-temporal.md §Lens 7`), 72 hours = 72 × 15° = 1080° = 3 × 360°. The 72-hour initiation traverses the entire clock face three times.

**72-EDO (equal divisions of the octave):** The 72-tone microtonal system provides exact integer representations of all Pythagorean just-intonation ratios. 72 / 12 = 6 subdivisions per semitone; 72 / 6 = 12 semitones per octave. The 72-EDO system can represent the 9:8 whole tone as exactly 12 steps (72/6 = 12 steps per whole tone), making the epogdoon an integer step in this tuning system. The 72-fold vibrational template of the clock is the spatial analog of 72-EDO in the frequency domain.

The common thread: 72 is the smallest integer that is simultaneously divisible by 8 (chakral series), 9 (planetary series), 12 (spanda/zodiacal period), and 6 (QL position count). It is the LCM of the fundamental counting units of the system:

```
LCM(8, 9)   = 72
LCM(6, 12)  = 12  (not 72 — the QL × spanda product gives 72 by multiplication, not LCM)
8 × 9       = 72  (the product, encoding the epogdoon)
12 × 6      = 72  (the product, encoding the full spanda-QL enumeration)
```

These two product decompositions (8×9 and 12×6) are both valid factorizations of 72. They correspond to the M2 route (8 chakras × 9 planets) and the M1 route (12 ticks × 6 QL positions) respectively.

## VI. WALK_HEXAGRAM and WALK_LINE_CHANGE in the 72-Fold Context

From `00-canonical-invariants.md §6` and `02-16-lenses-backbone-temporal.md`:

```
WALK_HEXAGRAM:    64 steps, ~5.625° per step, no matching lens
WALK_LINE_CHANGE: 384 steps (= 64 × 6), no matching lens
```

Both walks have no matching lens because 360 / 64 = 5.625° is not an integer. The 64-fold hexagram space cannot be a factor-pair in the 16-lens matrix, which requires integer division of 360.

**The 72-fold template as the bridge between degree space and hexagram space:**

```
Degree space (lens-based, integer):   360° / n for various integer n
Hexagram space (binary, 6-bit):       64 values, step size ~5.625°
72-fold template (bridge):            360° / 72 = 5° per half-decan (integer)
```

The half-decan at 5° IS the integer step size that approximates the hexagram step (5.625°) most closely while remaining an exact divisor of 360. The 72-fold template is the lens-compatible representation of the hexagram space — it is what you get when you "round" the 64-fold binary space to the nearest integer-divisor grid via the epogdoon transformation.

Importantly, this rounding is NOT an approximation error in the symbolic sense. The 64→72 step via 9:8 is exact integer arithmetic. What changes is the perspective: in degree space you work with 72 half-decan positions (5° each); in hexagram space you work with 64 binary codes. The epogdoon is the translation function between these two exact systems. No information is lost — the two systems are in bijective (if asymmetric) correspondence via the epogdoon transformation.

**Neither WALK_HEXAGRAM nor WALK_LINE_CHANGE traverses the 72-fold template directly:**

- WALK_HEXAGRAM operates in 64-fold M3 binary space
- WALK_ZODIAC and WALK_SPANDA operate in 12-fold M1 space
- The 72-fold template is traversed by applying the epogdoon transformation to either walk, not by a walk of its own

**WALK_HALF_DECAN (open implementation question):**

A hypothetical `WALK_HALF_DECAN` would have 72 steps of 5° each. This walk would directly traverse the vibrational template. It would have a matching lens if and only if 72 is a factor of 360 — and 360 / 72 = 5, which is an integer. So unlike WALK_HEXAGRAM, a WALK_HALF_DECAN WOULD have a matching lens: a hypothetical `Lens_HalfDecan` with `step_size = 5° × 72 = 360°`, which is Lens 0 applied at 5° granularity.

However: the 16-lens matrix as currently specified covers the 16 exhaustive archetypal factor-pairs of 360. A 5°×72 lens would be `Lens N: 5° × 72 = 360 segments` — but this is BETWEEN the current Lens 3 (8°×45) and Lens 4 (9°×40). It is a valid divisor of 360 but is NOT among the 16 archetypal lenses. Adding it would expand the lens count to 17 or require redefining the lens selection criterion.

This is an open architectural question. The spec does not mandate adding WALK_HALF_DECAN or a corresponding lens. It notes:

1. The 72-fold traversal is expressible as a 72-step walk with 5° steps
2. Such a walk would require a new lens entry not currently in the 16-lens matrix
3. Whether to add it depends on whether 5°×72 is considered an archetypal division of the circle — which is an onto-architectural decision, not a technical one

The current architecture handles 72-fold traversal through the M2 epogdoon transformation applied at the data-access layer (DECAN_BODY_PARTS, PLANET_CHAKRA lookups) rather than as a walk mode.
