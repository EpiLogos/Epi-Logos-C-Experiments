# [[L2-1]] — IS: Being, Assertion, Truth

> **Sub-lens #1 of [[L2]] [[Logical]]:** The cataphatic affirmation — what can be positively asserted. The first corner of the [[catuṣkoṭi]]: definite existence, verified fact, the law of identity.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L2-1]] is the first determination to emerge from the Tetralemmaic Ground of [[L2-0]]: the act of saying *it is*. This is the cataphatic gesture par excellence — the drawing of a boundary that says "here, something exists; here, something is true." [[Parmenides]]' *to eon* ("that which is") asserts that Being is, and non-being is not, and from this assertion the entire Western tradition of formal logic descends.

IS is not merely one position among four; it is the *first* position — the initial collapse of the pre-logical wave function into a definite state. Without IS, neither IS-NOT nor BOTH nor NEITHER would have anything to work upon. Affirmation precedes negation as figure precedes ground.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** IS — Being, Assertion, Truth
**Tradition:** [[Parmenides]] (*it is and cannot not be*); [[Aristotle]]'s law of identity (A = A); classical two-valued logic; [[Frege]]'s assertion sign; [[Tarski]]'s semantic conception of truth
**Concept:** The cataphatic affirmation — the domain of verified fact, definite existence, and logical truth. To say "IS" is to define, to draw the first boundary, to affirm identity. The position where the law of identity holds without qualification and [[excluded middle]] stands firm.

---

## §2 — Operation

[[L2-1]] receives the undetermined openness of [[L2-0]] and collapses it into definite assertion. It feeds into [[L2-2]] (IS-NOT), because every affirmation immediately implies its negation — to say "it IS X" is already to distinguish X from not-X. The operational pair [[L2-1]]/[[L2-2]] forms the binary foundation of classical logic: together they exhaust the space of bivalent reasoning. But the [[catuṣkoṭi]] reveals this binary as incomplete — hence the arc continues to [[L2-3]] (BOTH) and beyond.

The horizontal tension: [[L2-1]] is the domain of certainty, clarity, and decidability. Its strength is precision; its limitation is that not everything admits of definite assertion.
