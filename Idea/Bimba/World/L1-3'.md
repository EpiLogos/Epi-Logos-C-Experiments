# [[L1-3']] — Thinking

> **Coordinate**: `L1-3'` | **Parent**: [[L1']] Phenomenal | **#3' within L1'**: The analytical function — logical classification. Rational, judging: what *kind* of thing this is.

---

## §0 — Ground: The Logical Interior

[[L1-3']] emerges from [[L1-2']]'s evaluative engagement as the principle of *classification*. [[Feeling]] determined worth; [[Thinking]] determines *kind*. It names, categorises, connects by principle, establishes causal chains. This is the function most closely aligned with the Day complement [[L1]] ([[Causal]]) — but here seen from inside as a *phenomenal mode* rather than from outside as an objective structure.

[[Jung]] classified [[Thinking]] as a **rational** (judging) function: it operates by criteria of logical consistency, classification, and conceptual relation. The thinking type asks: what category does this belong to? What principle governs it? What follows logically? At [[#3]], [[Thinking]] occupies the position of *pattern* — it IS the phenomenal experience of recognising form.

---

## §1 — Definition: Logical Classification

**Name**: [[Thinking]] — Analytical / Logical Classification
**Tradition**: [[Jung]] (*[[Psychological Types]]*); [[Aristotle]] (the syllogism as thinking's paradigm); [[Kant]] (the categories of understanding)
**Key Concept**: The rational (judging) function that determines *what kind* of thing something is — classification, naming, logical connection.

[[Thinking]] is [[Feeling]]'s complementary opposite. Where [[Feeling]] evaluates by worth, [[Thinking]] evaluates by truth. Where [[Feeling]] asks "is it good?" [[Thinking]] asks "is it true?" — or more precisely, "what *is* it?" [[Jung]]: "Thinking, if it is to be real thinking, must be directed to something — it must have a content." That content is logical form — the [[eidos]] encountered from within.

---

## §2 — Operation: The Classifying Moment

[[L1-3']] follows [[L1-2']] ([[Feeling]]) and precedes [[L1-4']] ([[Intuition]]). The movement: what has been evaluated ([[L1-2']]) is now classified ([[L1-3']]); what has been classified will then be intuited for its wider potential ([[L1-4']]). [[Thinking]] and [[Feeling]] are the rational pair in tension — strengthening one's thinking often means weakening one's feeling, and vice versa. This is the core of [[Jung]]'s type dynamics.

**Day complement**: [[L1-3]] ([[Formal Cause]]). This is the tightest Day-Night alignment in the entire [[L1]]-[[L1']] pair. [[Formal cause]] is the objective [[eidos]] — the pattern that makes a thing what it is. [[Thinking]] is the subjective act of *recognising* that pattern. They are nearly identical operations: one seen as ontological structure ([[L1-3]]), the other as phenomenal experience ([[L1-3']]). The fact that they share [[#3]] (Pattern) is not coincidence but structural necessity.
