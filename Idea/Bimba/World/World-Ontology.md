## Centrality Contract

[[World-Ontology]] is the flat [[World]] nexus for durable system knowledge. It is not a plan, sprint note, or implementation shard. Its job is to make the whole architecture navigable from the crystallised ontology layer, then point each actionable detail back to the owning [[Seeds]] spec, [[World/Types]] MOC/canvas, and live [[Body]] substrate.

Use the three residency surfaces this way:

| Surface | Authority | What Belongs There |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [[World]] | Crystallised definitions and stable architecture-as-documentation | Flat canonical forms like [[World-Ontology]], [[P]], [[CT0]], [[NOW]], [[Seed]], and [[Task-Spec]] |
| [[World/Types]] | Type-local MOCs, canvases, and coordinate ontology mirrors | `Idea/Bimba/World/Types/Coordinates/**`, `*.canvas`, type indexes, and ontology incubation surfaces |
| [[Seeds]] | Planning, specs, source traces, architecture packs, and migrated legacy evidence | [[ARCHITECTURE-DIAGRAM-PACK]], [[S-SYSTEM-INDEX]], [[S-SHARD-HARMONIZATION-PROTOCOL]], shard specs, traceability indexes, and [[LEGACY-DOCS-MIGRATION-INDEX]] |

Centrality rule: when an agent needs durable architecture context, start here, then follow the appropriate route:

1. [[World-Ontology]] for the stable ontology frame.
2. [[ARCHITECTURE-DIAGRAM-PACK]] for current system diagrams and MOC residency law.
3. [[S-SYSTEM-INDEX]] or [[M'-SYSTEM-SPEC]] for the active build/system branch.
4. The exact coordinate spec and shard specs, such as [[S1-SPEC]], [[S1'-SPEC]], [[S1-0-SPEC]], or [[S1-0'-SPEC]].
5. The matching [[World/Types]] definition/canvas and live `Body/S` or `Body/M` code cited by the spec.

This keeps [[World]] central without letting it absorb the planning/source burden of [[Seeds]] or the type-local canvas burden of [[World/Types]].
