# [[L2-2]] — IS-NOT: Non-being, Negation, Falsity

> **Sub-lens #2 of [[L2]] [[Logical]]:** The apophatic negation — what can be definitively denied. The second corner of the [[catuṣkoṭi]]: boundary-drawing, definition by exclusion, the [[via negativa]].

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L2-2]] is the act of saying *it is not*. Where [[L2-1]] affirms, [[L2-2]] denies — and in denying, it *defines*. Every boundary is a negation: to say what something is requires saying what it is not. IS-NOT is not the mere absence of IS but an active operation — the cutting, distinguishing, separating function that gives contour to existence. Without negation, affirmation would be a featureless plenum, an undifferentiated "yes" with no content.

The [[via negativa]] of [[Pseudo-Dionysius]] understood this: God is better known by what God is *not* than by what God is. The *neti neti* ("not this, not this") of [[Advaita Vedānta]] reaches toward the Absolute precisely by exhaustive negation. [[L2-2]] is this apophatic power formalised.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** IS-NOT — Non-being, Negation, Falsity
**Tradition:** [[Parmenides]]' prohibition (and its violation); [[Plato]]'s *Sophist* (the "patricide" that admits non-being); [[Pseudo-Dionysius]]' [[via negativa]]; [[Advaita Vedānta]]'s *neti neti*; [[Spinoza]]'s *omnis determinatio est negatio*
**Concept:** The apophatic negation — the boundary-drawing function through which definition by exclusion operates. [[Spinoza]]'s insight that "all determination is negation" places IS-NOT at the heart of every act of defining. Negation IS an operation — the act of distinguishing, separating, cutting.

---

## §2 — Operation

[[L2-2]] receives [[L2-1]]'s affirmation and opposes it, generating the classical binary: IS / IS-NOT. Together they form the domain of [[Aristotle|Aristotelian]] bivalence — the assumption that every proposition is either true or false. [[L2-2]] feeds into [[L2-3]] (BOTH), because the binary of affirmation and negation eventually encounters cases where *both* seem to hold simultaneously — the crack in bivalence that opens onto [[dialetheism]].

The operational insight: negation is not parasitic on affirmation but *co-original* with it. The pair IS/IS-NOT arise together from [[L2-0]]'s undetermined ground. [[L2-2]] is not secondary; it is the second stroke of the same act.
