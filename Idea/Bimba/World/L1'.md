## $4 — Context: The Subjective Interior of Objective Structure

[[L1']] is the Night complement of [[L1]] ([[Causal]]). The inversion is precise: [[L1]] asks what *causes* things (objective, structural, from outside); [[L1']] asks what things *feel like* (subjective, immediate, from inside). These are not two different realities but two faces of the same reality revealed by the [[Klein]] traversal. Every causal chain, seen from within, is a sequence of [[qualia]]; every qualitative experience, seen from without, is a causal structure.

This lens is grounded in [[Jung]]'s *[[Psychological Types]]* (1921) — his most systematic attempt to map the **complete typology of how consciousness apprehends its world**. [[Jung]] identified four psychic functions modified by two attitudes:

- **Two attitudes**: [[Introversion]] (libido directed inward, toward internal objects) and [[Extroversion]] (libido directed outward, toward external objects)
- **Two rational (judging) functions**: [[Thinking]] (logical classification — what *kind* of thing) and [[Feeling]] (evaluative — what *worth* the thing has)
- **Two irrational (perceiving) functions**: [[Sensation]] (immediate perception — the "thereness" of phenomena) and [[Intuition]] (perceiving possibilities, hidden connections, future developments)

The 2 attitudes + 4 functions give 8 types, but the underlying structure is the **6-fold** arrangement preserved here: the two poles (Introversion/Extroversion) frame the four functions, creating a complete phenomenal field.

The [[Water]] element is fitting: [[L1']] is the fluid, reflective, feeling-toned surface of experience — consciousness as a medium through which reality is *lived* rather than merely *known*.

## $5 — Integration: The Six Phenomenal Modes

### [[L1-0']] — Introversion (Toward-Unconscious-Pole)

The inward orientation — consciousness directed toward internal objects, the [[unconscious]] pole. Not pathological withdrawal but a fundamental **attitude**: [[libido]] directed inward. [[Jung]]: the introvert's primary relationship is with the inner world of images, feelings, and archetypes. The introvert does not *lack* engagement with the outer world but *mediates* it through inner experience. At [[P0]], Introversion is the phenomenal face of Ground: the turn inward that discovers the psychic depths beneath all external givenness.

### [[L1-1']] — Sensation (Immediate Perception)

Immediate perception — the "thereness" of phenomena. **Irrational** (non-judging): simply registers what is present. [[Sensation]] answers the question *that* something is, before any judgment of what it means or whether it is good. The raw sensory datum: color, sound, texture, pressure, warmth. [[Jung]] classed Sensation as irrational not because it is unreasonable but because it does not evaluate — it receives. At [[P1]], Sensation is the phenomenal face of Definition: the most basic encounter with the material given.

### [[L1-2']] — Feeling (Evaluation of Worth)

Evaluative function — assigning worth. **Rational** (judging): good/bad, pleasant/unpleasant, harmonious/discordant. [[Feeling]] is not emotion (which is a complex reaction) but a **judging function** that determines the *value* of an experience. [[Jung]] emphasized that Feeling is as rational as [[Thinking]] — it operates by criteria, but criteria of worth rather than truth. At [[P2]], Feeling is the phenomenal face of Operation: the active engagement that determines what matters.

### [[L1-3']] — Thinking (Logical Analysis)

Analytical function — logical classification. **Rational** (judging): determines what *kind* of thing this is. [[Thinking]] names, categorizes, connects by principle, establishes causal chains. It is the function most closely aligned with the Day complement [[L1]] ([[Causal]]) — but here seen from inside as a *phenomenal mode* rather than from outside as an objective structure. At [[P3]], Thinking is the phenomenal face of Pattern: the recognition of logical form within experience.

### [[L1-4']] — Intuition (Perception of Possibilities)

Perceptive function — grasping possibilities, hidden connections, future developments. **Irrational** (non-judging): perceives the *field of potential*. [[Intuition]] does not analyze what is present but apprehends what is *possible* — the penumbra of meaning surrounding every fact. [[Jung]]: "Intuition is not mere perception, or vision, but an active, creative process." At [[P4]], Intuition is the phenomenal face of Context: the capacity to sense the whole configuration in which any particular stands.

### [[L1-5']] — Extroversion (Toward-Conscious-Pole)

The outward orientation — consciousness directed toward external objects, the conscious pole. [[Libido]] invested in the world. The extravert's primary relationship is with the outer world of objects, persons, and events. Extroversion completes the phenomenal circuit: where [[L1-0']] (Introversion) turned inward to discover the psychic depths, [[L1-5']] turns outward to discover the world as given. Together, the two attitudes frame the entire phenomenal field. At [[P5]], Extroversion is the phenomenal face of Integration: consciousness fully engaged with the manifest world.

## $0 — Inquiry: The Irreducible Interiority of Experience

### The Question Inverted

> *What is it like? — not what causes it, but what it feels like from inside.*

[[L1']] asks the question that no amount of [[Causal]] ([[L1]]) analysis can answer: the question of [[qualia]], of subjective immediacy, of what [[Thomas Nagel]] called "what it is like to be." The Night inversion reveals that objective structure (Day) always implies a subjective interior (Night) — and this interior is irreducible. No causal account, however complete, exhausts the lived quality of experience.
