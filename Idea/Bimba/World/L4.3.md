# [[L4.3]] — Zeit (Time / Temporality)

> **Sub-lens #3 of [[L4]] [[Phenomenological]]:** The threefold ecstatic structure of [[Dasein]]'s temporality: having-been, being-alongside, being-ahead-of-oneself. "The meaning of [[Dasein]] is temporality."

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L4.3]] names *[[Zeit]]* — temporality as the meaning of [[Dasein]]'s being. [[Heidegger]]'s claim is radical: time is not a container within which events happen, nor a sequence of now-points stretching from past to future. Time is the fundamental structure of [[Dasein]]'s existence — the way [[Dasein]] *is* its past, engages its present, and projects toward its future simultaneously.

The three temporal "ecstases" (literally, "standing-outside-oneself") are: **having-been** ([[Geworfenheit]]/past — carrying the weight of facticity), **being-alongside** (falling/present — absorbed engagement with current concern), and **being-ahead-of-oneself** (projection/future — the structure of possibility and anticipation). These are not three separate time-slices but three dimensions of a single temporal stretch. We carry our past, engage our present, and project toward our future *at once*.

This is [[L4]]'s encounter with [[P3]] (Pattern/Process): temporality IS the pattern of existence — the recurring structure of thrown-projection that constitutes [[Dasein]]'s processual nature.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** Zeit (Time / Temporality)
**Tradition:** [[Heidegger]]'s *Being and Time* Division II §61–§83; [[Husserl]]'s time-consciousness; [[Augustine]]'s *Confessions* Book XI
**Concept:** Ecstatic temporality — the threefold structure (having-been, being-alongside, being-ahead-of-oneself) that constitutes [[Dasein]]'s meaning. Time as the horizon for the understanding of [[Being]]. Authentic temporality includes being-toward-death: finitude is not a limitation added to existence but the condition that gives it urgency and wholeness.

---

## §2 — Operation

[[L4.3]] receives the disclosed world from [[L4.2]] and reveals its temporal scaffolding: every act of world-disclosure is stretched across the three ecstases. The hammer "ready-to-hand" has a past (the workshop tradition), a present (the current task), and a future (the building project). When the hammer breaks, all three ecstases converge in the moment of disruption. [[L4.3]] feeds into [[L4.4]] ([[Besorge]]) because *care* is the existential structure that unifies the three ecstases: "being-ahead-of-oneself-already-in-(the-world)-alongside-(entities-encountered-within-the-world)" — [[Heidegger]]'s formal definition of [[Sorge]].

The transition from [[L4.3]] to [[L4.4]] is the pivot from descriptive analysis to structural unity: temporality *describes* the shape of existence; care *names* the unity that holds those temporal dimensions together.
