# [[L4.1]] — Geworfenheit (Thrownness)

> **Sub-lens #1 of [[L4]] [[Phenomenological]]:** The facticity of [[Dasein]] — finding oneself already thrown into a world one did not choose. An ongoing condition of non-mastery over one's own origin.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L4.1]] names what [[Heidegger]] calls *[[Geworfenheit]]* — the structural fact that [[Dasein]] always finds itself *already* in a situation, already with certain moods, already understanding things in certain ways. [[Thrownness]] is not a one-time event (being born) but an ongoing condition: at every moment we discover ourselves already here, already committed, already disposed. As [[Heidegger]] writes, [[Geworfenheit]] names "There-being's non-mastery of its own origin and its referential dependence on other beings."

This is [[L4]]'s encounter with [[P1]] (Definition/Form): [[thrownness]] IS the first definition — the given conditions that constitute our facticity. We are defined before we define. Body, language, culture, historical epoch, mood — these are not chosen but *found*, and they constitute the horizon within which all choosing occurs. The factical is not opposed to the free; it is the medium through which freedom operates.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** Geworfenheit (Thrownness)
**Tradition:** [[Heidegger]]'s *Being and Time* §29–§38; [[Kierkegaard]]'s *anxiety*; [[Sartre]]'s *facticity*
**Concept:** The non-derivable givenness of [[Dasein]]'s situation. Not what I have chosen but what I find myself already within: body, birth, language, mood, historical moment. The condition of always already being "in the middle of things" without having willed it. [[Thrownness]] reveals that [[Dasein]] is *delivered over* to its existence — responsible for a being it did not originate.

---

## §2 — Operation

[[L4.1]] receives the [[Being]]-question from [[L4.0]] and concretises it: the question of [[Being]] is not asked by a free-floating subject but by a being that is *thrown* — already situated, already disposed, already in a mood. [[L4.1]] feeds into [[L4.2]] ([[Dasein]]) because the thrown being is precisely the one that exists as a clearing: facticity is the condition for the disclosure of a world. Without [[thrownness]], [[Dasein]] would be pure possibility without ground; without [[Dasein]]'s clearing ([[L4.2]]), [[thrownness]] would be mere factual occurrence without meaning. The two are structurally inseparable — [[Heidegger]]'s "thrown projection" (*geworfener Entwurf*).
