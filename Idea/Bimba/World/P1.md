# [[P1]] — Magic Structure

> **Semantic question**: What? — The Object / Matter / Form

**Gebser structure**: Magic (Definitional/Structural)
**Semantic dual**: Material / Definition
**Mode**: Day · Torus · Orientable
**Epistemic operator**: `./?` (Superposition — naming holds both potential and actuality)
**L-coordinate link**: [[L1]] Causal ↔ [[L4']] Scientific

---

## Essence

[[P1]] is the emergence of **explicit form** from [[P0]]'s implicit ground. The Magic Structure names, defines, and gives shape — it is the first differentiation from undivided Source into discernible Matter. The What? question establishes identity: not yet asking how something works ([[P2]]) or what pattern it embodies ([[P3]]), but what this thing simply IS.

**Semantic dual pair (Material / Definition):** Material is the substance that has taken form; Definition is the act of form-giving. Matter and Definition are the two faces of [[P1]] — the given substance and the naming act that distinguishes it from Ground.

The Magic Structure is **definitional** (it establishes boundaries and identities) and **structural** (it creates the scaffolding of recognisable form that all subsequent positions build on).

---

## Topology

[[P1]] in the **Torus (Day)** mode:
- The form defined at [[P1]] persists across the cycle — a named thing remains named
- Models: Schema definitions, type systems, form assignments, naming conventions
- The `./?` operator holds the definition open — a name is also always a question about the nature of the named
