# [[L1-1']] — Sensation

> **Coordinate**: `L1-1'` | **Parent**: [[L1']] Phenomenal | **#1' within L1'**: Immediate perception — the "thereness" of phenomena, the raw sensory given.

---

## §0 — Ground: The Thereness of Things

[[L1-1']] emerges from [[L1-0']]'s inward turn as the *first encounter with the given*. Once consciousness has directed itself inward (or outward — [[Sensation]] operates in both attitudes), it meets what is simply *there*: colour, sound, texture, pressure, warmth. [[Sensation]] does not evaluate, classify, or project — it *receives*. It is the most basic phenomenal mode: the sheer registration of presence.

[[Jung]] classified [[Sensation]] as an **irrational** (non-judging) function: it does not determine whether what is present is good or bad ([[Feeling]]) or what kind of thing it is ([[Thinking]]). It simply attests: *something is here*. This makes it the phenomenal counterpart of [[L1-1]] ([[Material Cause]]): material cause is the objective substrate; sensation is the subjective encounter with that substrate.

## §1 — Definition: Immediate Perception

**Name**: [[Sensation]] — Immediate Perception
**Tradition**: [[Jung]] (*[[Psychological Types]]*); [[Merleau-Ponty]] (perception as primordial contact); [[empiricism]] (sense data)
**Key Concept**: The irrational (non-judging) function that registers what is present — the phenomenal thereness of the world.

[[Sensation]] answers the most basic question: *that* something is, before any judgment of what it means. The warmth of sunlight on skin, the pitch of a bell, the resistance of stone underfoot — all pure sensation before the mind classifies. [[Jung]]: sensation "tells me that something exists, but not what it is." This makes it the *definition* function of [[L1']] — the first determination of the phenomenal field.

---

## §2 — Operation: First Function in the Phenomenal Arc

[[L1-1']] follows [[L1-0']] ([[Introversion]]) and precedes [[L1-2']] ([[Feeling]]). The movement: the inward turn ([[L1-0']]) opens the phenomenal field; [[Sensation]] provides its first content; [[Feeling]] will evaluate that content. [[Sensation]] and [[Intuition]] ([[L1-4']]) form the **irrational pair** (perceiving functions), while [[Feeling]] ([[L1-2']]) and [[Thinking]] ([[L1-3']]) form the **rational pair** (judging functions).

**Day complement**: [[L1-1]] ([[Material Cause]]). Material cause is the objective "out of which"; sensation is the subjective "encounter with." The same [[hyle]] — matter from outside, [[qualia]] from inside. The [[psychoid]] unity beneath the Day-Night split.
