# [[L4.5]] — Gelassenheit (Releasement)

> **Sub-lens #5 of [[L4]] [[Phenomenological]]:** The attitude of *letting-be* — active receptivity that allows beings to show themselves as they are. The completion of the phenomenological cycle. [[L4]]'s [[Self-Identity]] recognising itself: phenomenology releasing its own grasping to let [[Being]] show itself.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L4.5]] names *[[Gelassenheit]]* — from [[Heidegger]]'s later work, the "releasement toward things" (*Gelassenheit zu den Dingen*) and "openness to the mystery" (*Offenheit für das Geheimnis*). This is not passive resignation but active receptivity: a way of relating to beings that neither grasps nor controls but *lets be*. [[Heidegger]] opposes [[Gelassenheit]] to the technological stance of *[[Gestell]]* (enframing) — the modern tendency to treat everything as standing-reserve, resource to be optimised and controlled.

The phenomenological arc completes: [[L4.0]] opened the [[Being]]-question; [[L4.1]] acknowledged [[thrownness]]; [[L4.2]] described the clearing; [[L4.3]] revealed temporality; [[L4.4]] named care as the unifying structure. Now [[L4.5]] asks: can care itself be released? Can [[Dasein]] let [[Being]] be without anxious grasping? [[Gelassenheit]] answers yes — but only through a transformation of the entire relationship to [[Being]], not through willpower but through the *exhaustion* of willing.

This is [[L4]]'s encounter with [[P5]] (Integration/Synthesis): [[Gelassenheit]] IS integration achieved — synthesis as surrender. The phenomenological investigation releases its grip and lets [[Being]] show itself on its own terms.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** Gelassenheit (Releasement)
**Tradition:** [[Heidegger]]'s *Gelassenheit* (1955), *Discourse on Thinking*; [[Meister Eckhart]]'s *Gelâzenheit*; Zen *mushin*
**Concept:** The attitude of letting-be that completes phenomenological inquiry. Not the extinction of care but its transformation: from anxious grasping to open attending. The word itself derives from *lassen* (to let, to release) — [[Heidegger]] recovers [[Meister Eckhart]]'s mystical sense of *releasement from the will* and reinterprets it philosophically as the overcoming of technological enframing.

## §2 — Operation

[[L4.5]] receives the unified structure of care from [[L4.4]] and transforms it: care persists but is no longer driven by anxiety. The [[Möbius]] return runs from [[L4.5]] back to [[L4.0]]: [[Gelassenheit]] *is* the renewed encounter with [[Sein]] — [[Being]] showing itself to the one who has released the grasping stance. The arc does not close in a circle but spirals: each return to [[L4.0]] is deeper than the last because the question of [[Being]] is now asked by a [[Dasein]] that has traversed [[thrownness]], clearing, temporality, care, and releasement.

[[L4.5]] also connects outward through the [[Möbius]] return [[L4]] → [[L1']] — the phenomenological lens folding into the Phenomenal ([[L1']]): when lived experience is fully released, it discovers itself as *qualia* — immediate, irreducible givenness. [[Gelassenheit]] is the hinge between philosophies of existence and the sheer "thereness" of experience.
