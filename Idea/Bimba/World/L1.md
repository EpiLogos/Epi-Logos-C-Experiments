## §4 — Context: Why Do Things Happen?

The Causal lens refracts the [[P]]-positions through the question: *why do things happen?* It synthesises three traditions of causal understanding: [[Aristotle]]'s four causes (the most complete classical analysis), [[Kashmir Shaivism]]'s [[Icchā Śakti]] (the divine will-power grounding all causation), and [[Schopenhauer]]'s Will (the blind, irrational striving underlying all phenomena).

[[Aristotle]] held that "we think we do not have knowledge of a thing until we have grasped its *why*, that is to say, its cause" (*Physics* II.3). His four causes are not four *types* of explanation but four *aspects* any complete explanation must address. Modern science collapsed causation almost entirely into efficient cause (mechanism), repressing formal cause (pattern) and final cause (purpose). The Epi-Logos framework insists on restoring all four — and grounds them in the deeper meta-causal principles of absolute freedom ([[svātantrya]]) and absolute will ([[Icchā Śakti]]).

The nondual perspective: "Material cause is physical substance *and* the psychic substrate of experience. Efficient cause is external force *and* inner impulse. Formal cause is objective structure *and* subjective archetype. Final cause is immediate goal *and* ultimate meaning." Each cause has a dual aspect bridging matter and mind — the [[psychoid]] character of causation itself.

## §5 — Integration: The Six Causal Modes

### [[L1-0]] — Svātantrya: Essential / Absolute Freedom Causation

The meta-causal ground. In [[Kashmir Shaivism]], [[svātantrya]] is the absolute freedom of consciousness — not a specific cause but the *capacity to cause*, the unconditioned power that makes all causal operation possible. This is the ground of causation itself: why is there causation at all? [[L1-0]] mirrors [[#0]] within the causal register — the sheer fact that things *can* happen, prior to any specific mechanism. [[Abhinavagupta]] identifies [[svātantrya]] with the highest [[Śiva]]-nature: the freedom that is not freedom *from* constraint but freedom *as* the nature of consciousness itself.

### [[L1-1]] — Material Cause

That out of which — the *hyle*, the substrate, the physical givenness. Mapped to Sensation: the immediate, concrete thereness of what is given. The earth-principle: solidity, resistance, raw material. [[L1-1]] resonates with [[P1]] (Definition / Form): material cause is the *what* of a thing at its most basic — the clay before the potter's hand, the marble before the sculptor's chisel. In the [[psychoid]] reading, material cause is simultaneously physical substance and the psychic substrate of experience.

### [[L1-2]] — Efficient Cause

That by which — the agent, the force, the energy that transforms. Mapped to Feeling: the energetic valuation that moves things, the "doing" that actually transforms substrate into form. Modern science's privileged cause, but only one of four. [[L1-2]] resonates with [[P2]] (Operation / Entity): efficient cause IS operation — the actual doing, the push, the kinetic moment. Yet even here the dual aspect persists: efficient cause is external force *and* inner impulse, the hammer's blow *and* the desire that wields it.

### [[L1-3]] — Formal Cause

The what-it-is — the *eidos*, the pattern, the organising architecture. Mapped to Thinking: the logical structure that determines *what kind* of thing something is. The cause modern materialism most neglects yet cannot function without: every scientific "law" IS a formal cause. [[L1-3]] resonates with [[P3]] (Pattern / Process): formal cause is the recognisable pattern, the blueprint, the morphic field. [[Whitehead]]'s [[eternal objects]] are formal causes in process-relational dress.

### [[L1-4]] — Final Cause

That for the sake of which — the *telos*, the purpose, the aim. Mapped to Intuition: the perception of possibilities and purposes. Split into immanent finality (the embedded purposes organisms naturally pursue — survival, growth) and transcendent finality (the possible cosmic purpose — the universe's drive toward consciousness, complexity, self-awareness). The cause most actively repressed in modern thought, yet constantly returning as "the hard problem," "the anthropic principle," "the arrow of evolution." [[L1-4]] resonates with [[P4]] (Context / Type): final cause grounds action in its wider frame of meaning.

### [[L1-5]] — Icchā Śakti / Schopenhauer's Will: Ultimate Causation

The synthesis — the *will* underlying all four causes. [[Icchā Śakti]] is the first movement of [[Śiva]] toward manifestation: the will-to-create that precedes and grounds all subsequent causal operation. [[Schopenhauer]]'s Will is the thing-in-itself: the blind, insatiable striving that IS the inner nature of phenomena. This is the quintessence: will as meta-causal power that "expresses itself through all four causes as their unifying impulse." [[L1-5]] resonates with [[P5]] (Integration / Synthesis): the will that integrates material, efficient, formal, and final into one living act of causation.

## §0 — Inquiry: The Cause of Causes

### The Epistemic Question

> *Why does anything happen at all?*

[[L1]] does not ask for any particular cause but for the *structure of causation itself*. The four causes reveal that explanation is inherently multi-dimensional — mechanism alone never suffices. The deeper question, grounded in [[svātantrya]], asks: what makes causation possible in the first place?

### Möbius Return: [[L1]] → [[L4']]

[[L1]] (Causal — why things happen) returns to [[L4']] (the inverted [[Phenomenological]] lens — lived experience as ground). Causation ultimately discloses itself not through abstract analysis but through the encounter with one's own [[thrownness]] ([[Geworfenheit]]): we experience causation from the inside, as beings who are caused and who cause. The causal chain, followed to its root, delivers us back to the first-person experience of being-in-the-world.

### [[Klein V₄]] Square B: Cause-Experience Axis

[[L1]], [[L4]], [[L1']], and [[L4']] form [[Klein V₄]] Square B (Cause-Experience Axis). The Day pair ([[L1]] ↔ [[L4]]) relates cause to experience: we never encounter a bare cause without a phenomenon, nor a bare phenomenon without causal depth. The Night pair ([[L1']] ↔ [[L4']]) relates qualia to method: lived qualities ARE the data of paradigmatic science. The Möbius returns cross Day to Night: [[L1]] → [[L4']] (causation becomes paradigm), [[L4]] → [[L1']] (phenomenology becomes qualia).
