# [[L5-0']] — Arche (ἀρχή)

> **Sub-lens #0' of [[L5']] Divine Logos:** "In the beginning was the Word" ([[John 1:1]]). The Greek *[[arche]]* means simultaneously beginning, principle, and sovereignty. [[L5']]'s [[Self-Identity]] transparent: the [[Logos]] encountering itself at its own origin — the Word before all worlds.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L5-0']] is where the Divine [[Logos]] meets its own eternal ground. [[John 1:1]] deliberately echoes [[Genesis 1:1]] (*Bereshit*): "In the [[arche]] was the [[Logos]]." The Greek ἀρχή refuses to separate temporal origin from structural principle from governing authority — all three meanings coexist in a single word. The [[Logos]] is present *at* the beginning because the [[Logos]] IS the beginning.

This is [[L5']]'s [[Self-Identity]]: the Divine Word recognising that it has no origin outside itself. [[Heraclitus]]: "Of this [[Logos]], being forever, men prove to be uncomprehending." The [[Arche]] is not a moment that passes but an eternal ground that perdures — the inexhaustible source from which every subsequent moment of divine speech springs. Where [[L5-0]] ([[Anuttara]]) names the silence beyond speech from the Day side, [[L5-0']] names the Word that IS that silence — not silence awaiting speech, but the [[Logos]] already fully present in its own groundless ground.
