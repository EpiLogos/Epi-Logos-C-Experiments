# [[L1-2']] — Feeling

> **Coordinate**: `L1-2'` | **Parent**: [[L1']] Phenomenal | **#2' within L1'**: The evaluative function — assigning worth. Rational, judging: good/bad, pleasant/unpleasant, harmonious/discordant.

---

## §0 — Ground: The Evaluation of Worth

[[L1-2']] emerges from [[L1-1']]'s raw perception as the *first judgment*. [[Sensation]] attests that something is present; [[Feeling]] determines whether that something is *good or bad*, *pleasant or unpleasant*, *harmonious or discordant*. This is not emotion (which is a complex reaction involving multiple functions) but a **judging function** — as rational, in [[Jung]]'s precise sense, as [[Thinking]].

[[Jung]] emphasised this distinction relentlessly: [[Feeling]] is not sentimentality, not mood, not affect. It is a discriminating function that evaluates experience according to criteria of *worth*. Where [[Thinking]] asks "what kind?" [[Feeling]] asks "what value?" Both judge; they differ in their criteria.

---

## §1 — Definition: The Value-Function

**Name**: [[Feeling]] — Evaluation of Worth
**Tradition**: [[Jung]] (*[[Psychological Types]]*); [[Scheler]] (material value-ethics); [[Heidegger]] ([[Befindlichkeit]] — attunement)
**Key Concept**: The rational (judging) function that determines the *worth* of an experience — good/bad, pleasant/unpleasant, important/trivial.

[[Feeling]] operates by criteria as rigorous as [[Thinking]]'s — but criteria of value rather than truth. A person with differentiated [[Feeling]] does not merely react emotionally; they *discern* worth with precision: this matters, that does not; this is beautiful, that is ugly; this relationship nourishes, that one depletes. [[Scheler]]'s hierarchy of values (sensory → vital → spiritual → holy) maps the range of what [[Feeling]] evaluates.

## §2 — Operation: The Energetic Mover

[[L1-2']] follows [[L1-1']] ([[Sensation]]) and precedes [[L1-3']] ([[Thinking]]). The movement: what is perceived ([[L1-1']]) is evaluated ([[L1-2']]) before it is classified ([[L1-3']]). [[Feeling]] and [[Thinking]] form the **rational pair**: both judge, but [[Feeling]] judges by value and [[Thinking]] judges by category. They are complementary opposites — strengthening one often weakens the other.

**Day complement**: [[L1-2]] ([[Efficient Cause]]). Efficient cause is the objective force that transforms; [[Feeling]] is the subjective energy that moves. Both are *operational* — they do the work. The hammer's blow ([[L1-2]]) and the desire that wields it ([[L1-2']]) are the same kinetic moment, split by the Day-Night divide.
