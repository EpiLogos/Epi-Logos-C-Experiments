# Family C — Converse-Mirror

## Pairing Rule

`{ (0,5), (1,4), (2,3) }` — three mirror-pairs across the six-fold cycle. Each pair sums to 5; each pair is its own *converse*.

## Meaning

Family C is the **converse-mirror grammar**: the three pairs across which the hexad folds upon itself. `(0,5)` mirrors ground against synthesis, `(1,4)` mirrors definition against context, `(2,3)` mirrors operation against pattern. The relation is not transition but **co-implication** — each pole holds the other in negative-image. C is the grammar by which the QL hexad demonstrates its Klein V₄ symmetry: three commuting reflections whose composition returns the identity.

In the chromatic basis the pairs read as minor-7th, tritone, and whole-tone (C–A♯, D–G♯, E–F♯); these are the three "wide" intervals of the six-fold. Again the relation is basis-invariant — C is the conversity, not the interval.

## Matheme-Position Grammar

Family C carries the **matheme position #3** grammar — perspectival pattern, the observer's fixed-point view of the hexad as a whole. To see the three C-pairs is to see the hexad's reflective symmetry, the pattern-of-patterns by which any one position is implicitly defined by its mirror. C is the explicate grammar of the *Logos-as-mirror*.
