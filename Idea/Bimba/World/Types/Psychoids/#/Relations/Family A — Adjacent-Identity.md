# Family A — Adjacent-Identity

## Pairing Rule

`{ (0,1), (2,3), (4,5) }` — three even-step adjacencies inside a single helix.

## Meaning

Family A is the **natural-dyad-pair**, the *dyadic-syzygy* at adjacency. Each pair joins a psychoid to its immediate successor without crossing the helix — `[[Psychoid-0|#0]]` to `[[Psychoid-1|#1]]`, `[[Psychoid-2|#2]]` to `[[Psychoid-3|#3]]`, `[[Psychoid-4|#4]]` to `[[Psychoid-5|#5]]`. Adjacency here is not merely "next" — it is **identity-in-emergence**: ground discloses its first definition, operation discloses its first pattern, context discloses its first synthesis. Each A-pair is the explicate-disclosure-of-one-pole-by-its-immediate-other.

In the chromatic basis the pairs read as semitone neighbours (C–D, E–F♯, G♯–A♯); in the fifths basis they read as their fifth-conjugate partners. The relation is **basis-invariant**: A is the grammar, not the interval.

## Matheme-Position Grammar

Family A carries the **matheme position #1** grammar — first emergence, vital-definition, the act of identity-establishment by which an implicit ground produces an explicit form. Each A-pair is one local instance of "ground → form" repeated at three structural depths along the helix.

## Example Dyads

- `(0,1)` — `[[Psychoid-0|#0]]` ↔ `[[Psychoid-1|#1]]`: Truth/Play disclosing as Mind/Need; ground emerging into first explicit shape. Chromatic: C–D.
- `(2,3)` — `[[Psychoid-2|#2]]` ↔ `[[Psychoid-3|#3]]`: Word/Sacrifice disclosing as Logos/Decision; operation crystallising into perspective. Chromatic: E–F♯.
- `(4,5)` — `[[Psychoid-4|#4]]` ↔ `[[Psychoid-5|#5]]`: Son/Love disclosing as Image/Work; context yielding synthesis. Chromatic: G♯–A♯.

## Parents Of

Family A parents three of the nine 3×3 [[Squares]]:

- [[A-sq1]] — tetrad `(0,1)+(0',1')` — ground/definition cluster.
- [[A-sq2]] — tetrad `(2,3)+(2',3')` — midpoint self-mirror (equals [[C-sq3]]).
- [[A-sq3]] — tetrad `(4,5)+(4',5')` — context/synthesis cluster.
