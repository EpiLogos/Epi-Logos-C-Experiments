# Squares — 3×3 Grid Of Tetrads

This MOC indexes the **3×3 grid of harmonic tetrads** generated by doubling the within-pass [[Family A — Adjacent-Identity]], [[Family B — Offset-Transition]], and [[Family C — Converse-Mirror]] families into their bimba+pratibimba reflections. Each tetrad pairs a within-helix dyad with its conjugate on the primed helix — `(x,y)+(x',y')`.

## 9 Entries, 8 Unique Tetrads

The grid has **nine entries** but **eight unique tetrads**. The double-count sits at the centre: [[A-sq2]] and [[C-sq3]] both name the tetrad `(2,3)+(2',3')`. This is not a bookkeeping mistake — it is the structural **midpoint self-mirror invariant**: the position-pair `(2,3)` is simultaneously an A-family adjacent-pair *and* a C-family mirror-pair (`2+3=5`). The hexad's midpoint reflects onto itself. Preserve the nine-entry structure so that the invariant remains visible; the 8-vs-9 count is the proof.

## Basis-Invariance

Each cell of the 3×3 carries a chromatic-basis tetrad (e.g. `{C, D, C♯, D♯}`) and an equivalent fifths-basis tetrad. **The basis-interval is incidental.** The structural relation — A, B, or C grammar doubled across the helix-pair — is what the cell names. The midpoint invariant `A-sq2 == C-sq3` is itself basis-invariant: it holds at both Lens 0 (chromatic) and Lens 1 (fifths).

## C-Family → L-Lens Square Correspondence

The C-family row of the grid corresponds to the three Klein V₄ L-lens [[Square A]] / [[Square B]] / [[Square C]] of the lens system:
