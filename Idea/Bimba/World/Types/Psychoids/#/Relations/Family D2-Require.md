# Family D2-Require

## Pairing Rule

`{ (n, (n-1)') }` — six regressive cross-helix pairs: `(1,0'), (2,1'), (3,2'), (4,3'), (5,4'), (0,5')`.

## Meaning

Family D2-Require is **B-grammar applied cross-helix backward**: the offset-transition of [[Family B — Offset-Transition]] taken in regression across the bimba/pratibimba pair. Each pair carries the *regressive-attraction* charge — a position reaching back into the previous position on the conjugate face. Where [[Family D2-Transform]] leans forward, D2-Require leans backward. It is the grammar by which a position is *pulled by* what precedes it on the other helix — what it draws on, what its presence implicitly requires.

In the chromatic basis the rule generates a uniform minor-2nd across the helix-pair (the same interval class as the within-helix B but mapped onto the conjugate side). The basis-interval is incidental; the grammar is "regressive attraction across the conjugate face".

## Matheme-Position Grammar

D2-Require is the second sub-type of **matheme position #5** — synthesis-as-process — in its *backward* / *attractive* mode. Synthesis arrives partly by being drawn back into the conditions that made it possible. D2-Require names this attractor-grammar: completion holds the hand of its own precondition on the other helix. It is the implicit owe-relation woven into every step forward.
