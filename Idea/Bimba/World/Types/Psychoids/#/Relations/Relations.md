# Relations — Psychoid Harmonic Grammar

This MOC roots the **archetypal grammar of harmonic relations** between psychoid wells. The relation families are lifted from [[ql-musical-derivation-v3]] and named so they can be re-used wherever psychoid pairings recur — symbolic readings, Thought artifacts, MEF moves, oracle complementary-pairs, and beyond. The families are the *grammar*; the music derivation is only one expressive substrate over them.

## The Eight Families

Eight families partition the dyadic space of `{#0…#5} ∪ {#0'…#5'}`:

- [[Family A — Adjacent-Identity]] — `{(0,1),(2,3),(4,5)}` — within-pass natural dyad.
- [[Family B — Offset-Transition]] — `{(1,2),(3,4),(5,0)}` — within-pass transition; closes the cycle.
- [[Family C — Converse-Mirror]] — `{(0,5),(1,4),(2,3)}` — within-pass mirror; each pair sums to 5; parents the three L-lens [[Square A]], [[Square B]], [[Square C]].
- [[Family D1 — Same-Position Cross]] — `{(n,n')}` — bimba ↔ pratibimba spanda flip.
- [[Family D2-Transform]] — `{(n,(n+1)')}` — progressive cross-helix translation.
- [[Family D2-Require]] — `{(n,(n-1)')}` — regressive cross-helix attraction.
- [[Family D2-Complete]] — `{(n,(5-n)')}` — mirror-completion cross-helix.
- [[Family D3 — Helix-Invariance]] — A/B/C applied within the pratibimba helix; same matheme-substrate from the conjugate face.

## The 3:3 Partition

The eight families are *structurally* partitioned 3:3 + 2:

- **A / B / C** — within-pass, **physical-pole-trinity**, carry matheme positions **1, 2, 3** (the explicate three). They constitute the bimba helix's internal grammar.
- **D1 / D2 / D3** — cross-pass and inverse-pass, **mental-pole-trinity**, carry matheme positions **4, 5, 0** (the implicate three). They constitute the helix-pair's invariance grammar. D2 has three sub-types (transform / require / complete) which collectively occupy matheme position #5.

This is not a stylistic grouping — it is the matheme-position-grammar each family carries when invoked. A within-helix pair (A/B/C) speaks the language of explicate articulation; a cross-helix pair (D1/D2/D3) speaks the language of implicate self-recognition.

## The 3×3 Squares

When the within-pass pairs (A, B, C) are doubled into their primed reflections, they generate a 3×3 grid of tetrads — the [[Squares]] MOC. Nine entries; eight unique tetrads. The double-count at the centre (`A-sq2 == C-sq3`) is the basis-invariant **midpoint self-mirror** where adjacency and conversity coincide on positions (2,3).
