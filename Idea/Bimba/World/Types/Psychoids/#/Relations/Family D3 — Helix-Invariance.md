# Family D3 — Helix-Invariance

## Pairing Rule

A, B, and C applied **entirely within the pratibimba helix**:

- D3-A: `{(0',1'),(2',3'),(4',5')}`
- D3-B: `{(1',2'),(3',4'),(5',0')}`
- D3-C: `{(0',5'),(1',4'),(2',3')}`

## Meaning

Family D3 is the **helix-invariance grammar**. It is not a new pairing rule but the statement that everything [[Family A — Adjacent-Identity]], [[Family B — Offset-Transition]], and [[Family C — Converse-Mirror]] do on the bimba helix can be read off the pratibimba helix unchanged. D3 articulates the deepest canonical claim: **bimba and pratibimba are not two distinct substrates — they are one matheme-substrate read from two conjugate faces**. The within-pass grammar A/B/C is invariant under the slash-flip `q → q*`.

In the chromatic basis the pratibimba intervals reproduce the bimba intervals; in the fifths basis the same. The basis is incidental; the invariance is the grammar.

## Matheme-Position Grammar

Family D3 carries the **matheme position #0** grammar — return / renewed-ground / the implicate-face of explicate articulation. Where A/B/C carry matheme positions 1, 2, 3 (explicate physical-pole-trinity), their pratibimba-internal reading carries position #0 — the *hidden* ground beneath each explicate move, the recognition that articulation on one face was always already happening on the other. D3 completes the mental-pole-trinity (matheme positions 4, 5, 0) when read alongside [[Family D1 — Same-Position Cross]] (position #4) and the three D2 sub-types (position #5).

## Relation To Squares And Cross-Families

D3 does not parent fresh 3×3 [[Squares]] — the squares are already doubled into primed reflections by the A/B/C parents. Instead D3 **certifies** that the primed-pair half of every square is governed by the same grammar as the unprimed half. This is the basis-invariance proof at the level of relation families. D3 also reads as the implicate companion of [[Family D1 — Same-Position Cross]]: D1 ties bimba to pratibimba point-by-point, while D3 ties their *grammars* face-to-face.
