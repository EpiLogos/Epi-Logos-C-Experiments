# [[S5]]: Integral World Boundary

> The outermost fold of the stack: where the system meets the world, manifests into it, and receives the world's return.

## #5 Integration — What This Layer IS

[[S5]] is the integral layer of the technology stack. It is the place where the stack stops being only internally coordinated and begins to participate in a wider field: external services, publication surfaces, notifications, webhook exchanges, public outputs, and the return currents that bring new material back into the system.

The older identification of [[S5]] with [[Notion]] captured one possible manifestation but is too narrow for the current system. The more recent reduction of [[S5]] to "raw sync" is too thin in the other direction. The canon that best fits the present repo is:

- [[S5]] is the **world-boundary itself**
- external connectors are one part of it
- integrative memory-return surfaces are another part of it
- the layer is defined by **world exchange and return**, not by any single vendor or protocol

This is why [[S5]] now has to be wide enough to encompass both:

- eventual outward integrations such as [[Notion]], Telegram, n8n, email, and generic webhook delivery
- the freshly implemented knowledge-return systems that already perform integral-world work in practice: [[Gnostic]] via [[RAG-Anything]] and [[Graphiti]] as episodic memory substrate

These latter systems do not erase the boundary rule. They clarify it. [[Gnostic]] and [[Graphiti]] are not "just S2 again." They are part of the world's return to the system: ingested corpus, lived history, and temporally qualified memory becoming available as integral material for renewed action.

## The Internal 0-5 of [[S5]]

| Sub | Name | Function |
|-----|------|----------|
| [[S5.0]] | Boundary Ground | The fact of world contact: endpoints, ingress points, publication surfaces, addressability |
| [[S5.1]] | Connector Form | Service contracts and destination definitions: what external form is being addressed |
| [[S5.2]] | Exchange Operation | Publish, notify, ingest, trigger, receive, promote |
| [[S5.3]] | Integration Pattern | Workflow composition, protocol translation, ingress-egress loops, relay patterns |

This gives a clear internal 0-5 without collapsing the layer into a single tool. [[S5]] is not merely transport, and it is not merely pedagogy. It is the full integral arc of outward manifestation and inward return.

## Boundary Clarifications

Several historical identifications need to be kept but corrected:

- [[Gateway]] belongs primarily to [[S3]], not [[S5]]
- [[SpacetimeDB]] and live shared presence belong primarily to [[S3']], not [[S5]]
- [[Chronos]] determines when world-facing acts occur; it is not itself the world-boundary
- [[S5']] determines which integration is fitting and authorized

So [[Gateway]] and [[SpacetimeDB]] are not rewritten here as [[S5]]. They remain the lower transport and shared-state strata that feed the integral boundary. [[S5]] begins where those substrates become world-directed manifestation or world-return.

## Cross-Layer Actualisation

[[Aletheia]] currently actualises the richest living part of [[S5]] by making world-return concrete: [[Gnostic]] ingestion and retrieval, [[Graphiti]] episodic memory, thought routing, crystallisation, and seed refresh. [[Chronos]] actualises the temporal face of [[S5]] by scheduling when integral deliveries and returns matter. [[S3]] and [[S3']] actualise the transport and presence substrate that make those acts reachable. Future external connectors such as [[Notion]] and n8n will actualise the outward publication and automation face of the same layer.

## #0 Ground — What Returns to Source

The present ambiguity of [[S5]] is real and must remain visible:

- the old repo treated [[S5]] as [[Notion]] or dashboard
- the newer canonical plan treats [[S5]] as generic external API access
- the newest live implementation shows the layer already being actualised through [[Aletheia]], [[Gnostic]], and [[Graphiti]]

The synthesis taken here is therefore deliberate: [[S5]] names the integral world-boundary, while specific implementation ownership remains distributed across adjacent modules. That keeps the layer wide enough for what now exists, without falsely relocating [[Gateway]] or [[SpacetimeDB]] out of [[S3]]/[[S3']].
