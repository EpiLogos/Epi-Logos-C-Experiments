# [[S2]]: GraphDB Substrate

> The raw shared relational substrate: [[Neo4j]] for graph persistence, [[Redis]] for volatile and fast-path state.

## #5 Integration — What This Layer IS

[[S2]] is the explicate [[GraphDB]] layer: the shared infrastructure surface where graph persistence and cache transport are made available as primitives. In the eventual shape, [[S2]] does not decide what a coordinate means, what a cache tier signifies, or how a session lineage should be modeled. It gives the system one common relational arena and one common volatile arena, nothing less and nothing more.

The old [[S2]] world text rightly preserves the larger telos: the graph as universal topology, the place where repository knowledge becomes relationally present. The later canonical system plan resolves that vision by separating substrate from law. The universal graph remains the destiny of the layer, but [[S2]] itself is the raw exposure of [[Neo4j]] and [[Redis]] as shared infrastructure: connection/session management, raw [[Cypher]], raw key-value and pub-sub operations, and the health or retry mechanics required to keep the substrate usable.

In the target architecture, this substrate is shared across lawful namespaces: canonical [[Bimba]] ontology, corpus-bearing [[Gnostic]] content, and episodic [[Graphiti]] memory may all inhabit the same physical [[Neo4j]] database, while [[Redis]] carries fast-path state and cache transport. [[S2]] is therefore the common floor beneath graph meaning, not graph meaning itself. Every higher graph-facing concern must stand on this floor, but none may collapse back into it.

## Cross-Layer Actualisation

[[S2']] actualises this substrate into lawful graph meaning and is the required contract surface above it. [[Hen]] consumes that lawful surface for topology-aware retrieval and vault-to-graph coordination. [[Khora]] consumes the same substrate for cache residency and session-state transport. [[Moirai]] within [[Aletheia]] consumes it for assertion, query, and reflective validation. [[Pleroma]] may expose bounded primitives that touch the substrate, but never as an alternate source of graph semantics.

## #0 Ground — What Returns to Source

[[S2]] returns to source through a few non-negotiable laws:

- [[S2]] must remain raw infrastructure: no coordinate semantics, no template law, no artifact-residency doctrine inside the substrate itself
- [[GraphRAG]] is combined [[Neo4j]] plus [[Redis]], not [[Neo4j]] alone
- no Layer-3 module should treat [[S2]] as its public semantic interface; graph meaning routes through [[S2']]
- the substrate must be namespace-capable and schema-agnostic enough to host canonical ontology, corpus ingestion, and episodic memory without conflating their laws

The return of [[S2]] is therefore purity of role: one shared relational floor, one shared volatile floor, and no semantic inflation inside the substrate.
