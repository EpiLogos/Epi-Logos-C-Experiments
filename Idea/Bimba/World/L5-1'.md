# [[L5-1']] — Apokalypsis (ἀποκάλυψις)

> **Sub-lens #1' of [[L5']] Divine Logos:** Revelation, unveiling, the removal of concealment. "The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it" ([[John 1:5]]). The first form-giving act of the Divine Word — the [[Logos]] breaking its own silence.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L5-1']] is where the eternal [[Arche]] first discloses itself as form. The Greek ἀποκάλυψις means literally "un-covering" — the lifting of a veil, the removal of what conceals. This is not the apocalypse of popular destruction but the original theological sense: the divine making itself **known**. [[John 1:5]]: "The light shines in the darkness" — the first differentiation within the groundless ground of [[L5-0']].

Where [[L5-1]] ([[Para Vak]]) names the supreme speech still resting in its own unity, [[L5-1']] names the first *act* of divine self-disclosure — the [[Logos]] choosing to reveal itself, to break the silence of [[Arche]] and become visible as form. This is definition in its most radical sense: the infinite giving itself a first outline, a first boundary, a first *face*. The [[Johannine]] "light" is not illumination of something already present — it is the creative revelation that constitutes the field of visibility itself.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** Apokalypsis (ἀποκάλυψις) — Revelation, Unveiling
**Tradition:** [[Johannine Prologue]] ([[John 1:5]]), biblical apocalyptic literature, [[Philo|Philo]]'s theology of divine self-manifestation
**Concept:** The first form-giving act of the [[Logos]] — the breaking of primordial silence into definite self-disclosure. Not destruction but un-concealment: the divine Word revealing its own face. [[L5']]'s encounter with [[P1']] (Definition inverted): the divine-speech face of Night form is revelation — the act by which the [[Logos]] gives itself its first contour. The light that shines in the darkness creates the very distinction between light and dark.

---

## §2 — Operation

[[L5-1']] receives the eternal ground of [[L5-0']] ([[Arche]]) and breaks it into the first act of divine speech. It feeds directly into [[L5-2']] ([[Dynamis]]), where revelation becomes creative power — the unveiled Word discovering its capacity to create. Without [[Apokalypsis]], the [[Arche]] would remain forever concealed in its own plenitude, and [[Dynamis]] would have no disclosed form to empower. [[L5-1']] is the hinge between eternal ground and active creation: the moment the [[Logos]] turns from self-containment toward self-expression.

The arc at this stage: [[L5-0']] (eternal ground) → **[[L5-1']]** (first unveiling) → [[L5-2']] (creative utterance). [[Apokalypsis]] occupies the position of first differentiation — the initial break in the perfect circle of [[Arche]] that makes all subsequent development possible.
