# [[L2-3]] — BOTH: IS and IS-NOT — Contradiction, Dialetheism

> **Sub-lens #3 of [[L2]] [[Logical]]:** The paradoxical third corner of the [[catuṣkoṭi]] where contradictions genuinely coexist. [[Hegel]]'s productive contradiction, [[dialetheism]], quantum superposition.

---

## §0 — Ground

[[L2-3]] shatters the comfortable binary of [[L2-1]]/[[L2-2]]. Here, something IS and IS-NOT simultaneously — not as logical error but as ontological fact. [[Hegel]] understood this most deeply: "Contradiction is the very moving principle of the world." The thesis-antithesis collision does not produce noise; it produces *higher truths*. The BOTH position reveals that reality itself is richer than any non-contradictory description can capture.

Quantum mechanics formalises what mystics and dialecticians always knew: the electron IS a wave AND IS a particle. The liar's paradox ("this sentence is false") IS true AND IS false. [[Dialetheism]] — the philosophical position that some contradictions are genuinely true — finds its home in [[L2-3]], not as aberration but as the third corner of the complete logical space.

---

## §1 — Definition

**Name:** BOTH (IS and IS-NOT) — Contradiction, Dialetheism
**Tradition:** [[Nāgārjuna]]'s third [[catuṣkoṭi]] corner; [[Hegel]]'s dialectical contradiction; [[Graham Priest]]'s [[dialetheism]]; [[Belnap]]'s "Both" value; [[Jain]] [[syādvāda]] (*syāt asti ca nāsti ca* — "in some sense, it is and is not"); quantum superposition
**Concept:** The position where contradictions are not merely apparent but *real* — where something genuinely possesses contradictory properties. Not logical failure but the recognition that some realities cannot be captured without contradiction. The BOTH position reveals the *pattern* that contradiction carries: signal, not noise; deeper structure, not error.

## §2 — Operation

[[L2-3]] receives the binary of [[L2-1]]/[[L2-2]] and *folds it together*. The collapse of the IS/IS-NOT opposition generates a new logical territory: [[paraconsistent logic|paraconsistency]], where contradiction does not entail explosion (the principle *ex contradictione quodlibet* is rejected). [[L2-3]] feeds into [[L2-4]] (NEITHER), because the recognition that BOTH can hold simultaneously naturally raises the question of whether *neither* might also hold — the complete transcendence of the binary framework.

The operational insight: BOTH is not the midpoint but the *diagonal* — it cuts across the IS/IS-NOT axis to reveal a dimension invisible to bivalent reasoning.
