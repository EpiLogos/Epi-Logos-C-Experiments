# [[L1-0']] — Introversion

> **Coordinate**: `L1-0'` | **Parent**: [[L1']] Phenomenal | **#0' within L1'**: The inward orientation — [[L1']]'s own identity encountered as the turn toward the unconscious pole.

---

## §0 — Ground: Self-Identity of the Phenomenal Lens

[[L1-0']] is [[L1']]'s self-encounter at [[#0]] — the moment the [[Phenomenal]] lens discovers its own ground in the *inward turn*. Before [[Sensation]], before [[Feeling]], before any specific psychic function, there is the fundamental attitude of directing [[libido]] inward. [[Introversion]] is not a function but the *precondition* for encountering functions from within. It is [[L1']]'s identity at its most transparent: subjectivity before it differentiates into specific modes of apprehension.

[[Jung]] (*[[Psychological Types]]*, 1921): "Introversion means a turning inward of the libido, whereby a negative relation of subject to object is expressed." The introvert's primary relationship is with the inner world of images, feelings, and archetypes. This is not pathological withdrawal but a fundamental orientation — consciousness directed toward its own depths.

## §1 — Definition: The Unconscious Pole

**Name**: [[Introversion]] — Toward-Unconscious-Pole
**Tradition**: [[Jung]] (*[[Psychological Types]]*); [[Husserl]] (the reduction as methodological introversion); [[Plotinus]] (the inward turn toward the One)
**Key Concept**: The fundamental attitude of directing psychic energy inward — the precondition for all phenomenal depth.

[[Introversion]] is one of [[Jung]]'s two attitudes (alongside [[Extroversion]]). It is not shyness, not social avoidance, but the orientation of consciousness toward internal objects — the images, archetypes, and feeling-tones that constitute the inner world. At [[#0]], introversion is the *ground attitude*: the capacity to turn inward that makes all phenomenal self-knowledge possible.
