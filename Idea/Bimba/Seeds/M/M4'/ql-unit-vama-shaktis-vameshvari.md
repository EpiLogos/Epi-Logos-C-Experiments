# QL Unit — The Vāma Śaktis and Vāmeśvarī

## How Consciousness Knows Itself, Unawake and Awake

**Parent register:** Self-Identity as Śakti — the power by which awareness knows, acts, turns outward into world, and returns through recognition.

**QL parent 0/1:** **Jñāna Śakti / Kriyā Śakti** — knowing-power and action-power, not as two separated substances, but as the twin polarity of one living consciousness.

**Sixfold unit:**

```text
#0  Vāma-śakti      — the seed-current, the left/reverse/emissive power-field
#1  Khecarī         — the subject-power, moving in the sky of consciousness
#2  Gocarī          — the mind/speech-power, inner articulation
#3  Dikcarī         — the sense/direction-power, perceptual and active orientation
#4  Bhūcarī         — the earth/object-power, the field of things
#5  Vāmeśvarī       — the Mistress of the Vāma powers, the recognized sovereign wheel
```

**Core formula:**

```text
Vāma-śakti
→ Khecarī
→ Gocarī
→ Dikcarī
→ Bhūcarī
→ Vāmeśvarī
```

Or, in ordinary language:

```text
The current of knowing-action
→ becomes a subject
→ speaks inwardly as mind
→ opens as senses
→ appears as world
→ is recognized as the Goddess who was governing the whole circulation.
```

---

## 1. Orientation: What This Unit Is Doing

This unit is a QL rendering of the **Wheel of Vāmeśvarī** from the Kashmir Śaiva/Spanda-Krama context. The scholarly source-material usually presents a **fourfold** or **fivefold** structure:

- the four operative powers: **Khecarī, Gocarī, Dikcarī, Bhūcarī**;
- and, in the pentadic presentation, **Vāmeśvarī** as their governing source, centre, or mistress.

Kṣemarāja’s account, as summarized by Mark Dyczkowski, says that the pure universal pulsation of consciousness manifests as five cycles or pulses of power, arranged from the subtlest and most subjective to the grossest and most objective. The four linking circles are Khecarī as individual subject, Gocarī as the inner organ of mentation, Dikcarī as the senses, and Bhūcarī as the objects of the senses; in their centre is the fifth circle, pure consciousness.[^1]

QL does not replace that source structure. QL reads it as a **six-position contemplative circuit** by distinguishing two faces that are often compressed together in the textual material:

```text
#0 = Vāma-śakti
     the seed-current, the undivided power-field, the raw turn of knowing-action

#5 = Vāmeśvarī
     the sovereign of the powers, the whole wheel recognized as governed by Śakti
```

The tradition gives us the strong clue for this distinction. In *The Stanzas on Vibration*, Dyczkowski’s translation states that the Wheel of Vāmeśvarī is also known as the Wheel of Vāma, consisting of the Vāma energies. These energies have a double function: for the awakened yogi they run counter to saṃsāra, while they also emit the universe of unity, diversity, and duality; they proclaim diversity for the ignorant and bestow unity amid multiplicity for the wise.[^2]

That is exactly the QL 0/5 polarity:

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti  = the power-current before its sovereignty is recognized
#5 Vāmeśvarī   = the current recognized as Mistress, source, ruler, and whole
```

So the unit is not claiming that Kashmir Śaivism literally teaches six separate goddesses here. It is doing QL anatomy: drawing out the sixfold circulation implicit in the way the powers bind and liberate.

## 2. A Minimal Glossary for the Reader

### Śakti

**Śakti** means power, capacity, energy, potency. In Kashmir Śaivism, Śakti is not a secondary force outside Śiva. Śiva and Śakti are the luminous and dynamic faces of one reality: consciousness as light and consciousness as self-knowing power.

### Jñāna Śakti

**Jñāna Śakti** is the power of knowing. This is not merely discursive knowledge or information. It is the power by which consciousness manifests reality as knowable: the capacity for awareness, disclosure, intelligibility, recognition.

### Kriyā Śakti

**Kriyā Śakti** is the power of action. This is not merely physical movement. It is the power by which consciousness performs, emits, differentiates, forms, and enacts what it knows.

### Icchā Śakti and Spanda

Between knowing and action lies **Icchā Śakti**, the power of will, urge, or desire. In this unit, Icchā is read as the **slash** between Jñāna and Kriyā: the pulse by which knowing wants to act and action wants to be known.

**Spanda** means vibration, throb, pulsation — not mechanical vibration, but the living pulse of consciousness. The Vāma current is a specific way of reading that pulse: consciousness turns, emits, reverses, and recognizes.

### Vāma

**Vāma** can carry the sense of left, reverse, contrary, or counter-current. In the Vāmeśvarī context, Dyczkowski emphasizes a double reversal: for the unawakened, Vāma is the source of diversity and potential bondage, the reverse of Śiva’s unity and freedom; for the awakened, Vāma is the current that runs counter to the ordinary course of saṃsāra and bestows unity in multiplicity.[^3]

### Vāmeśvarī

**Vāmeśvarī** means, in the QL reading, the Mistress of the Vāma powers. The texts may identify her with the central pure consciousness, source, or foundation from which the four powers emerge. QL places her at **#5** in order to mark the moment when the whole circulation is no longer merely operating but is recognized as sovereignly governed by Śakti.

### The four *carī* powers

The suffix **-carī** means “moving,” “wandering,” or “operating in.” Each power operates in a specific field:

| Term | Field | Basic meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Khecarī** | *kha*, sky/space | moving in the sky of consciousness; subject-power |
| **Gocarī** | *go*, read by Kṣemarāja as speech | moving as inner speech, mind, thought |
| **Dikcarī** | *dik*, direction | moving through directions; senses and action-faculties |
| **Bhūcarī** | *bhū*, earth | moving in earth/objectivity; sense-objects and world |

A Trika glossary likewise identifies Khecarī, Gocarī, Dikcarī, and Bhūcarī as four śaktis or powers emerging from Vāmeśvarī.[^4]

## 3. The Parent 0/1: Jñāna Śakti / Kriyā Śakti

Before the six positions unfold, the parent relation must be named.

```text
0/1 = Jñāna Śakti / Kriyā Śakti
```

In QL terms, a parent node is the non-dual root from which the positions emerge. It is not a seventh thing beside the six. It is the generative relation that the six positions unfold, differentiate, and return to.

Here the parent is:

```text
Jñāna Śakti / Kriyā Śakti
Knowing-power / Action-power
Disclosure / Enactment
Awareness / Manifestation
```

But the slash matters. The slash is not an empty separator. The slash is the living **going** by which knowing becomes action and action becomes knowable. This going is the Vāma turn itself.

So the parent can be written more fully:

```text
0 = Jñāna Śakti
    consciousness as the power to know, illuminate, disclose, and recognize

/ = Icchā-Spanda-Vāma
    the urge, pulse, and contrary turn by which knowing moves into display

1 = Kriyā Śakti
    consciousness as the power to act, form, emit, objectify, and embody
```

This parent relation gives rise to the 4+2 structure.

The **+2** are the two implicate poles of the current:

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti  — the current in seed, the power-field before recognition
#5 Vāmeśvarī   — the current recognized as sovereign, mistress of the wheel
```

The **4** are the four operative powers by which consciousness becomes a knowable world:

```text
#1 Khecarī  — subject
#2 Gocarī  — mind/speech
#3 Dikcarī — senses/directions
#4 Bhūcarī — objects/world
```

The sixfold is therefore:

```text
2 implicate poles + 4 operative powers = QL 4+2
```

Or:

```text
Vāma current + subject + mind + senses + world + Vāmeśvarī recognition
```

## 5. The Two Sides of the Wheel

The Vāma powers circulate in two opposed but inseparable ways.

### The descending side

The descending side is the side of **emission, objectification, manifestation, and possible bondage**.

```text
#0 → #1 → #2 → #3 → #4 → #5
```

In ordinary language:

```text
current
→ subject
→ thought
→ senses
→ objects
→ world-system
```

This is the Kriyā side: consciousness acts, emits, performs, differentiates. There is nothing wrong with this descent. Without it, there would be no experience, no world, no body, no speech, no relational life. The problem begins when the descent is read without recognition. Then action seems separated from knowing; the world seems separated from awareness; the subject seems trapped inside what it perceives.

### The ascending side

The ascending side is the side of **recognition, return, purification, and awakened knowing**.

```text
#5 → #4 → #3 → #2 → #1 → #0
```

In ordinary language:

```text
recognized whole
→ luminous objects
→ awakened senses
→ mantra-mind
→ sky-subject
→ original current
```

This is the Jñāna side: consciousness knows itself through what it has emitted. The return does not abolish the descent. It does not reject subject, mind, senses, or world. It re-reads them.

Dyczkowski states this principle directly in relation to the Wheel of Energies: the same forces can either bind the deluded or empower the enlightened; “that which binds the ignorant sets free the man of wisdom.”[^5]

So this unit must always be read in two ways:

```text
unawake: the powers are taken as proof of separation
awake:   the powers are recognized as modes of consciousness
```

## 6. #0 — Vāma-śakti

### Ground / seed-current / the first turning of knowing-action

At #0 we place **Vāma-śakti** rather than Vāmeśvarī proper.

This is the refinement that makes the unit cleaner. Vāmeśvarī is the sovereign mistress of the wheel. Vāma-śakti is the current itself: the leftward, contrary, emissive, reversing power-field before the whole circulation has become explicit.

At #0, consciousness has not yet stabilized as a subject facing an object. There is not yet “me over here” and “world over there.” There is the pulse of awareness-power: the capacity to know and act before knower, knowing, and known have separated.

The Vāma current is “contrary” because manifestation is already a kind of reversal. Pure unity turns into the possibility of difference. The seamless becomes articulated. The unbounded becomes a field of positions. Consciousness does not cease to be itself; it becomes capable of appearing as other than itself.

This is why Vāma is ambiguous. It can be the power of bondage or the power of liberation.

On the descending side, Vāma is the current by which unity emits diversity. If this emission is unrecognized, the current becomes the beginning of sleep.

On the ascending side, Vāma is the current that runs counter to saṃsāra. It reverses the reversal. It does not destroy diversity; it reveals unity in the midst of diversity.

### Actual concept

Dyczkowski’s *Stanzas on Vibration* translation gives the key: the Vāma energies both emit the universe of unity and diversity and, for the awakened yogi, run counter to saṃsāra.[^2] The Wheel of Vāmeśvarī is therefore not merely a cosmological diagram. It is a map of the two-way power of consciousness: outward into manifestation, inward into recognition.

### Descending / unawake view

At #0 unawake, the current is present but not known. The power begins to move, but awareness does not recognize itself as the mover.

The unawake seed-thought is:

```text
Something is happening.
```

But the deeper truth is missed:

```text
Awareness is happening as this.
```

When Vāma-śakti descends unrecognized, the world will soon appear as external, thought as private, senses as outward-running, and the subject as trapped inside its limits.

### Ascending / awake view

At #0 awake, the current is felt as pulse, freedom, and potential. One does not need to suppress the world to feel the source. The source is felt as the very power by which experience is arising.

The awakened seed-recognition is:

```text
The current by which this appears is not other than awareness.
```

### Contemplative practice

Before asking what anything is, sense the fact that experience is moving.

Do not yet name the object. Do not yet name the subject. Feel the pulse by which a moment becomes available at all.

Ask:

```text
What is the living current before I divide it into me, thought, sense, and world?
```

Rest there, not as blankness, but as potency.

## 7. #1 — Khecarī

### Definition / subject / the sky-mover

**Khecarī** is the power operating in *kha*, the sky or space of consciousness. In the Wheel, this is the subject-plane.

Dyczkowski’s summary of Kṣemarāja says that the Khecarī energies constitute the individual subject.[^1] In *The Stanzas on Vibration*, the Khecarīs are said to operate in the sky of consciousness and thus reside on the plane of the subject.[^6]

This is QL **#1 Definition** because the first determinate articulation of experience is:

```text
There is an experiencer.
```

The open current becomes a point of view. The field folds into an “I.”

### Actual concept

Khecarī occupies the difficult threshold where the universal subject appears as individual subject. In the expanded condition, the powers associated with Khecarī are qualities of consciousness itself: unity, omnipotence, omniscience, fullness, pervasion. But when consciousness limits itself, those same powers appear as the limiting coverings or **kañcukas**: time, limited agency, limited knowledge, attachment, and natural law.[^7]

This is crucial. The limited subject is not made of a different substance than consciousness. It is consciousness under constriction.

The same power that, awake, is fullness can, unawake, be felt as lack.

The same power that, awake, is freedom can, unawake, be felt as limited agency.

The same power that, awake, is pervasive knowing can, unawake, be felt as “I only know this little fragment.”

### Descending / unawake view

In the descending view, Khecarī becomes the contracted subject:

```text
I am this one.
I am here and not there.
I know only what I know.
I can do only what I can do.
I am bound by time.
I am driven by lack and attachment.
```

This is not simply false. Empirically, the person is located, finite, historical, embodied. Bondage begins when this empirical subject is taken to be the whole truth of the Self.

The subject forgets that it moves in the sky of consciousness. It mistakes itself for a sealed point.

### Ascending / awake view

In the ascending view, Khecarī is not destroyed. The subject remains, but becomes transparent.

The awakened subject says:

```text
This “I” is a local movement in the sky of awareness.
```

The subject is no longer a prison. It is an aperture.

The “I” is still useful. It can speak, choose, remember, love, act, and take responsibility. But it no longer claims to be self-grounding. It knows itself as a finite expression of a larger field.

### Contemplative practice

Notice the felt sense of “I.”

Do not analyze it yet. Do not condemn it as ego. Do not inflate it as divine identity. Simply feel the centre from which experience seems to be happening.

Then ask:

```text
Is this “I” also appearing?
In what sky does the sense of “I” arise?
```

If the “I” can be known, then awareness is more spacious than the “I.” That spaciousness is the sky in which Khecarī moves.

## 8. #2 — Gocarī

### Dynamis / mind / inner speech

**Gocarī** is the power of inner articulation. Kṣemarāja reads **go** here as speech. Dyczkowski explains that the Gocarī energies are the primal energies of the inner organ, **antahkaraṇa**, constituting the subtle inner discourse of thought.[^8]

This is QL **#2 Dynamis** because mind is movement. It is not a static container. It names, compares, interprets, remembers, imagines, projects, narrates, and evaluates.

Once there is a subject, there is a story.

The subject says:

```text
This is happening.
This means something.
This is mine.
This is not mine.
I want this.
I fear that.
```

### Actual concept

In the unawake condition, the Gocarī powers operate through:

- **buddhi**, the intellect, as distinction-determination;
- **ahaṃkāra**, egoity, as identification of the Self with diversified objectivity;
- **manas**, mind, as thought-construction centred on diversity.

Dyczkowski’s summary says that, for the unawakened, these energies determine distinction, identify the Self with objectivity, and form diversity-centred thought-constructs. For those in grace, the same energies give rise to pure determinate awareness, the reflective recognition “I am Śiva,” and the synthesis of diversity into a unified whole.[^8]

Thus Gocarī is one of the most important positions for contemplation, because most bondage is not the raw fact of perception; it is the inner speech that claims perception, narrates it, and builds identity from it.

### Descending / unawake view

In the descending view, Gocarī becomes compulsive inner speech:

```text
This proves I am lacking.
This proves I am right.
This is mine.
That is against me.
This object will complete me.
This thought is reality.
```

Mind divides the field and then forgets that it has divided. It converts useful distinction into ontological separation.

At #2 unawake, thought does not merely describe bondage. Thought manufactures bondage by repeating it as obvious.

### Ascending / awake view

In the ascending view, thought becomes transparent. It becomes closer to mantra than chatter.

A thought can still arise. A concept can still function. Speech can still discriminate. But the mind no longer mistakes its articulation for the whole of reality.

The awakened Gocarī says:

```text
This thought is a movement of awareness.
This word points; it does not imprison.
This distinction serves recognition.
```

The mind becomes a reflective instrument. It gathers diversity into intelligible unity rather than scattering unity into defensive fragments.

### Contemplative practice

Listen to the next thought as if it were sound.

Do not ask first whether it is true. Ask first:

```text
What is this thought made of?
Where does it appear?
What happens if I hear it as movement rather than obey it as command?
```

A thought is not outside awareness. It is awareness taking the form of inner speech.

## 9. #3 — Dikcarī

### Pattern / senses / directions

**Dikcarī** is the power moving through **dik**, direction. It is the directional opening of perception and action.

Dyczkowski places Dikcarī in the sphere of the ten senses: the five powers of knowledge and the five powers of action, symbolized by the ten directions. Through these powers, the unawakened perceive only multiplicity; when purified, the awakened yogi perceives Śiva’s unity through the diversity of sensations.[^9]

This is QL **#3 Pattern** because the senses pattern the field. They give experience its orientation: here/there, near/far, visible/audible/tangible, reachable/unreachable, spoken/unspoken, grasped/released.

The senses are the directional grammar of embodiment.

### Actual concept

In the Indian philosophical vocabulary, one can distinguish:

- **jñānendriyas**: faculties of knowledge, such as seeing, hearing, touching, tasting, smelling;
- **karmendriyas**: faculties of action, such as speaking, grasping, moving, excreting, generating.

Dikcarī gathers these as directions of the power of consciousness.

Dyczkowski later emphasizes that the physical organs are merely doors or channels through which the power of consciousness flows; the organs alone do not account for perception.[^10]

This is important pedagogically. The senses are not merely mechanical openings in a body-machine. They are Śakti moving outward as knowledge and action.

### Descending / unawake view

In the descending view, the senses scatter awareness:

```text
Sight runs outward.
Sound distracts.
Touch grasps.
Taste seeks.
Smell recoils or desires.
Speech reacts.
The hand seizes.
The feet pursue.
```

The senses appear to drag the subject away from itself. They seem to prove that reality is outside awareness.

At #3 unawake, multiplicity becomes compelling. The senses deliver difference, and the mind rushes to narrate it.

### Ascending / awake view

In the ascending view, the senses are not enemies. They are rays.

Seeing is awareness seeing. Hearing is awareness hearing. Touch is awareness touching itself in the form of contact. Speech is awareness sounding. Action is awareness extending without leaving itself.

The awakened Dikcarī says:

```text
The senses do not exile me from awareness.
They are awareness moving directionally.
```

The senses become precise, vivid, and sacramental. Not because the world is denied, but because it is felt as a living disclosure.

### Contemplative practice

Choose one sense.

For sight, look at one form without immediately naming it.

For hearing, receive one sound without pursuing its source.

For touch, feel one contact without turning it into preference.

Ask:

```text
Is this sense moving away from awareness, or is awareness moving as this sense?
```

Let the sense reveal the direction of Śakti.

## 10. #4 — Bhūcarī

### Context / earth / objects

**Bhūcarī** is the outermost operative power: the power moving in **bhū**, earth, objectivity, concrete existence.

Dyczkowski identifies Bhūcarī as the sphere of objectivity, where the energies manifest as the five objects of sense: form, taste, sound, smell, and touch. For the awakened, these are experienced in harmony with the senses and in their ultimate identity with consciousness; for others, they display limitation everywhere.[^11]

This is QL **#4 Context** because objects form the world-horizon in which subject, thought, and sense now seem situated.

At #4, the subject appears to live in a world.

### Actual concept

Bhūcarī does not simply mean “matter” in the reductive modern sense. It means the realm of objectivity: the things, textures, forms, appearances, and sense-fields through which experience becomes world-like.

The five sense-objects may be read as:

```text
form / sound / touch / taste / smell
```

or, depending on the order used in the source, the tangible qualities by which the world appears as something given.

At Bhūcarī, Śakti has become maximally explicit. The original Vāma current has become the visible and tangible world.

### Descending / unawake view

In the descending view, Bhūcarī is the hard external world:

```text
Objects are outside me.
Things are inert.
The world threatens or tempts me.
I must acquire, avoid, defend, consume, possess.
```

The object appears as proof that consciousness is secondary. The object seems to stand there by itself, independent of the light by which it is known.

At #4 unawake, the world becomes opaque. Śakti is hidden by her own form.

### Ascending / awake view

In the ascending view, objects are not abolished. A cup remains a cup. A tree remains a tree. A face remains a face. But each object is recognized as appearing in awareness, made knowable by awareness, and not separate from the light in which it shines.

The awakened Bhūcarī says:

```text
The object is not outside the field of knowing.
The world is Śakti visible.
```

This is why the object is so important in the return. The object that seemed to prove separation becomes the doorway through which non-separation is recognized.

### Contemplative practice

Take one ordinary object: a cup, a hand, a wall, a stone, a phone.

Let it be fully itself. Do not dissolve it into an abstraction.

Then ask:

```text
Where is this object known?
What of it is outside the field in which it appears?
What is its colour, weight, meaning, use, and presence made of in experience?
```

Do not conclude too quickly that “it is just mind.” That is too thin. Instead, feel the richer recognition:

```text
Object and knowing arise together in one living field.
```

## 11. #5 — Vāmeśvarī

### Realisation / sovereignty / the Mistress of the wheel

At #5 we place **Vāmeśvarī**.

This is the completed and recognized form of the unit. The powers are no longer merely operating. Their sovereignty is known.

In the source-material, Vāmeśvarī may be named as the central pure consciousness, root-consciousness, or source from which the four powers emerge. Dyczkowski describes her as the Supreme Goddess who acts as root-consciousness and ground of sensory perception, personifying the inner and outer senses and their objects.[^12] He also says that the four circles evolve out of her as aspects of her nature when the split between perceiver and perceived occurs.[^13]

QL accepts that textual source-role. But structurally it places **Vāmeśvarī at #5** to mark the recognitive completion:

```text
At #0, the powers stir as Vāma-śakti.
At #5, the powers are known as Vāmeśvarī’s wheel.
```

This is the movement from power-field to sovereign recognition.

### Actual concept

Vāmeśvarī is not merely an additional item after the world. She is the whole wheel known as governed. She is the recognition that subject, mind, senses, and objects are not accidental fragments but the ordered circulation of Śakti.

Dyczkowski’s *Stanzas* translation states that Khecarī, Gocarī, Dikcarī, and Bhūcarī emanate from the power Vāmeśvarī.[^14] Another passage notes the alternation between fourfold and fivefold schemes: Kṣemarāja uses the five esoteric Krama cycles of Vāmeśvarī, Khecarī, Gocarī, Dikcarī, and Bhūcarī, while Bhagavadutpala supports a fourfold scheme omitting Vāmeśvarī.[^15]

This alternation is exactly why QL can read the system as 4+2:

```text
four operative powers
+ seed-current at #0
+ sovereign recognition at #5
```

### Descending / unawake view

At #5 unawake, the wheel completes as a closed world. Vāmeśvarī is present, but veiled.

The unawake completion says:

```text
I am a subject inside a world.
My thoughts describe reality.
My senses report external objects.
Objects are simply outside me.
This is just how things are.
```

This is saṃsāra as completed reading. The whole circulation is functioning, but its source and sovereignty are not recognized.

### Ascending / awake view

At #5 awake, the whole wheel is recognized as Vāmeśvarī.

The awakened completion says:

```text
Subject is Śakti localized.
Mind is Śakti articulating.
Senses are Śakti directing.
Objects are Śakti appearing.
The world is Śakti’s wheel.
```

This is not a retreat from manifestation. It is manifestation known as the play of consciousness.

The key is sovereignty. At #5 the practitioner does not merely have an insight about awareness. One sees that the entire circulation has been governed by the Goddess from the beginning.

### Contemplative practice

Let the whole present moment appear at once:

```text
body
breath
room
sound
thought
mood
object
space
attention
```

Do not select one part. Let the whole field be present.

Ask:

```text
Is anything here outside awareness?
Is anything here outside Śakti?
Who is mistress of this wheel?
```

Let the answer be the field itself.

## 12. The Descending Pass in Detail

The descending pass is the pass of manifestation and potential forgetting.

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti
→ #1 Khecarī
→ #2 Gocarī
→ #3 Dikcarī
→ #4 Bhūcarī
→ #5 Vāmeśvarī veiled as world-system
```

### #0 → #1: Current becomes subject

The Vāma current stirs. Knowing-action turns toward manifestation. The first determinate formation is the subject: the one to whom experience appears.

Unawake, this is contraction:

```text
I am this finite experiencer.
```

Awake, this is the first aperture:

```text
Awareness has localized as a point of experience.
```

### #1 → #2: Subject becomes mind

The subject does not remain mute. It speaks inwardly. It interprets, names, remembers, anticipates.

Unawake:

```text
Thought defines reality and builds identity around difference.
```

Awake:

```text
Thought is recognized as inner articulation of awareness.
```

### #2 → #3: Mind becomes sense-orientation

Inner articulation opens into directional contact. The body-world relation becomes vivid through the senses and actions.

Unawake:

```text
The senses scatter the subject outward.
```

Awake:

```text
The senses are directions of Śakti.
```

### #3 → #4: Senses become objects

Directional experience stabilizes as a world of things. Forms, sounds, textures, tastes, smells, uses, meanings, and distances become the context of life.

Unawake:

```text
Objects appear external and binding.
```

Awake:

```text
Objects appear as the visible body of consciousness.
```

### #4 → #5: Objects become world-system

The world coheres. A whole situation is now present: person, mind, body, senses, objects, space, time, relation.

Unawake:

```text
The wheel closes as ordinary realism: “I am simply inside the world.”
```

Awake:

```text
The wheel is available for recognition as Vāmeśvarī.
```

### The descending view summarized

```text
Vāma current unrecognized
→ limited subject
→ divisive thought
→ scattered senses
→ external objects
→ closed saṃsāric world
```

The error is not manifestation. The error is misreading manifestation as separation.

---

## 13. The Ascending Pass in Detail

The ascending pass is the pass of recognition.

```text
#5 Vāmeśvarī
→ #4 Bhūcarī
→ #3 Dikcarī
→ #2 Gocarī
→ #1 Khecarī
→ #0 Vāma-śakti
```

This pass begins not by escaping the world, but by recognizing the whole field.

### #5 → #4: Whole field illuminates objects

The practitioner begins with the whole situation and turns toward the object-field.

Awake:

```text
Objects are appearances of consciousness, not alien things outside knowing.
```

This does not deny empirical objectivity. It simply refuses to let empirical objectivity become metaphysical exile.

### #4 → #3: Objects return through senses

The object is known through seeing, hearing, touching, tasting, smelling, and acting. The object cannot be contemplated apart from the directional powers by which it appears.

Awake:

```text
The senses are the living paths by which the object shines.
```

### #3 → #2: Senses return into mind

Sensation is interpreted, named, remembered, and understood. The practitioner notices the mind forming the world-image.

Awake:

```text
Mind is not the enemy; it is the articulating power of awareness.
```

### #2 → #1: Mind returns into subject

Thoughts are known. Inner speech appears to someone. That someone — the subject — is now itself noticed.

Awake:

```text
The “I” is known; therefore the field of knowing is deeper than the “I.”
```

### #1 → #0: Subject returns into current

The subject is felt as a movement in the sky of consciousness. Beneath the subject is the Vāma current: the living pulse by which knowing becomes experience and experience returns to knowing.

Awake:

```text
The original current was never absent.
```

### The ascending view summarized

```text
Vāmeśvarī recognized
→ objects as luminous
→ senses as Śakti-rays
→ mind as mantra
→ subject as sky-mover
→ Vāma current as original pulse
```

The return does not cancel the descent. It releases the descent into its truth.

## 14. The Inverse Positional Sixfold Mapping

The QL inverse geometry is:

```text
0 ↔ 5
1 ↔ 4
2 ↔ 3
```

These are the three essential contemplative inversions.

---

### 14.1 — 0 ↔ 5: Vāma-śakti and Vāmeśvarī

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti ↔ #5 Vāmeśvarī
```

At #0, the current is in seed. At #5, the current is recognized as sovereign.

This is the relation of potency and mastery, emission and recognition, power-field and Mistress.

Unawake:

```text
The current emits difference, but sovereignty is hidden.
```

Awake:

```text
The current is known as Vāmeśvarī’s own wheel.
```

Formula:

```text
Vāma is the current.
Vāmeśvarī is the current recognized as sovereign.
```

---

### 14.2 — 1 ↔ 4: Khecarī and Bhūcarī

```text
#1 Khecarī ↔ #4 Bhūcarī
```

Khecarī is the subject-pole. Bhūcarī is the object-pole.

Unawake:

```text
I am here.
The world is there.
I must manage, fear, acquire, resist, or possess it.
```

Awake:

```text
The object appears to the subject.
The subject is disclosed through the object.
Both arise in awareness.
```

Formula:

```text
The object is the subject’s hidden light appearing outwardly.
The subject is the object’s inward point of recognition.
```

This does not flatten subject and object into sameness. It recognizes them as inverse poles in one field.

---

### 14.3 — 2 ↔ 3: Gocarī and Dikcarī

```text
#2 Gocarī ↔ #3 Dikcarī
```

Gocarī is inner articulation. Dikcarī is directional sensing and acting.

Unawake:

```text
The senses deliver multiplicity.
Mind divides and narrates it.
The senses chase what mind desires.
Mind justifies what the senses pursue.
```

Awake:

```text
The senses open the field.
Mind articulates the field.
The senses reveal diversity.
Mind gathers diversity into intelligible unity.
```

Formula:

```text
Sense without awakened mind scatters.
Mind without awakened sense abstracts.
Together, purified, they reveal the pattern of Śakti.
```

## 15. The Muṇḍa / Daṇḍa Cross-Reading

The *Cidgaganacandrikā* commentary preserves a useful Jñāna/Kriyā cross-reading. It says Vāmeśvarī’s fivefold current takes two forms: **Muṇḍakrama**, related to Jñānaśakti, and **Daṇḍakrama**, related to Kriyā Śakti. In that source, Bhūcarī with Gocarī, Dikcarī, and Khecarī establishes Daṇḍakrama, while Bhūcarī with Gocarī, Dikcarī, Khecarī, and Vāmeśvarī forms Muṇḍakrama.[^16]

QL reads this as a major confirmation of the unit’s structure.

### Daṇḍakrama / Kriyā side

```text
Bhūcarī + Gocarī + Dikcarī + Khecarī
```

This is the action-current: the powers operate as the machinery of manifestation, cognition, embodiment, and object-engagement.

In QL terms, this corresponds to the descending/activity side, where the powers function as operative differentiations.

### Muṇḍakrama / Jñāna side

```text
Bhūcarī + Gocarī + Dikcarī + Khecarī + Vāmeśvarī
```

This is the knowledge-current: the same powers are gathered into recognition by the inclusion of Vāmeśvarī.

In QL terms, the crucial difference is #5. Without Vāmeśvarī, the powers operate. With Vāmeśvarī, the powers are known as a sovereign wheel.

### QL conclusion

```text
Kriyā = the powers in operation
Jñāna = the powers recognized in their mistress
```

Or:

```text
Daṇḍa = the staff/current of action
Muṇḍa = the head/current of knowing
```

The two are not enemies. They are two readings of the one circulation.

## 16. The Four Powers and Speech / Bliss / Will / Knowledge / Action

Another useful cross-mapping appears in Dyczkowski’s discussion of the fourfold scheme. The four spheres correspond to the four levels of speech — Supreme, Intuitive, Middling, and Corporeal — as well as to the powers of bliss, will, knowledge, and action.[^15]

This gives a secondary interpretive table:

| Power | Plane | Speech level | Śakti resonance | QL reading |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Khecarī** | subject / sky | Supreme speech | bliss | subject as the first expansion of conscious bliss |
| **Gocarī** | inner speech / mind | intuitive speech | will | thought as intent, desire, inner formative speech |
| **Dikcarī** | senses / directions | middling speech | knowledge | senses as directional knowing |
| **Bhūcarī** | objects / earth | corporeal speech | action | world as embodied utterance and completed act |

This table should not replace the main QL mapping. It enriches it. It shows that the four powers are not merely psychological compartments; they are also levels of expression, speech, and manifestation.

The world is speech become object.

The object is action become visible.

The senses are knowledge moving directionally.

Mind is will taking inner form.

The subject is bliss contracted or expanded, depending on whether it knows its source.

## 17. One Ordinary Moment: Seeing a Cup

To make the unit practical, take a simple perception: seeing a cup.

### Descending / unawake reading

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti
```

The current of experience is already moving, but unnoticed.

```text
#1 Khecarī
```

“I see the cup.” A subject has formed.

```text
#2 Gocarī
```

“That is my cup. I need coffee. I am tired. I should answer that message.” Inner speech proliferates.

```text
#3 Dikcarī
```

Sight fixes the form. Hand may reach. Body orients.

```text
#4 Bhūcarī
```

The cup appears as an external object in a world of objects.

```text
#5 Vāmeśvarī veiled
```

The whole circuit closes as ordinary unconscious realism: “I am just a person looking at a cup.”

Nothing is wrong. But nothing is recognized.

### Ascending / awake reading

```text
#5 Vāmeśvarī
```

Let the whole scene appear: body, cup, room, sight, thought, breath. The field is one display.

```text
#4 Bhūcarī
```

The cup is known as appearing in awareness. Its colour, shape, use, and meaning are not outside the knowing field.

```text
#3 Dikcarī
```

Seeing is felt as a directional ray of consciousness, not merely a mechanical report.

```text
#2 Gocarī
```

The word “cup” is heard as inner speech: useful, but not imprisoning.

```text
#1 Khecarī
```

The “I who sees” is noticed. It too appears in the sky of awareness.

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti
```

The whole event rests in the original pulse of knowing-action.

Then action can continue. Pick up the cup. Drink. Speak. Work. Kriyā Śakti continues, but no longer as exile from Jñāna Śakti.

## 18. How One Knows Oneself Unawake

The unawake self-knowing is still a kind of knowing. It is just inverted, narrowed, and misread.

The unawake one knows itself as:

```text
I am a limited subject.
My thoughts are my reality.
My senses show me an external world.
Objects stand apart from me.
The world is the final frame.
```

Mapped to the positions:

| Position | Unawake self-knowledge |
|---:|---|
| **#0 Vāma-śakti** | the current is unfelt; manifestation begins as unconscious impulse |
| **#1 Khecarī** | “I am this finite experiencer” |
| **#2 Gocarī** | “My thought-story is reality” |
| **#3 Dikcarī** | “The senses carry me outward into multiplicity” |
| **#4 Bhūcarī** | “Objects are outside, inert, desirable, threatening, binding” |
| **#5 Vāmeśvarī veiled** | “The world-system is simply given; no sovereign awareness is recognized” |

This is not absence of consciousness. It is consciousness knowing itself through limitation while forgetting that limitation is its own power.

Unawake self-knowledge is therefore:

```text
self-recognition under the sign of separation
```

The Self is present in every stage, but it knows itself as less than itself.

---

## 19. How One Knows Oneself Awake

The awake one does not abandon the structures of experience. Rather, each structure becomes transparent to its source.

The awake one knows itself as:

```text
I am the field in which subject, thought, sense, and world arise.
The powers that seemed to bind me are my own modes of manifestation.
The world is not outside Śakti.
The wheel is Vāmeśvarī.
```

Mapped to the positions:

| Position | Awake self-knowledge |
|---:|---|
| **#5 Vāmeśvarī** | the whole field is recognized as Śakti’s wheel |
| **#4 Bhūcarī** | objects are luminous appearances in consciousness |
| **#3 Dikcarī** | senses are rays/directions of awareness |
| **#2 Gocarī** | mind is mantra-like articulation, not prison |
| **#1 Khecarī** | the subject is a local sky-movement of awareness |
| **#0 Vāma-śakti** | the original current is recovered as living pulse |

Awake self-knowledge is therefore:

```text
self-recognition through the structures that once concealed the Self
```

The Self is not known by escaping experience, but by recognizing the powers of experience as its own.

## 20. The Full Circulation

The complete unit is not just descent or ascent. It is circulation.

```text
        #0 Vāma-śakti
              ↓
        #1 Khecarī
              ↓
        #2 Gocarī
              ↓
        #3 Dikcarī
              ↓
        #4 Bhūcarī
              ↓
        #5 Vāmeśvarī
              ↑
        recognized return through
        objects → senses → mind → subject → current
```

More precisely:

```text
Descending pass:
#0 → #1 → #2 → #3 → #4 → #5

Ascending pass:
#5 → #4 → #3 → #2 → #1 → #0

Inverse pairings:
#0 ↔ #5
#1 ↔ #4
#2 ↔ #3
```

The full contemplative circulation is:

```text
Vāma-śakti emits
Khecarī centers
Gocarī articulates
Dikcarī directs
Bhūcarī objectifies
Vāmeśvarī gathers
Bhūcarī illumines
Dikcarī purifies
Gocarī mantricizes
Khecarī opens
Vāma-śakti pulses again
```

## 21. Practice Sequence: A Sixfold Contemplation

This practice can be done with any object or moment.

### Step 0 — Vāma-śakti

Feel the moment before naming.

```text
There is a current of appearing.
```

### Step 1 — Khecarī

Notice the subject.

```text
There is an “I” to whom this appears.
```

Then soften:

```text
This “I” moves in the sky of awareness.
```

### Step 2 — Gocarī

Listen to the mind.

```text
Thought is naming, judging, remembering, projecting.
```

Then soften:

```text
Thought is inner speech moving in awareness.
```

### Step 3 — Dikcarī

Open to the senses.

```text
Seeing, hearing, touching, acting: these are directions.
```

Then soften:

```text
The senses are rays of Śakti.
```

### Step 4 — Bhūcarī

Let the object be fully present.

```text
The world appears as form, sound, touch, taste, smell.
```

Then soften:

```text
Objects are not outside the light by which they are known.
```

### Step 5 — Vāmeśvarī

Let the whole field gather.

```text
Subject, mind, senses, and objects are one wheel.
```

Then recognize:

```text
The wheel is Vāmeśvarī.
```

### Return

Move back inward:

```text
Vāmeśvarī
→ world as luminous
→ senses as rays
→ mind as mantra
→ subject as sky
→ Vāma as pulse
```

Then act.

The aim is not withdrawal from life. The aim is action that remembers its source.

## 22. Final QL Unit Form

### Parent 0/1

```text
Jñāna Śakti / Kriyā Śakti
```

Expanded:

```text
Jñāna Śakti = the power of knowing, disclosure, recognition
Icchā-Spanda-Vāma = the pulse/turn by which knowing moves
Kriyā Śakti = the power of action, emission, formation
```

### 4+2 structure

```text
+2 implicate poles:
#0 Vāma-śakti
#5 Vāmeśvarī

4 operative powers:
#1 Khecarī
#2 Gocarī
#3 Dikcarī
#4 Bhūcarī
```

### Descending / objectifying

```text
#0 Vāma-śakti
→ #1 Khecarī: subject appears
→ #2 Gocarī: mind speaks and distinguishes
→ #3 Dikcarī: senses open directions
→ #4 Bhūcarī: objects appear as world
→ #5 Vāmeśvarī veiled as the closed order of saṃsāra
```

### Ascending / recognitive

```text
#5 Vāmeśvarī recognized
→ #4 Bhūcarī: objects shine as consciousness
→ #3 Dikcarī: senses become Śakti-rays
→ #2 Gocarī: thought becomes mantra/vimarśa
→ #1 Khecarī: subject opens into the sky of awareness
→ #0 Vāma-śakti: the original pulse is recovered
```

### Inversions

```text
0 ↔ 5  Vāma-śakti ↔ Vāmeśvarī
1 ↔ 4  Khecarī ↔ Bhūcarī
2 ↔ 3  Gocarī ↔ Dikcarī
```

### Core mantra

```text
Knowing acts.
Action becomes world.
World is recognized as knowing.
```

Or:

```text
Vāma is the current.
Khecarī is the subject.
Gocarī is the mind.
Dikcarī is the sense-direction.
Bhūcarī is the world.
Vāmeśvarī is the wheel recognized as sovereign Śakti.
```

### Closing contemplation

```text
I am not merely the subject facing the world.
I am the field in which subject, mind, senses, and world arise.
The powers that scattered me are the powers that return me.
The wheel that bound me is the wheel by which I know myself.
Vāma turns.
Vāmeśvarī gathers.
The current and the Mistress are one circulation.
```
